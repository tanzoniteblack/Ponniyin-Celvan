naalaam attiyaayam Chapter 4
kaʈampuur maaɭikai Kadamboor Fort
ittaṉai neeram iɭaippaaṟiyirunta vallavaraiyaṉuʈaiya kutirai ippootu nalla cuṟucuṟuppaip peṟṟiruntatu; oru naaɻikai neerattil kaʈampuurc campuvaraiyar maaɭikai vaacalai aʈaintuviʈʈatu. antak kaalattuc cooɻa naaʈʈup peruŋkuʈit talaivarkaɭil ceŋkaɳɳar campuvaraiyar oruvar. avaruʈaiya maaɭikaiyiṉ vaacal oru periya nakarattiṉ kooʈʈai vaacalaip pool iruntatu. vaacalukku irupuṟattilum eɻunta neʈuɲcuvarkaɭ kooʈʈaic cuvarkaɭaip poolavee vaɭaintu ceṉṟaṉa. The horse had by now rested well and become quite energetic. Within a few hours it reached the gates of Kadamboor. Lord Sengannan Sambuvaraya was an important Chozla nobleman in those days. His castle gates resembled the entrance of a large city-stronghold. The towering walls on both sides of the gate curved around like a fortress.
kooʈʈai vaacalil yaaṉaikaɭum, kutiraikaɭum, riʂapaŋkaɭum, anta mirukaŋkaɭaiyellaam piʈittuk kaʈʈuvoorum, tiiṉi vaippoorum, taɳɳiir kaaʈʈuvoorum, aaŋkaaŋku tiivartti tuukkip piʈittu vauiccam pooʈuvoorum, tiivarttikaɭukku eɳɳey viʈuvoorumaaka, oree koolaakalamaayiruntatu. itaiyellaam paartta vallavaraiyaṉiṉ uɭɭattil ciṟitu tayakkamum tuɳukkamum eeṟpaʈʈaṉa. 'eetoo iŋkee periya viceeʂam oṉṟu naʈaipeṟukiṟatu. intac camayattil naam vantu ceerntoomee' eṉṟu eɳɳiṉaaṉ. naʈakkum viceeʂam eṉṉaveṉpataip paarttut terintu koɭɭum aavalum orupakkam poŋkik koɳʈiruntatu. kooʈʈai vaacaṟ katavukaɭ tiṟantutaaṉiruntaṉa. aaṉaal tiṟantirunta vaacalil veel piʈitta viirarkaɭ cilar niṉṟu koɳʈiruntaarkaɭ. avarkaɭai paarttaal yamakiŋkararkaɭaip pooliruntatu. A number of elephants, horses, large bullocks as well as grooms to hold, feed and water these animals stood near the gates. Here and there men stood with flaming torches to provide light; oil pourers were ready to add oil to the lighted torches. Sighting all these bustling activities, Vandiya Devan felt a bit dismayed and hesitant. Something special seems to be taking place here; why have I come here now ... At the same time he felt an overpowering urge to find out what the special occasion was. The fortress gates were wide open although men holding long lances stood near the gates. They looked like the messengers of Yama, the Lord of Death.
tayaŋki niṉṟaal taṉṉai avarkaɭ niṟuttiviʈuvaarkaɭ eṉṟum tairiyamaakak kutiraiyai viʈ_ʈukoɳʈu uɭɭee poovatutaaṉ ucitam eṉṟum anta viira vaalipaṉ eɳɳiṉaaṉ. anta eɳɳattai uʈaṉee kaariyattil niṟaiveeṟṟiṉaaṉ. aaṉaal eṉṉa eemaaṟṟam? kutirai kooʈʈai vaacalai aɳukiyatum veel piʈitta viirarkaɭ iruvar taŋkaɭ veelkaɭaik kuṟukkee niṟutti vaɻimaṟittaarkaɭ. iṉṉum naalu peer vantu kutiraiyiṉ talaikkayiṟṟaip piʈittuk koɳʈaarkaɭ. avarkaɭil oruvaṉ vantiyatteevaṉai uṟṟup paarttaaṉ. iṉṉoruvaṉ tiivartti koɳʈuvantu uyarat tuukki mukattukku neeree piʈittaaṉ. The dauntless youth decided that the best thing to do would be to ride boldly and go in; any hesitation on his part would alert the guards to stop him. He translated his thoughts into action. But what a disappointment?! As soon as the horse neared the gates, lances crossed in front to obstruct the way and stop him. Four men came forward and held onto the horse's reins. One of them peered at Vandiya Devan. Another held up the flaming torch to light his face.
vallavaraiyaṉ mukattil koopam kotikka, "itutaaṉ uŋkaɭ uur vaɻakkamaa? vanta viruntaaɭikaɭai vaacalileeyee taʈuttu niṟuttuvatu....?" eṉṟaaṉ. With anger darkening his face, Vallavarayan Vandiya Devan asked "Is this customary in your town? Stopping guests at your gates ...?"
"nii yaar tampi ivvaɭavu tuʈukkaakap peecukiṟaay? enta uur? eṉṟaaṉ vaacaṟkaavalaṉ." "Who are you? Impudent fellow. Where do you come from?"
"eṉ uurum peerumaa keeʈkiṟaay? vaaɳakappaaʈi naaʈʈut tiruvallam eṉ uur. eṉṉuʈaiya kulattu muṉṉoorkaɭiṉ peyarkaɭai oru kaalattil uŋkaɭ naaʈʈu viirarkaɭ taŋkaɭ maarpil eɻutik koɳʈu perumaiyaʈaintaarkaɭ! eṉ peyar vallavaraiyaṉ vantiyatteevaṉ! terintataa?" eṉṟaaṉ. "Are you asking me my name and town? Thiruvallam in the Vanakapadi Kingdom is my town. Once upon a time, soldiers in your country were proud to tattoo the names of my ancestors on their breasts. My name is Vallavarayan Vandiya Devan! Do you know?"
"ivvaɭavaiyum colvataṟku oru kaʈʈiyakkaaraṉaiyum kuuʈa aɻaittu varuvatutaaṉee?" eṉṟaaṉ kaavalarkaɭil oruvaṉ. itaik keeʈʈa maṟṟavarkaɭ cirittaarkaɭ. "Why did you not bring a liveried herald to announce all this?" said the gate-keeper. Others around him laughed.
"nii yaaraayiruntaalum iṉi uɭɭee pooka muʈiyaatu! iṉṟaikku varaveeɳʈiya viruntaaɭikaɭ ellaam vantaakiviʈʈatu. iṉimeel yaaraiyum viʈaveeɳʈaam eṉṟu ejamaaṉiṉ kaʈʈaɭai!" eṉṟaaṉ kaavalar talaivaṉ. "Whoever you are, you cannot enter! All the expected guests have already arrived. The Master's orders are to not let in anyone after that," said their leader.
eetoo vaakkuvaatam naʈakkiṟataip paarttuk kooʈʈaikkuɭɭee caṟṟu tuurattil niṉṟa cila viirarkaɭ arukil vantaarkaɭ. avarkaɭil oruvaṉ, "aʈee! naam aŋkee tiruviɻaak kuuʈʈattil viraʈʈiyaʈittoomee, antak kurutai poola irukkiṟataʈaa!" eṉṟaaṉ. Hearing this altercation, some footmen who were standing nearby came closer. "Hey! Is this not the same mule that we chased into the festival crowd?" said one of them.
iṉṉoruvaṉ "kaɻutai eṉṟu collaʈaa" eṉṟaaṉ. "Donkey, not mule," corrected another.
"kaɻutai meel uʈkaarntirukkiṟavaṉ eṉṉa viṟaippaaka uʈkaarntirukkiṟaaṉ paaraʈaa!" eṉṟaaṉ maṟṟoruvaṉ. "Look at the starched up fellow sitting on his donkey," added another.
vallavaraiyaṉ kaatil intac coṟkaɭ viɻuntaṉa. Vallavarayan heard these words.
avaṉ maṉatiṟkuɭ, "eṉṉattiṟku viiɳ vampu? tirumpip pooy viʈalaamaa? allatu, iɭavaracar aatitta karikaalariṉ muttirai patitta ilacciṉaiyai ivarkaɭiʈam kaaʈʈiviʈʈu uɭɭee pookalaamaa?" eṉṟa yoocaṉai tooṉṟi iruntatu. vaʈaticaip paʈaiyiṉ maataɳʈa naayakaraakiya iɭavaracariṉ ilacciṉaiyaip paarttuviʈʈut taṉṉait taʈukkakkuuʈiyavarkaɭ vaʈapeɳɳaiyiliruntu kumarimuṉai varaiyil yaarum kiʈaiyaatu allavaa? ippaʈi avaṉ maṉattiṟkuɭ vivaatittuk koɳʈiruntapootutaaṉ paɻuveeʈʈaraiyar aaʈkaɭiṉ keelip peeccu avaṉ kaatil viɻuntatu. uʈaṉee eṉṉa ceyya veeɳʈum eṉpatai muʈivu ceytu koɳʈaaṉ. He had been thinking, why get involved? Perhaps I should go away quietly. Maybe I should show the token bearing the seal of Prince Aditya Karikala and then go in. When I have the signet of the Prince, the Commander-in Chief of the Northern Armies, none in the regions between River Pennar and Cape Comorin can impede my way. It was in the midst of these ponderings that he heard the mocking words of the Pazluvoor lackeys. Immediately deciding his course of action, he said
"kutiraiyai viʈuŋkaɭ; tirumpip pookiṟeeṉ!" eṉṟaaṉ. taʈutta viirarkaɭ kutiraiyiṉ mukakkayiṟṟai viʈʈaarkaɭ. "Let go of my horse. I am going back." The gate-keepers let go of the reins.
kutiraiyiṉ aʈivayiṟṟil vantiyatteevaṉ taṉ iru kaalkaɭiṉaalum oru aɻuttu aɻuttiṉaaṉ. atee neerattil uʈaivaaɭai uṟaiyiliruntu uruvi eʈuttaaṉ. miṉṉal oɭiyuʈaṉ kaɳɳaip paṟitta anta vaaɭ cuɻaṉṟa veekattiṉaal avaṉuʈaiya kaiyil tirumaaliṉ cakkaraayutattai vaittuk koɳʈu cuɻaṟṟuvatu pool tooṉṟiyatu. kutirai muṉṉookkik kooʈʈaikkuɭɭee paayntu ceṉṟatu. vaɻiyilirunta viirarkaɭ tiʈiir tiʈiireṉṟu kiiɻee viɻuntaarkaɭ. veelkaɭ caʈacaʈaveṉṟu aʈittuk koɳʈu viɻuntaṉa. vampu peeciya paɻuvuur viirarkaɭiṉ peeril kutirai paayntatu. inta miṉṉal taakkutalaic ciṟitum etirpaaraata viirarkaɭ naaṟpuṟamum citaṟic ceṉṟaarkaɭ. Vandiya Devan employed both his legs to apply a sudden pressure to the underbelly of his horse. At the same time he drew his sword from its scabbard at his waist. The swirling sword flashing like swift lightning in his hand appeared like God Vishnu's spinning Chakra (discus). The horse galloped forward into the fortress. The men in the way seemed to suddenly fall down. Lances and spears were thrown apart, clanging noisily. The horse flew amidst the gossiping men of Pazluvoor. The totally unexpected, lightning attack scattered the men in all four directions.
itaṟkuɭ veeṟu pala kaariyaŋkaɭ nikaɻntu viʈʈaṉa. kooʈʈaik katavukaɭ taʈaal, taʈaal eṉṟu caattappaʈʈaṉa. "piʈi! piʈi!" eṉṟa kuukkuralkaɭ eɻuntaṉa. veelkaɭum vaaɭkaɭum uraayntu 'kiɭaaŋ' 'kiɭaaŋ' eṉṟu olittaṉa. tiʈiireṉṟu apaayam aṟivikkum muracu 'ʈaʈam!' 'ʈaʈam!' eṉṟu muɻaŋkiṟṟu. Several other actions took place immediately: The fortress gates banged shut thunderingly. "Catch him! Hold!" shouts arose; spears rubbed against swords making a "clang, clang" noise. Suddenly the drums announcing danger, boomed "dadam, dadadam".
vantiyatteevaṉ kutiraiyaic cuṟṟilum viirarkaɭ vantu cuuɻntu koɳʈaarkaɭ. irupatu, muppatu, aimpatu peerukku meeleeyee irukkum. kutiraiyiṉ meelirunta vantiyatteevaṉ paayntu taraiyil kutittaaṉ. kaiyilirunta vaaɭaic cuɻaṟṟik koɳʈee, "kantamaaṟaa! kantamaaṟaa! uṉ aaʈkaɭ eṉṉaik kollukiṟaarkaɭ!" eṉṟu kattiṉaaṉ. Several men -- maybe twenty, thirty, fifty or even more -- surrounded Vandiya Devan and his horse. He jumped down to the ground; swirling his sword in all directions he shouted "Kandamara! Kandamara! Your men are killing me!"
itaik keeʈʈatum avaṉaic cuuɻntirunta viirarkaɭ tiʈukkiʈʈuc ciṟitu tayaŋki vilaki niṉṟaarkaɭ. Hearing his words, the men were stunned into hesitation and moved away a little.
accamayam maaɭikaiyiṉ meelmaaʈa mukappiliruntu, "aŋkee eṉṉa kuuccal? niṟuttuŋkaɭ!" eṉṟa oru iʈi muɻakkak kural keeʈʈatu. antak kural keeʈʈa iʈattil eeɻeʈʈup peer niṉṟu kiiɻee naʈappataip paarttuk koɳʈiruntaṉar. At the same time a thundering voice was heard from the upper balcony of the palace tower. "What is that noise out there? Stop it!" Several persons were standing there, looking down on the disturbance below.
"ejamaaṉ! yaaroo oru aaɭ kaavalai miiṟip pukuntu viʈʈaaṉ. ciṉṉa ejamaaṉ peyaraic collik kuuvukiṟaaṉ!" eṉṟu kiiɻeeyirunta oruvaṉ coṉṉaaṉ. "Master! Some fellow has broken our guard and entered here. He is shouting the young master's name," replied the gate-keeper.
"kantamaaṟaa! nii pooyk kalavaram eṉṉaveṉṟu paar!" - ivvitam meel maaʈattiliruntu atee iʈimuɻakkak kural colliṟṟu. antak kuralukku uʈaiyavartaaṉ ceŋkaɳɳar campuvaraiyar poolum eṉṟu vantiyatteevaṉ eɳɳiṉaaṉ. "Kandamara, go find out what the commotion is about," said the thundering voice from the tower. Vandiya Devan surmised the voice to be that of Lord Sambuvaraya.
avaṉum avaṉaic cuṟṟi niṉṟa viirarkaɭum ciṟitu neeram appaʈiyee niṉṟu koɳʈiruntaarkaɭ. He and the men surrounding him waited for a while.
"iŋkee eṉṉa aarppaaʈʈam?" eṉṟa oru iɭaŋkural keeʈʈatu. antak kural keeʈʈa iʈattil niṉṟavarkaɭ vilakik koɳʈu vaɻi eeṟpaʈuttiṉaarkaɭ. vaalipaṉ oruvaṉ anta vaɻiyaaka viraintu vantaaṉ. kaiyil piʈitta kattiyai ileecaakac cuɻaṟṟik koɳʈu cuuracamhaaram ceyta cuppiramaɳiyaraip poola niṉṟa vantiyatteevaṉai orukaɳam viyappuʈaṉ nookkiṉaaṉ. "What is all this about?" a young voice said. The men parted to make way for a youth coming hurriedly. He noticed Vandiya Devan who stood there twirling his sword, like God Muruga after killing the Demon Soora.
"vallavaa, eṉ arumai naɳpaa! uɳmaiyaakavee niitaaṉaa?" eṉṟu uɳarcci tatumpak kuuvik koɳʈu oʈic ceṉṟu vallavaraiyaṉai anta iɭaiɲaṉ kaʈʈit taɻuvik koɳʈaaṉ. "Vallava! Is it truly you?" he said in an emotional voice and ran forward to heartily embrace his friend.
"kantamaaṟaa! nii paʈittup paʈittup pala taʈavai coṉṉaayee eṉṟu uṉ viiʈʈukku vanteeṉ. vanta iʈattil eṉakku ittakaiya viira varaveeṟpuk kiʈaittatu" eṉṟu vantiyatteevaṉ taṉṉaic cuṟṟi niṉṟavarkaɭaic cuʈʈikkaaʈʈiṉaaṉ. "Kandamara because you repeatedly insisted, I came to your house. I received this warlike welcome here," Vallavarayan pointed to the men around him.
avarkaɭaip paarttu, "cii! muʈʈaaɭkaɭee! pooŋkaɭaʈaa! uŋkaɭ aṟivu ulakkaik koɻuntutaaṉ!" eṉṟaaṉ kantamaaṟaṉ. Kandamaran said to his men, "You idiots! Move aside. Your brains are like budding shoots on a pounding block."
kantamaaṟaṉ vantiyatteevaṉiṉ kaiyaip piʈittup paraparaveṉṟu iɻuttuk koɳʈu pooṉaaṉ. avaṉuʈaiya kaalkaɭ taraiyil nillaamal kutittuk koɳʈeeyiruntaṉa. avaṉuʈaiya uɭɭamum tuɭɭik kutittatu. yauvaṉap piraayattil uɳmaiyaaka uɭɭam oṉṟupaʈʈa oru naɳpaṉ kiʈaittaal ataikkaaʈʈilum oruvaṉaip paravacappaʈuttak kuuʈiyatu veeṟu eṉṉa uɳʈu? aam, kaatal eṉpatu oṉṟu irukkattaaṉ ceykiṟatu. aaṉaal kaatalil iṉpamum kutuukalamum ettaṉai uɳʈoo atai viʈa atikamaaṉa tuṉpamum veetaṉaiyum uɳʈu. yauvaṉattuc cineeka kutuukalattiloo tuṉpattiṉ niɻal kuuʈa viɻuvatillai. oree aaṉantamayamaaṉa itayap paravacantaaṉ. Kandamaran took hold of his friend's hand and led him hastily into the castle. His feet did not stay put on earth; and his heart danced with joy. What can captivate the heart of a youth more than a true friendship from his youthful days? Yes, of course there is a thing called kaadal (love). But being in love has as much sadness and pain as joy and pleasure. In the cheerful friendship of youth there is not even a shadow of sadness. All is heart pleasing happiness.
pookiṟa pookkil, vallavaraiyaṉ, "kantamaaṟaa! iṉṟaikku eṉṉa iŋkee eekataʈapuʈalaayirukkiṟatu? ivvaɭavu kaʈʈukkaaval ellaam etaṟkaaka?" eṉṟaaṉ. "Kandamara, what is all this hustle and bustle about? What is happening here today?" asked Vandiya Devan.
"iṉṟaikku iŋkee eṉṉa viceeʂam eṉpataip paṟṟi appuṟam vivaramaaka colkiṟeeṉ. niiyum naaṉum peɳɳaiyaaṟṟaŋkaraip paacaṟaiyil taŋkiyirunta pootu, 'paɻuveeʈʈaraiyaraip paarkka veeɳʈum; maɻavaraiyaraip paarkka veeɳʈum; avaraip paarkka veeɳʈum; ivaraip paarkka veeɳʈum' eṉṟu colvaayee? anta avar, ivar, cuvar - ellooraiyum iṉṟaikku iŋkeeyee nii paarttuviʈalaam!" eṉṟaaṉ kantamaaṟaṉ. "Oh! I'll explain what's happening here afterwards. Remember the days when you and I were at the army garrison near the Pennar river? Remember your wishes `We must meet Lord Pazluvoor, I must get the acquaintance of the great Lord of Mazluvoor, I must meet that one and this one'? That lord, this lord, every fellow - you can meet them all here tonight," said Kandamaran.
piṟaku, viruntaaɭikaɭ amarntirunta maaɭikai meel maaʈattukku vallavaraiyaṉaik kantamaaṟaṉ aɻaittuc ceṉṟaaṉ. mutalil taṉ tantaiyaakiya campuvaraiyariʈam koɳʈu pooy niṟutti, "appaa! eṉ tooɻaṉ vaaɳarkulattu vantiyatteevaṉaip paṟṟi aʈikkaʈi taŋkaɭiʈam collik koɳʈiruppeeṉee? avaṉ ivaṉtaaṉ!" eṉṟaaṉ. vantiyatteevaṉ periyavaraik kumpiʈʈu vaɳaŋkiṉaaṉ. varaiyar avvaɭavaaka makiɻcciyaʈaintataakat tooṉṟavillai. He then took his friend up to the upper chambers where the guests were seated. He presented him to his father Lord Sambuvaraya and said "Father! I have mentioned several times about my friend Vandiya Devan of the Vaanar Clan. This is he." Vandiya Devan bowed and greeted him with reverence. However, Lord Sambuvaraya did not seem too happy about it.
"appaʈiyaa? kiiɻee araɳmaṉai vaacalil avvaɭavu kalavaram ceytavaṉ ivaṉtaaṉaa?" eṉṟu keeʈʈaar. "Is that so? Is he the fellow who created all that disturbance at the palace gates below?" asked the father.
"kalavarattukkuk kaaraɳam eṉ tooɻaṉ alla; vaacal kaappataṟku naam amarttiyirukkum muuʈarkaɭ!" eṉṟaaṉ kantamaaṟaveeɭ. "He was not the cause for the disturbance. It's those idiots we have for gate-keepers" said the son.
"iruntaalum iṉṟaiya tiṉam paarttu, atuvum iruʈʈi arai jaamattiṟkup piṟaku, ivaṉ ivvaɭavu aarppaaʈʈattuʈaṉ vantirukka veeɳʈiyatillai!" eṉṟaar campuvaraiyar. "Still, he need not have arrived in this sensational manner; today of all days and that too hours after darkness," said lord Sambuvaraya.
kantamaaṟaveeɭiṉ mukam curuŋkiṟṟu; meelum tantaiyuʈaṉ vaatamiʈa avaṉ virumpavillai. vantiyatteevaṉai appaal aɻaittuc ceṉṟaaṉ. vantirunta viruntaaɭikaɭukku mattiyil naʈunaayakamaaka or uyarnta piiʈattil amarntirunta paɻuveeʈʈaraiyariʈam aɻaittup pooy, "maamaa! ivaṉ eṉ aaruyir naɳpaṉ vantiyatteevaṉ, vaaɳap peeraracar kulattavaṉ. ivaṉum naaṉum vaʈapeɳɳaikkaraip paacaṟaiyil ellaik kaaval purintu koɳʈiruntoom. appoɻutellaam 'viiraati viirar periya paɻuveeʈʈaraiyaraip paarkka veeɳʈum' eṉṟu oyaatu collik koɳʈiruppaaṉ. 'paɻuveeʈʈaraiyar tirumeeṉiyil aṟupattu naalu poork kaayaŋkaɭ iruppatu uɳmaitaaṉaa?' eṉṟu keeʈʈuk koɳʈiruppaaṉ. 'orunaaɭ niiyee eɳɳip paarttuk koɭ' eṉṟu naaṉ colluveeṉ" eṉṟaaṉ. Kandamaran frowned. He did not wish to continue the debate with his father. He led Vandiya Devan aside. He presented his friend to Lord Pazluvoor, seated on a lofty throne amidst the other guests, and said "Uncle! This is my dear friend Vandiya Devan. He is from the noble Vaanar clan. He and I were on guard duty at the army base north of the Pennar river. In those days he would often express a wish to `See and meet the bravest among warriors, the great Lord of Pazluvoor' and would often ask `Is it really true that he has sixty-four war-wounds on his body?' I would tell him `One day you can meet him and count them'."
paɻuveeʈʈaraiyar curuŋkiya mukattuʈaṉ, "appaʈiyaa, tampi! niiyee eɳɳip paarttaal oɻiya nampa maaʈʈaayoo? avvaɭavu avanampikkaiyaa uṉakku? 'vaaɳar kulattaik kaaʈʈilum veeṟu kulattil viiram irukka muʈiyumaa?" eṉṟa canteekamoo?" eṉṟaar. "Is that so Thambi? Will you not believe it unless you personally count them? Such distrust? You suspect that none but one of the Vaanar clan can be brave?" said Lord Pazluvoor.
tooɻarkaɭ iruvarumee tiʈukkiʈʈup pooṉaarkaɭ. toottiramaakac coṉṉatai ippaʈi ivar kutarkkamaaka eʈuttuk koɭvaar eṉṟu etirpaarkkavillai. Both friends were startled. They had not expected the nobleman to thus misconstrue their words of praise.
vantiyatteevaṉuʈaiya maṉattil ericcal kumuṟiyatu. aayiṉum vauiyil kaaʈʈik koɭɭaamal, "aiyaa! paɻuveeʈʈaraiyar kulattiṉ viirappukaɻ kumari muṉaiyiliruntu imayam varaiyil paraviyirukkiṟatu. ataip paṟṟic canteekippataṟku naaṉ yaar?" eṉṟu paɳivuʈaṉ coṉṉaaṉ. Vandiya Devan felt irritated. Without showing his feelings he said "Sir! The fame of the brave Pazluvoor clan has spread from Cape Comorin to the Himalayas. Who am I to doubt it?"
"nalla maṟumoɻi; keʈʈikkaarap piɭɭai!: eṉṟaar paɻuveeʈʈaraiyar. "Good reply. Intelligent fellow" muttered Lord Pazluvoor.
intamaʈʈil piɻaittoom eṉṟu vaaliparkaɭ iruvarum aŋkiruntu vauiyeeṟiṉaarkaɭ. appootu campuvaraiyar tamatu makaṉai aɻaittuk kaatooʈu, "uṉ tooɻaṉukkuc ciikkiram uɳavu aɭittu eŋkeeyaavatu oru taṉi iʈattil paʈukkac collu! niiɳʈa pirayaaɳam ceytu kaɭaittup pooyirukkiṟaaṉ" eṉṟaar. maaṟaveeɭ koopattuʈaṉ talaiyai acaittu viʈʈup pooṉaaṉ. With a feeling of having escaped with that, the friends went out. Lord Sambuvaraya called out to his son and whispered, "Serve your friend some food as early as possible and tell him to go and sleep in some secluded spot. He seems tired after a long journey!" Kandamaran shook his head angrily and walked on.
piṟaku maaṟaveeɭ vantiyatteevaṉai antappurattukku aɻaittu ceṉṟaaṉ. aŋkee peɳkaɭ palar iruntaarkaɭ. maaṟaveeɭiṉ aṉṉaikku vantiyatteevaṉ namaskaaram ceytaaṉ. avaɭukkup piṉṉaal kuuccattuʈaṉ maṟaintirukkum peɳtaaṉ kantamaaṟaṉiṉ cakootariyaayirukka veeɳʈum eṉṟu uukittuk koɳʈaaṉ. Later Kandamaran took his friend to the inner chambers of his mother. Several women were gathered there. Vandiya Devan bowed low and greeted Kandamaran's mother. He surmised that a girl hiding shyly behind her was Kandamaran's sister.
'taŋkacci'yaip paṟṟi maaṟaveeɭ pala taʈavai coṉṉatil eeteetoo kaṟpaṉai ceytu koɳʈiruntaaṉ vantiyatteevaṉ. ippootu oruvaaṟu eemaaṟṟamee aʈaintaaṉ. He had imagined all sorts of things based upon Kandamaran's descriptions of his younger sister. In a way, he was now disappointed.
antap peɳkaɭiṉ kuuʈʈattilee paɻuveeʈʈaraiyaruʈaṉ pallakkil vanta maatu yaaraaka irukkalaam eṉpatai aṟiya vantiyatteevaṉuʈaiya kaɳkaɭ teeʈi alaintaṉa. His eyes searched around among those women, with an eagerness to find the lady who had come in the palanquin with Lord Pazluvoor.
