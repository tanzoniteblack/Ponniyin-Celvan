35. mantiravaati Chapter 35 -- A Sorcerer
tuurattil peerikaikaɭiṉ peru muɻakkam keeʈʈatu. ekkaaɭaŋkaɭ captittaṉa. maṉitarkaɭiṉ kuralkaɭ jayakooʂam ceytaṉa. kooʈʈaik katavukaɭ tiṟantu muuʈikkoɭɭum cattamum yaaṉaikaɭ kutiraikaɭiṉ kaalaʈic cattamum eɻuntaṉa. In the distance the booming of large drums could be heard. Cymbals sounded and men raised cheers. They heard the fortress-gates close shut with a bang; hoof-beats of horses and elephants.
nantiṉiyiṉ kavaṉattai antac cattaŋkaɭ kavarntaṉa eṉpatai vantiyatteevaṉ aṟintu koɳʈaaṉ. kaaval purinta taatippeɳ tiʈukkiʈʈu eɻuntu caṟṟu arukil vantu, "ammaa! ejamaaṉ vantuviʈʈaar poolirukkiṟatu" eṉṟaaɭ. Vandiya Devan realized that those noises had distracted Nandini's attention. The maid sitting on guard came up, "My Lady! it seems that the Master has returned."
nantiṉi, "eṉakkut teriyum; nii uṉ iʈattukkup poo!" eṉṟaaɭ. Nandini said, "I know. Go back to your place."
piṟaku vantiyatteevaṉaip paarttu, "taṉaatikaari kooʈʈaiyil piraveecikkiṟaar. cakkaravarttiyiṉ kʂeemattai vicaarittu viʈʈu, kooʈʈait taɭapatiyaip paarttup peeciviʈʈu iŋkee varuvaar. varuvataṟkuɭ nii pooyviʈa veeɳʈum. aaɻvaarkkaʈiyaar kuuṟiya ceyti eṉṉa?" eṉṟu viṉaviṉaaɭ. She then turned towards Vandiya Devan, "The Finance Officer is entering the fort. He will go to the palace to enquire after the health of the Emperor. Then meet and talk with the Commander and then come here. You must depart before he comes here. What was the message from Mr. Azlvar-adiyan Nambi?" she asked.
"ammaɳi! anta viira vaiʂɳava cikaamaɳi taŋkaɭai avaruʈaiya cakootari eṉṟu collik koɳʈaar. atu uɳmai taaṉaa?" eṉṟu vallavaraiyaṉ keeʈʈaaṉ. "My Lady! That fanatic Vaishnava fellow declared that you were his sister. Is that true?"
"ataippaṟṟi nii eeṉ canteekappaʈukiṟaay?" "Why do you disbelieve that?"
"paccaik kiɭiyum kaʈuvaṉ kuraŋkum oru taayiṉ kuɻantaikaɭ eṉṟaal eɭitil nampa muʈiyumaa?" "Can I easily believe, if someone says that the jeweled parrot and the wild ape are born of the same mother?"
nantiṉi cirittuviʈʈu, "oruvitattil avar coṉṉatu uɳmaitaaṉ. naaŋkaɭ oree viiʈʈil oree kuʈumpattil vaɭarntoom. uʈaṉ piṟanta taŋkaiyaip poolavee eṉṉiʈam piriyam vaittiruntaar. paavam! avarukkup perum eemaaṟṟam aɭittu viʈʈeeṉ!" Nandini laughed, "In a way what he said is correct. We grew up in the same house in the same family. He loved and cherished me as his own sister. It's a pity! I disappointed him greatly."
"appaʈiyaaṉaal cari! aaɻvaarkkaʈiyaar taŋkaɭukkuc colli aṉuppiya ceyti kiruʂɳapakavaaṉ taŋkaɭukkaakak kaattuk koɳʈirukkiṟaar eṉpatutaaṉ. taaŋkaɭ kaɳɳaṉai maɳantu koɭɭum kaliyaaɳak kaaʈciyaip paarkka viira vaiʂɳava pakta kooʈikaɭum kaattuk koɳʈirukkiṟaarkaɭaam!" "Then it is alright. The message from Mr. Nambi is this: God Krishna is waiting for you. All the ardent Vaishnava devotees are waiting anxiously to see you wed the holy Lord Krishna!"
nantiṉi oru perumuuccu viʈʈaaɭ. "aakaa! iṉṉum avarukku antac capalam niiŋkavillai. poolirukkiṟatu! nii avaraip paarttaal eṉakkaaka itaic colliviʈu! eṉṉai aʈiyooʈu maṟantuviʈac collu! aaɳʈaaɭaip pool paramapaktaiyaakak koɲcamum takutiyaṟṟavaɭ naaṉ eṉṟu collu!" Nandini signed loudly. "Ah! He has not forgotten those foolish dreams. If you see him again tell him this from me: Ask him to completely forget me. Tell him that I am in no way deserving to become a great apostle like Andal."
"naaṉ atai oppuk koɭɭavillai, ammaa!" "I don't agree with that, My Lady."
"eṉṉattai oppukkoɭɭavillai?" "What don't you agree with?"
"taaŋkaɭ aaɳʈaaɭ aaka muʈiyaatu eṉpataittaaṉ oppuk koɭɭavillai. aaɳʈaaɭ pakti ceytu, paaʈʈup paaʈi, aɻutu kaɳɳiir viʈʈu, puumaalai toʈuttuc cuuʈʈi, - ippaʈiyellaam ceytu kaɳɳaṉai maɳantu koɭɭa veeɳʈiyiruntatu. aaṉaal taŋkaɭukku attakaiya kaʂʈamee teevaiyillai. taŋkaɭaik kiruʂɳapakavaaṉ paarttuviʈa veeɳʈiyatutaaṉ. rukmaɳi, cattiya paamaavaiyum, raataavaiyum, koopikaastiriikaɭaiyum uʈaṉee kaiviʈʈu avarkaɭ viiṟṟirunta cimmaacaṉattil taŋkaɭai eeṟṟi uʈkaara vaittuviʈuvaar!" "I don't agree with your saying that you cannot become another apostle like Andal! Andal had to immerse herself in devotion and worship, sing verse and psalm -- shed tears and weave flower garlands before she could marry the Lord. But you will have no such difficulty. All that is needed is that Lord Krishna see you -- He will forsake all those divine maids like Rukmini, Bhama, Radha and the mystic milkmaids. He will seat you on his throne besides him!"
"aiyaa! niir mukastuti ceyvatil camarttaraayirukkiṟiir! atu eṉakkup piʈippateeyillai." "Sir! You are an expert in flattery. I have never liked that."
"ammaɳi! mukastuti eṉṟaal eṉṉavoo?" "My Lady, what is the meaning of flattery?"
"mukattukku neeree oruvaraip pukaɻvatutaaṉ." "Praising someone to their face."
"appaʈiyaaṉaal caṟṟee niiŋkaɭ tirumpi mutukaik kaaʈʈik koɳʈu uʈkaaruŋkaɭ..." "If so, please turn around a little and show me your back."
"etaṟkaaka?" "Why? ..."
"mukattaip paarkkaamal mutukaip paarttuk koɳʈu pukaɻcci kuuṟuvataṟkaakattaaṉ. atil oṉṟum tavaṟu illaiyallavaa?" "To praise you, without looking at your face. That's why. Is there anything wrong in that?"
"niir peeccil mika keʈʈikkaararaayirukkiṟiir." "You talk very cleverly."
"ippootu taaŋkaɭ allavaa mukastuti ceykiṟiirkaɭ?" "Now, you are flattering me!"
"niirum umatu mukattait tiruppikkoɳʈu, mutukaik kaaʈʈuvatutaaṉee?" "Why don't you also turn around, and show me your back?"
"makaaraaɳi! poorkkaɭattilaakaʈʈum, peɳmaɳikaɭiʈamaakaʈʈum, naaṉ mutuku kaaʈʈuvatu eppootum kiʈaiyaatu. taaŋkaɭ taaraaɭamaay eṉṉai mukastuti ceyyalaam!" "Great Queen, it is never my habit, be it in the battlefield or in the presence of women, to turn my back. You can freely flatter me."
itaik keeʈʈu viʈʈu nantiṉi 'kaliir' eṉṟu cirittaaɭ! Upon hearing this, Nandini laughed loudly, gleefully.
"niir mantiravaatitaaṉ; canteekamillai, naaṉ immaatiri vaayviʈʈuc cirittu veku kaalam aayiṟṟu!" eṉṟu coṉṉaaɭ. "You are surely a magician. There is no doubt now. It has been a long time since I laughed so joyously."
"aaṉaal, ammaɳi! taŋkaɭaic cirikkap paɳɳuvatu veku apaayam! taʈaakattil taamarai cirittu makiɻntatu; teeṉ vaɳʈu mayaŋki viɻuntatu!" eṉṟaaṉ vantiyatteevaṉ. "But My Lady, it is very dangerous to make you laugh: the lotus in the pool bloomed in joyous laughter; the honey bee fell swooning down!" said Vandiya Devan.
"niir mantiravaati maʈʈumalla; kaviyum poolirukkiṟatee!" "You are not only a magician; you also seem to be a kavi!" (The Tamil word kavi has two meanings: 1. poet 2. monkey.)
"naaṉ mukastutikkum aɲcamaaʈʈeeṉ; vacavukkum kalaŋka maaʈʈeeṉ." "I am not afraid of flattery; nor do I fear scolds."
"ummai yaar vaitatu?" "Who scolded you?"
"caṟṟu muṉ eṉṉaik 'kavi' eṉṟiirkaɭee?" "Did you not call me `kavi' just now?"
"appaʈiyeṉṟaal?" "So?"
"naaṉ ciṟuvaṉaayiruntapootu eṉṉaic cilar 'kuraŋku muuɲci!' eṉṟu colvatuɳʈu. veku naaɭaikkup piṟaku iṉṟaikkuttaaṉ taŋkaɭuʈaiya pavaɭac cevvaayiṉaal ataik keeʈʈeeṉ." "When I was a youngster, some of my playmates used to call me `monkey face.' After many years, I have heard that nickname uttered by your coral red lips!"
"ummaiyaa kuraŋku muuɲci eṉṟaarkaɭ? yaar appaʈip paʈʈa putticaalikaɭ?" "You were called `monkey face!' Who were those scholars?"
"avarkaɭil yaarum ippootu uyirooʈillai." "None of them are alive now."
"ummai naaṉ avvita collavillai. kavipaaʈak kuuʈiyavar poolirukkiṟatee eṉṟu coṉṉeeṉ." "I didn't call you that! I said you seemed like a poet able to compose verse!"
"koɲcam kaviyum paaʈuveeṉ. aaṉaal pakaivarkaɭukku muṉṉaaltaaṉ paaʈuveeṉ. villampiṉaal caakaatavarkaɭ, collampiṉaal caakaʈʈum eṉṟu!" "Oh yes! I can also compose verse; but I usually sing in front of my enemies. Those who do not die by my piercing arrows can die by my piercing words!"
"aiyaa, kaviraaja viiraciŋkamee! ummuʈaiya peyar eṉṉaveṉṟu iṉṉamum collavillaiyee?" "My dear Sir! Oh you bravest lion among poets! You have not yet told me your name?" asked Nandini with laughter.
"eṉ contap peyar vantiyatteevaṉ; paʈʈap peyar vallavaraiyaṉ." "My given name is Vandiya Devan. I also have the title Vallavarayan."
"araca kulattiṉaraa?" "Of a noble family?"
"paɻaiya pukaɻ peṟṟa vaaɳaati raajar kulattil vantavaṉ!" "I come from the ancient and famous Vaanar clan."
"ippootu uŋkaɭ raajyam?" "These days ... Your kingdom ...?"
"meelee aakaacam! kiiɻee puumi; ippootu naaṉ cakala puumaɳʈalattukkum eeka cakraatipati!" "The sky above me; the earth below my feet; now I am the sole emperor of all the known worlds!"
nantiṉi ciṟitu neeram vallavaraiyaṉai eeṟattaaɻap paarttuk koɳʈiruntaaɭ. Nandini examined Vallavarayan from head to toe for sometime.
"appaʈi oṉṟum naʈakkaata kaariyam illai. ummuʈaiya puurviika raajyattai niir tirumpavum peṟalaam." "There is nothing impossible. You can get back your ancient territories."
"atu eppaʈi caattiyam? puliyiṉ vayiṟṟukkuɭɭee pooṉatu tirumpavum varumaa? cooɻa caamraajyattil ceernta aracu tirumpak kiʈaikkumaa?" "How is that possible? How can anything swallowed by the tiger come out again? Can I get back my kingdom swallowed by the Chozla Empire?"
"kiʈaikkumpaʈi ceyya eṉṉaal muʈiyum." "I can arrange to get it back!"
"ammaɳi! veeɳʈaam! iraajyam aaɭum aacai eṉakku eppootum kiʈaiyaatu. koɲcam iruntatum iṉṟaikkuc cuntara cooɻa cakkaravarttiyaip paartta piṟaku aʈiyooʈu pooyviʈʈatu. immaatiri piṟar kaiyai etirpaarttuc cakkaravarttiyaayiruppataik kaaʈʈilum maṟu naaɭ uɳavu eŋkee kiʈaikkum eṉṟu teriyaata cutantira maṉitaṉaayiruppatee meel." "My Lady! Please don't! No need! I never had any wish to rule a kingdom. Whatever tiny wish I had, it totally vanished today, after I saw Emperor Sundara Chozla. It is better to remain a free pauper without knowing where my next meal comes from than be a prince beholden to everyone."
"eṉṉuʈaiya karuttum atutaaṉ!" eṉṟaaɭ nantiṉi. piṟaku eetoo maṟantupooṉa viʂayattai ɲaapakappaʈuttik koɳʈavaɭ pool, "ciṉṉap paɻuveeʈʈaraiyariṉ aaʈkaɭ ummai etaṟkaakat teeʈukiṟaarkaɭ?" eṉṟu keeʈʈaaɭ. "That is my opinion too." Nandini spoke again as if she remembered something that she had forgotten. "Why are the Commander's men looking for you?"
"taŋkaɭuʈaiya taatip peɳɳaip pool avarukkum eṉ peeril canteekam uɳʈaakiviʈʈatu." "Like your maid, he too had a suspicion about me!"
"eṉṉa canteekam?" "What suspicion?"
"paṉai ilacciṉai uɭɭa muttirai mootiram eṉṉiʈam eppaʈi vantatu eṉṟu?" "About how I came to posses a signet ring with the palmtree symbol."
nantiṉiyiṉ mukattil payattiṉ caayal ciṟitu teṉpaʈʈatu. A tiny shade of fear engulfed Nandini's face.
"mootiram eŋkee?" eṉṟu tiʈukkiʈʈa kuralil keeʈʈaaɭ. "Where is the ring?" she asked in a shaken voice.
"itoo irukkiṟatu, ammaɳi! ileecil ataip pookka aʈittu viʈuveeṉaa?" eṉṟu kuuṟikkoɳʈee mootirattai eʈuttuk kaaʈʈiṉaaṉ. "Here it is my Lady. Will I loose it so easily?" saying it, he took out the ring from his pouch and showed it to her.
"itu ummiʈam iruppatu avarukku eppaʈit terintatu?" eṉṟu nantiṉi keeʈʈaaɭ. "How did he know that you posses this?" asked Nandini.
"cuntara cooɻa cakkaravarttiyaip paarkka veeɳʈum eṉṟa aacai eṉ maṉattil neʈunaaɭaaka iruntatu. ataṟku inta muttirai mootirattai upayookappaʈuttik koɳʈeeṉ. paarttu muʈinta piṟaku inta mootiram eṉṉiʈam eppaʈi vantatu eṉṟu kooʈʈait taɭapati keeʈʈaar..." "For a long time now, I had the wish to meet Emperor Sundara Chozla. I used this signet ring to fulfil that ambition. After being presented to the Emperor, the Commander asked me about how I came to possess this ring ..."
"niir eṉṉa coṉṉiir!" eṉṟu nantiṉi viṉaaviya kuralil tikil toṉittatu. "What reply did you give?" asked Nandini in a frightened voice.
"taŋkaɭ peyaraic collavillai, ammaɳi! periya paɻuveeʈʈaraiyar koʈuttaar eṉṟu coṉṉeeṉ..." "I did not bring up your name, My Lady! I said that the Elder Lord Pazluvoor had given it. I said that he gave it to me at Kadamboor ...."
nantiṉi perumuuccu viʈʈaaɭ. avaɭ mukattilum kuralilum irunta tikil niiŋkiyatu. The fear on her face and voice had now disappeared,
"niir coṉṉatai avar nampiṉaaraa?" eṉṟu keeʈʈaaɭ. "Did he believe you?" she asked.
"muɻutum nampiyataakat teriyavillai. ataṉaaltaaṉee eṉṉaip piṉ toʈarumpaʈi aaʈkaɭai viʈʈirukka veeɳʈum? tamaiyaṉaar tirumpi vantatum eṉṉai avar muṉṉaal niṟutti uɳmaiyai aṟiya eɳɳiyirukkalaam!" eṉṟaaṉ vantiyatteevaṉ. "I don't think he believed me fully. That's why he must have sent those guards to follow me. He might have intended to place me before his elder brother and find the truth when he returned."
nantiṉi puṉṉakai purintu, "periya paɻuveeʈʈaraiyariʈam niir payappaʈaveeɳʈaam. avar ummaik kaʈittut tiṉṟuviʈaamal naaṉ paarttukkoɭkiṟeeṉ" eṉṟaaɭ. Nandini said with a smile, "You don't have to fear the Elder Lord. I shall take care that he does not chew you out."
"ammaɳi! taṉaatikaariyiṉ peeril taŋkaɭuʈaiya celvaakku evvaɭavu eṉpatu ulakam aṟinta ceyti. aaṉaal eṉakku veɭiyil avacara kaariyam irukkiṟatu. aakaiyiṉaal taaṉ tappic cellat taŋkaɭ utaviyaik koorukiṟeeṉ." "My Lady! The whole world knows of your influence over the Finance Officer. But I have some urgent chores outside the fort. That's why I seek your help in escaping."
"appaʈi eṉṉa avacara veelai irukkiṟatu?" "What are those urgent chores?"
"ettaṉaiyoo irukkiṟatu. utaaraɳamaaka aaɻvaarkkaʈiyaaraip paarttu taŋkaɭ maṟumoɻiyaic colla veeɳʈum. avarukku eṉṉa collaʈʈum?" "I have several. For example I have to meet Mr. Azlvar-adiyan and give him your reply. What shall I tell him?"
"avarukku 'nantiṉi eṉṟu oru cakootari iruntaaɭ' eṉpatai aʈiyooʈu maṟantuviʈumpaʈi collu!" "Tell him to completely forget that he had a sister known as Nandini."
"colliviʈalaam; aaṉaal naʈakkiṟa kaariyamillai" "I can tell him that; but it may not be possible."
"etu?" "What?"
"taŋkaɭai maṟappatutaaṉ. iraɳʈu taʈavai taṟceyalaakap paartta eṉṉaaleeyee taŋkaɭai maṟakkamuʈiyaatu poolirukkiṟatee! vaaɻnaaɭellaam taŋkaɭooʈu iruntavaraal eppaʈi maṟakkamuʈiyum?" "Forgetting you completely. I who have casually met you twice cannot forget you. How can he who spent his lifetime with you, forget you?"
nantiyiṉ mukattil veṟṟip perumitattiṉ caayal pariɳamittatu. avaɭuʈaiya veelviɻikaɭ vantiyatteevaṉuʈaiya neɲcai uuʈuruviṉa pool nookkiṉa. A trace of victory and pride dwelt on Nandini's face. Her lance like eyes pierced through Vandiya Devan's heart as she looked at him.
"cakkaravarttiyaip paarppataṟku niir eeṉ avvaɭavu aaval koɳʈiruntiir?" eṉṟu keeʈʈaaɭ. "Why were you so eager to meet the Emperor?"
"ulakap piracitti peṟṟa antac cuntara puruʂaraip paarkka naaṉ virumpiyatil viyappu eṉṉa? ulakattil viira maṉṉarkaɭ taŋkaɭ viiramum pauruʂamum peruka veeɳʈum eṉṟum, iraajyamum kiirttiyum vistarikka veeɳʈum eṉṟum virumpuvaarkaɭ. avvitamee pirajaikaɭaip piraarttaṉai ceyyumpaʈiyum colvaarkaɭ. aaṉaal nammuʈaiya cakkaravarttiyaip paṟṟip putta pikʂukkaɭiṉ maʈaŋkaɭil eṉṉa piraarttaṉai ceykiṟaarkaɭ? "What is so surprising about my wanting to see that world famous, handsome personality? Most monarchs on this world wish to establish the fame of their bravery, their prestige, and the grandeur of their empires for ever. Their subjects pray for such fame. But do you know what the buddhist monks in the monasteries pray for?
".................cuntarac
cooɻar vaɳmaiyum 'vaṉappum'
tiɳmaiyum ulakiṟ ciṟantu vaaɻkeṉavee"
... May Sundara Chozla
grow in prosperity, benevolence, and in good looks and health; ...
eṉṟu piraarttaṉai celuttukiṟaarkaɭ. ittakaiya kaliyuka maṉmataṉaip paarkkaveeɳʈum eṉṟu eṉakku veku naaɭaaka aacaiyaayiruntatu." I had a long time urge, to see this modern day Cupid, the God of Good Looks. ..."
"aamaam; cakkaravarttikkut tammuʈaiya aɻakaip paṟṟi rompap perumaitaaṉ. avaruʈaiya celvak kumaarikku ataiviʈa atika karvam..." "Yes; the Emperor is quite proud of his good looks. His darling girl is even more proud ....."
"kumaariyaa? yaaraic collukiṟiirkaɭ?" "His girl? ... Whom are you talking about?"
"paɻaiyaaṟaiyilee irukkiṟaaɭee, oru akampaavam piʈitta karvi, anta iɭaiyapiraaʈʈi kuntavai teeviyaittaaṉ collukiṟeeṉ." "She who lives at Pazlayarai. That arrogant, proud and prejudiced female -- I am talking about that Younger Pirati Kundavai Devi!"
vantiyatteevaa! nii atirʂʈakkaaraṉ. nii teeʈik koɳʈirunta upaayam itoo uṉ muṉṉaal taaṉee vantu niṟkiṟatu! atai naṉku upayookappaʈuttik koɭ! - ivvaaṟu vallavaraiyaṉ taṉakkuttaaṉee collikkoɳʈaaṉ. Vandiya Deva! You are fortunate! The tool you were searching for has come to you, here it is in your hands. Use it well! Vandiya Devan's mind advised him thus.
ittaṉai neeramum oyyaaramaakap paʈukkaiyil caayntu paʈuttirunta nantiṉi tiʈiireṉṟu eɻuntu nimirntu uʈkaarntaaɭ. Nandini, who had been reclining languorously all this time, now sat up suddenly.
"aiyaa! naaṉ oṉṟu colkiṟeeṉ. atai oppukkoɭviiraa?" eṉṟu keeʈʈaaɭ. "Sir! I shall tell you one thing. Will you agree?"
"colluŋkaɭ, ammaɳi!" "Tell me, My Lady!"
"niirum naaṉum or uʈaṉpaʈikkai ceytu koɭɭalaam. niir eṉakku utavi ceyyaveeɳʈiyatu; naaṉ umakku utavi ceyya veeɳʈiyatu; eṉṉa colkiṟiir!" "Let us, you and I make a pact. You will help me and I shall help you. What do you say?"
"ammaɳi! taaŋkaɭ cooɻa makaaraajyattil carva cakti vaaynta taṉaatikaariyiṉ raaɳi. niṉaittatai niṉaittapaʈi caatikkakkuuʈiya cakti vaayntavar. naaṉoo oruvitac celvaakkum illaatavaṉ. taŋkaɭukku naaṉ eṉṉa vitattil utavi ceyyamuʈiyum?" eṉṟaaṉ. "My Lady! You are the Queen of the all powerful Finance Officer of the Chozla Empire! You have the power to translate thought into deed. I am a nobody with no authority. In what way can I be of help to you?" he asked.
avaṉ uɭɭattiliruntu peecukiṟaaṉaa, utaʈʈiliruntu peecukiṟaaṉaa eṉṟu terintukoɭɭa virumpiya nantiṉi taṉ kuuriya viɻikaɭai avaṉ miitu celuttiṉaaɭ. Nandini wishing to find out if he was sincere or if he was merely mouthing words of courtesy, looked at him with piercing eyes.
vantiyatteevaṉ ataṟkuc ciṟitum kalaŋkaamal niṉṟaaṉ. He stood undisturbed.
"eṉakku antaraŋkamaaṉa paɳi aaɭ oruvar teevaiyaayirukkiṟatu. inta araɳmaṉaiyil umakku veelai vaaŋkik koʈuttaal, oppukkoɭviiraa?" eṉṟu keeʈʈaaɭ. "I need a confidential retainer. If I find you some such job in this palace, will you accept?" she asked.
"itee maatiri ceevaiyai iṉṉoru maataracikkuc ceyvataaka eeṟkaṉavee oppukkoɳʈu viʈʈeeṉ. avaɭ veeɳʈaameṉṟu niraakarittaal, taŋkaɭiʈam varukiṟeeṉ." "I have already agreed to serve another Lady in such a fashion. If she refuses to accept me, I shall surely come back to you."
"atu yaar avaɭ, eṉṉooʈu pooʈʈikku varukiṟavaɭ?" "Who is she? In competition with me?"
"caṟṟu muṉ mikap piriyattooʈu peeciṉiirkaɭee, anta iɭaiyapiraaʈʈi kuntavai teevitaaṉ." "You talked with great affection about her a little while ago, that very same Younger Pirati Kundavai Devi."
"poy! poy! appaʈi orunaaɭum irukkamuʈiyaatu! eṉṉai veeʈikkai ceyyap paarkkiṟiir..." "Lies! Lies! It can never be possible! Are you jesting with me ..."
"makaaraaɳi! inta olaiyai eeṟkaṉavee palar tiruʈip paarttuviʈʈaarkaɭ. aakaiyaal taaŋkaɭum itaip paarppatiṉaal moocam oṉṟum vantuviʈaatu!" eṉṟu collik koɳʈee vantiyatteevaṉ aatitta karikaalar kuntavaikkuk koʈutta olaiyai eʈuttu niiʈʈiṉaaṉ. "Great Queen, several persons have already stolen and read this letter. There can be no harm in you also reading this." Saying this, Vandiya Devan extended the letter given by Aditya Karikala to Kundavai.
nantiṉi olaiyai viɭakkiṉaʈiyil piʈittukkoɳʈu paʈittaaɭ. paʈittu muʈittapootu avaɭuʈaiya kaɳkaɭiliruntu kiɭampiya miṉṉal juvaalai naaka carppattiṉ vaayiliruntu veɭivantu maṟaiyum ataṉ piɭavupaʈʈa naavai vantiyatteevaṉukku niṉaivuuʈʈiyatu. avaṉaiyaṟiyaamal avaṉ uʈampu naʈuŋkiyatu. Nandini held the letter under the lamp light and read it. As she was reading, the lightning darts that jumped out of her eyes seemed like the forked, darting tongue of a king cobra to Vandiya Devan. Unconsciously, his body shivered.
nantiṉi kampiira paavattuʈaṉ vantiyatteevaṉaip paarttu, "aiyaa! niir intak kooʈʈaiyiliruntu uyirooʈu tappic cella eɳɳukiṟiir allavaa?" eṉṟu keeʈʈaaɭ. Nandini looked at him with great dignity, "Sir! You intend to escape from this fort with your life, don't you?"
"aam, ammaa! ataṟkuttaaṉ taŋkaɭ utaviyai naaʈi vanteeṉ." "Yes, My Lady, that is why I sought your help and came here."
"oru nipantaṉaiyiṉ peeriltaaṉ ummait tappittuviʈa naaṉ utavi ceyyalaakum." "Only on one condition will I help you escape from here."
"nipantaṉaiyaic colluŋkaɭ!" "Tell me the condition, My Lady."
"inta olaikkuk kuntavai eṉṉa maṟu olai koʈukkiṟaaɭoo, atai maṟupaʈiyum, eṉṉiʈam koɳʈuvantu kaaʈʈa veeɳʈum. cammatamaa?" "Whatever letter Kundavai gives in reply to this, you must bring that and come back to me. Do you agree?"
"mika apaayamaaṉa nipantaṉai pooʈukiṟiirkaɭ!" "A very dangerous condition!"
"apaayattukku aɲcaatavar eṉṟu caṟṟu muṉṉaal perumai aʈittuk koɳʈiiree?" "You boasted some time ago about not minding any danger."
"apaayattukkut tuɳivatu eṉṟaal ataṟkut takunta paricu kiʈʈa veeɳʈum allavaa?..." "Being courageous in such danger should merit an appropriate reward? ..."
"paricaa? paricaa veeɳʈum? niir kaṉavilum aʈaiyak karutaata paricu umakkuk kiʈaikkum. cooɻa caamraajyattil iṉṟu carva cakti vaayntavaraay viɭaŋkum periya paɻuveeʈʈaraiyar entap paricukkaaka varuʂakkaɳakkaakat tavam kiʈakkiṟaaroo attakaiya paricu umakkuk kiʈaikkum!" eṉṟu kuuṟi nantiṉi vantiyatteevaṉ peeril maṟupaʈiyum mookaṉaastirattait tuuviṉaaɭ. "Reward? You want a reward? You will get a reward beyond your wildest dreams. You will get a reward for which, the all powerful chief of this Chozla Empire, the Elder Lord Pazluvoor has been yearning for years. Such a reward will be yours!" saying this, Nandini once again threw her weapons of enchantment at Vandiya Devan.
paavam! vallavaraiyaṉuʈaiya talai cuɻaṉṟatu. neɲcee! tairiyattai kaʈaippiʈi! aṟivai iɻantuviʈaatee! - eṉṟu taṉakkuɭ collik koɳʈaaṉ. Poor Vandiya Devan! His head swam in dizziness. He muttered to himself: "Dear heart be courageous, don't loose your reason."
accamayam avaṉukkut tuɳai ceyya vantataip pool arukiluɭɭa tooʈʈattiliruntu aantaiyiṉ kaʈuuramaaṉa kural keeʈʈatu. In that moment, as if to help him, the horrible screech of an owl could be heard in the garden.
oru taʈavai, iraɳʈu taʈavai, muuṉṟu taʈavai keeʈʈatu. Once, twice, thrice -- they heard the hoot.
vantiyatteevaṉuʈaiya uʈampu cilirttatu. His body shivered.
nantiṉi tooʈʈattil aantaik kural vanta iʈattai nookki "nija mantiravaatiyee vantuviʈʈaaṉ!" eṉṟaaɭ. Nandini looked towards the garden -- from where the hoots were heard -- saying, "The real sorcerer has come."
piṟaku vantiyatteevaṉaip paarttu "avaṉ eṉakku iṉit teevaiyillai. aaṉaalum iraɳʈu vaarttai avaṉiʈam colli aṉuppukiṟeeṉ. oru veeɭai, ummait tappittu viʈuvataṟku avaṉ upayookamaayirukkalaam. caṟṟu neeram niir atoo antap pakkam pooy iruʈʈil maṟaintu nillum!" eṉṟu muṉṉam avaɭuʈaiya taatip peɳ pooṉa ticaikku neer etirt ticaiyaik kaaʈʈiṉaaɭ. She looked at Vandiya Devan again and continued, "I don't need him anymore. But let me say a few words to him before sending him away. Perhaps he will be useful in helping you escape. Please step aside for a while and hide yourself in that darkness!" She pointed in a direction quite opposite to the doorway in which her maid sat guarding the path.
