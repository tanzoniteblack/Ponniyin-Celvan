34. lataa maɳʈapam Chapter 34 -- Garden Gazebo
aʈarnta maantooppukkiʈaiyee ceṉṟa oṟṟaiyaʈip paataiyiṉ vaɻiyaaka am maŋkai viʈuviʈuveṉṟu naʈantu cella, vantiyatteevaṉum viraivaakat toʈarntu ceṉṟaaṉ. maraɲceʈikaɭiṉ miitu mootik koɭɭaamal anta iruɭil naʈantu celluvatu kaʂʈamaakattaaṉ iruntatu. oru camayam ivaṉ marattil mootik koɭɭap paarttut tayaŋki niṉṟapootu, anta maŋkai tirumpip paarttu, "eeṉ niṟkiṟaay? vaɻi maṟantu pooy viʈʈataa? niitaaṉ iruʈʈil kaɳ terikiṟa maṉitaṉ aayiṟṟee!" eṉṟaaɭ. The maiden walked briskly down the garden trail winding through a thick grove of mango trees. Vandiya Devan followed as quickly as he could. It was quite difficult to walk in that darkness without running into some bush or creeper. Once, when he hesitated, almost colliding against a flowering bush, the girl looked back to ask, "Why do you stop? Have you forgotten the way? Are you not the fellow who can see in darkness?"
ataṟkup patilaaka vantiyatteevaṉ utaʈʈil viralai vaittu muṉṉaal avaɭ coṉṉatu pool "uʂ!" eṉṟaaṉ. atee neerattil matil cuvarukku veɭiyee eetoo captam keeʈʈatu. maṉita naʈamaaʈʈam poolat toṉittatu. piṟaku iruvarum naʈantaarkaɭ. koɲca tuuram pooṉatum vallavaraiyaṉ ileecaakac cirittaaṉ. anta maŋkai tirumpip paarttu, "eṉṉattaik kaɳʈu cirikkiṟaay?" eṉṟaaɭ. In reply, Vandiya Devan imitated her earlier action and placed his finger upon his lips saying, "Ushh!" They could now hear some noises outside the garden wall and they could hear the presence of some men. They walked on further. Vandiya Devan laughed lightly when they had gone some distance. The girl looked back and asked, "What did you see, to laugh at?"
"kaɳʈu cirikkavillai; keeʈʈuc cirikkiṟeeṉ!" eṉṟaaṉ. "I didn't see anything -- to laugh at. I heard and laughed."
"appaʈiyeṉṟaal?..." "What do you mean?"
"eṉṉait teeʈi vantavarkaɭiṉ kaalaʈic cattattaic caṟṟu muṉ nii keeʈkavillaiyaa? avarkaɭ eemaantu pooṉatai eɳɳic cirikkiṟeeṉ!" "Did you not hear the footsteps of the men who came in search of me? I laughed thinking of them being fooled."
avaɭ ciṟitu payattuʈaṉ, "uṉṉai yaaraavatu teeʈi varukiṟaarkaɭaa, eṉṉa? etaṟkaaka?" eṉṟaaɭ. She now asked with some fear, "What? Is someone looking for you? Why?"
"illaaviʈʈaal etaṟkaaka intak kuruʈʈu iruʈʈil matil cuvaril vantu mootik koɳʈu uʈkaarntirukka veeɳʈum?" "Otherwise why should I be sitting in this pitch darkness, leaning against the wall?"
accamayam kaaṟṟiṉ acaivil marakkiɭaikaɭ vilaki nilaak katir oṉṟu vantiyatteevaṉuʈaiya mukattiṉ miitu viɻuntatu. The breeze parted the upper branches to throw a moonbeam on Vandiya Devan's face.
antap peɳ caṟṟu viyappuʈaṉum tikaippuʈaṉum avaṉaip paarttaaɭ. The girl now looked at him with some surprise and incredulity.
"eṉṉa paarkkiṟaay?" eṉṟu keeʈʈaaṉ. "What are you staring at?" asked Vandiya Devan.
"nii, niitaaṉaa eṉṟu paartteeṉ!" "I wondered if you were you!"
"naaṉ, naaṉ illaaviʈʈaal veeṟu yaaraayiruppeeṉ?" "If I am not me, who else will I be?"
"pooṉataʈavai nii vantiruntapootu periya miicai vaittiruntaayee!" "When you came last time, did you not have a big moustache?"
"nalla keeɭvi keeʈkiṟaay! eṉṉaip pool cuvar eeṟik kutittu varukiṟavaṉ aʈikkaʈi veeʂattai maaṟṟik koɭɭaaviʈʈaal eppaʈi?" "What a question! How can fellows like me who have to come in by jumping over walls not change their disguise?"
"muṉṉaikku ippootu iɭamaiyaayt tooṉṟukiṟaayee?" "You look younger than the last time!"
"uṟcaakam irukkumpootu iɭamai taaṉee varukiṟatu!" "Youth comes with enthusiasm!"
"appaʈi uṉakku eṉṉa uṟcaakam vantatu?" "Why the enthusiasm?"
"uŋkaɭ makaaraaɳiyiṉ tayavu irukkumpootu uṟcaakattukku eṉṉa kuṟaivu?" "Why lack enthusiasm when I have the patronage of a great queen like yours?"
"parikaacam ceyya veeɳʈaam. iṉṟaikku eŋkaɭ ejamaaṉi iɭaiya raaɳitaaṉ. orunaaɭ niccayamaaka makaaraaɳi aavaarkaɭ!" "Don't jest! Today my mistress is merely the Young-Queen. One day she will surely become The Great Queen!"
"ataittaaṉ naaṉum collukiṟeeṉ." "That is what I said."
"itutaaṉaa colvaay? uṉṉuʈaiya mantira caktiyiṉaal taaṉ makaaraaɳi aaṉaarkaɭ eṉṟukuuʈac colvaay! paati raajyattaik koʈu eṉṟu keeʈʈaalum keeʈpaay!" "Is that all you can say! Perhaps you will declare that she became the Queen because of your magic and sorcery. Perhaps you will even demand half the kingdom."
vantiyatteevaṉ, aṟiya virumpiyatai oruvaaṟu aṟintu koɳʈaaṉ. piṟaku avaṉ oṉṟum peecavillai. tiiviramaaka yoocittuk koɳʈee naʈantaaṉ. Vandiya Devan now guessed half of what he wanted to find out. He did not talk further but walked on in deep thought.
taaṉ cantikkappookiṟatu yaarai? paɻuvuur iɭaiya raaɳiyaayirukkalaam. allatu maturaantakat teevarai maɳantu koɳʈa ciṉṉap paɻuveeʈʈaraiyariṉ makaɭaayirukkalaam. taṉṉai mantiravaatiyeṉṟu eɳɳi antap peɳ aɻaittukkoɳʈu pookiṟaaɭ. pooy, anta 'iɭaiyaraaɳi' yaaraayiruntaalum avaɭaic cantikkumpootu eppaʈi naʈantukoɭvatu? - neɲcamee! tairiyattaik kaiviʈaatee! tairiyam uɭɭa varaiyil jayamum uɳʈu! camayattil eeteeṉum yukti tooṉṟaamal pookaatu! itu varaiyil enta nerukkaʈiyilum naam toolviyuṟṟu vantatillai. atilum peɳ piɭɭai oruttiyiʈamaa toolviyaʈaiyap pookiṟoom? Whom am I going to meet? It may be the Young-Queen of Pazluvoor. Or, it could be the Princess who married Prince Madurandaka, who is the daughter of the Commander. This maid is leading me on, thinking I am some magician or fortuneteller. How should I behave when I meet that Young-Queen, whoever she is? -- Dear heart! Do not give up your courage! Courage will lead to victory. Some idea will occur at the right moment. I have survived all sorts of tricky situations so far .. How can I loose in front of a woman?
oru periya maaɭikaiyai avarkaɭ neruŋkic ceṉṟaarkaɭ. aaṉaal maaɭikaiyiṉ muṉ vaacalai nookkic cellavillai. piṉpuṟa vaacalaiyum neruŋkavillai. maaɭikaiyiṉ oru pakkattil tooʈʈattukkuɭ niiʈʈiviʈʈirunta ciruŋkaara lataa maɳʈapattai neruŋkiṉaarkaɭ. They neared a large mansion. But they did not go around to the front porch. Neither did they go towards the back doorway. They walked towards a creeper laden pathway that connected to a flower laden bower -- a garden gazebo.
iṉṉum arukil neruŋkiya pootu, anta lataa maɳʈapam iraɳʈu periya pirammaaɳʈamaaṉa maaɭikaikaɭai oṉṟu ceerkkum paataiyaippool amaintiruppatu terintatu. appaʈic ceerkkappaʈʈa iru kaʈʈiʈaŋkaɭum oruvitattil maaṟupaʈʈiruntaṉa. valatupuṟattu maaɭikai ataṉ uɭɭee cuʈar viʈʈu erinta pala tiipaŋkaɭiṉaal jolittuk koɳʈiruntatu. uɭɭiruntu palavita kalakalappaaṉa toṉikaɭ vantu koɳʈiruntaṉa. iʈatupuṟattuk kaʈʈiʈattiloo, oru ciṉṉaɲciṟu tiipamkuuʈa eriyavillai. nilaa veɭiccattil ataṉ veɭic cuvarkaɭ neʈituyarntu terintaṉa. aaṉaal anta maaɭikaiyiṉ uɭɭee nicaptamum iruɭum kuʈikoɳʈiruntaṉa. Upon coming closer he saw that the flower arbor was situated in the middle of two mansions and connected to both by covered walkways. The mansions connected by the gazebo differed from each other in one important way. The mansion on the right was shining with brilliant lights that could be seen through its windows. They could hear the noise and activity of several persons inside it. There was not even a tiny lamplight in the building to the left. He could see its tall walls in the moonlight. But silence and darkness dwelt in that mansion.
vantiyatteevaṉai aɻaittuk koɳʈu vanta peɳ, lataa maɳʈapattai aɳukiyatum avaṉaip paarttuc camikɲaiyiṉaal aŋkeeyee niṟkumpaʈi coṉṉaaɭ. avaṉum appaʈiyee niṉṟaaṉ. avvitam niṉṟapootu taaṉ anta iʈattil niṟaintirunta malarkaɭiṉ naṟumaɳattai avaṉ uɳarntaaṉ. appappaa! eṉṉa vaacam! eṉṉa vaacam! muukkil neʈipoola eeṟit talaiyaik kiṟukiṟukka aʈikkiṟatee! Upon reaching the steps of the gazebo, the maid made a sign for Vandiya Devan to wait. He stood on the steps. His nostrils now realized the fragrance of the blooming flowers around him. What aroma! What a heady fragrance! They filled his nose and made his head swim.
antap peɳ lataa maɳʈapattukkuɭ nuɻaintatum, avaɭuʈaiya kuralum iṉṉoru iṉiya peɳ kuralum keeʈʈaṉa. "varac col uʈaṉee! keeʈpaaṉeeṉ? naaṉ taaṉ ittaṉai neeramaayk kaattirukkiṟeeṉ eṉṟu teriyumaa?" eṉṟa coṟkaɭ avaṉukku mayakkattai uɳʈaakkiṉa. antak kural paɻuvuur iɭaiya raaɳiyiṉ kuraltaaṉ! canteekamillai! The maid entered the gazebo; he could hear the noises of her talking to someone else with a pleasant voice. "Ask him to come in instantly! Why ask? Haven't I been waiting here all this while?" the words filled him with dizziness! It was the voice of the Young-Queen of Pazluvoor. No doubt about it: It was Nandini Devi.
aʈutta kaɳam avaɭ muṉṉaal pooy niṟkap pookiṟoom. anta nilaimaiyai evvitam camaaɭikkap pookiṟoom? etirpaartta mantiravaatikkup patilaakap pallakkil vantu mootiya maṉitaṉ vantu niṟpataik kaɳʈu avaɭ eṉṉa niṉaippaaɭ. aaccariyappaʈuvaaɭaa? koopam koɭvaaɭaa? oru veeɭai makiɻcci aʈaivaaɭaa?... allatu entavita uɳarcciyaiyum veɭiyil kaaʈʈaamal naʈantu koɭvaayaa? I am going to stand in front of her this next minute ... How am I going to manage that situation? What will she think upon finding the man who dashed against her palanquin, instead of the soothsayer she was expecting? Will she be surprised? Show anger? Perhaps be happy? .. Or maybe, appear emotionless?
avaṉai aɻaittu vanta maŋkai lataa maɳʈapattu vaacalil niṉṟapaʈi camikɲaiyaal aɻaittaaɭ The maid who had led him there made a sign from the doorway.
vantiyatteevaṉ avaɭ niṉṟa iʈattai aʈaintu maɳʈapattiṉ uʈpuṟam nookkiṉaaṉ. oru noʈip poɻutil aŋkee tooṉṟiya kaaʈci avaṉ kaɳ vaɻiyaaka maṉattil patintatu. Vandiya Devan went up and looked into the flower arbor. In one second, the scene before him was registered by his eyes to be embossed in his mind.
taŋka viɭakku stampattil oɭirnta tiipaccuʈar poṉ oɭiyaip parappiyatu. eetoo or apuurvamaaṉa vaacaṉait tailattai anta viɭakkil viʈʈirukka veeɳʈum. aataliṉ tiipac cuʈariṉ pukai kamakamaveṉṟu maɳam viiciṟṟu. pala varɳa naṟumaɳa malarkaɭaip parappiya caprakuuʈa maɲcattil oru peɳ oyyaaramaaka caayntu koɳʈu viiṟṟiruntaaɭ. avaɭ paɻuvuur iɭaiya raaɳitaaṉ. pakalil pallakkil paarttapootu avaɭ aɻakiyaakat tooṉṟiṉaaɭ. iravil taŋkak kuttuviɭakkiṉ veɭiccattil aɻakeṉṉum teyvamee uruveʈuttatu poolak kaaɳappaʈʈaaɭ. The light burning brightly in the tall metal lamp spread a golden glow. The lamp must have contained some unique fragrant oil. The burning light spread a heady fragrance all around in that bower. A beauty was reclining comfortably on the soft pillows of a swinging couch spread with multicolored fragrant flowers. She was the Young-Queen of Pazluvoor. In the daylight inside the ivory palanquin she looked ravishing. Now in the night, in the glow of the golden lamp she looked like beauty personified.
malariṉ maɳamum viɭakkiṉ pukai maɳamum paɻuvuur iɭaiya raaɳiyiṉ mookaṉa uruvamum ceerntu vantiyatteevaṉaip pootai koɭɭac ceytaṉa. The fragrance of the flowers, the smell of the incense and the enchanting face of the Young-Queen intoxicated Vandiya Devan.
vantiyatteevaa! jaakkiratai! oree oru taʈavai nii matupaaṉam ceytaay! uṉ aṟivu kalaŋkuvatai aṟintaay! piṟaku matuvait toʈuvatillai eṉṟu capatam ceytaay! ippootu atai ɲaapakappaʈuttik koɭ! matuviṉ pootaiyaik kaaʈʈilum cakti vaaynta inta mayakkattil uṉ aṟivaip paṟi koʈuttuviʈaatee! Vandiya Deva! Be careful! You have drunk intoxicating liquor before. At that time you realized the confusion to your intellect and after that you took an oath to never touch liquor -- remember that now! Do not loose your intellect in this mesmerizing power more intoxicating than liquor! warned his mind.
vantiyatteevaṉaip paartta paɻuvuur iɭaiyaraaɳi nantiṉi, avaɭuʈaiya pavaɻa itaɻkaɭ ciṟitu virintu muttup paṟkaɭai veɭikkaaʈʈumpaʈi viyappuʈaṉ nookkik koɳʈiruntaaɭ. peeca muʈiyaata nilaiyai avaɭ accamayam aʈaintiruntatu vantiyatteevaṉukku aṉukuulamaakap pooyiṟṟu. Upon seeing Vallavarayan, the Young-Queen Nandini stared at him with surprise: slightly parted coral lips revealing tiny sparkling pearls. Her speechlessness helped Vandiya Devan.
ileecaaka avaṉ oru cirippuc cirittuviʈʈu, "ammaɳi! taŋkaɭ taatip peɳɳukkut tiʈiireṉṟu canteekam vantu viʈʈatu; - naaṉ mantiravaatiyaa illaiyaa eṉṟu! atai eppaʈik keeʈʈaaɭ eṉṟu niṉaikkiṟiirkaɭ? 'nii niitaaṉaa?' eṉṟu keeʈʈaaɭ!" eṉṟu maṟupaʈiyum cirittaaṉ. He laughed lightly and said, "My Lady, your maid had a sudden doubt! Whether I was the magician or not! Do you know how she expressed her doubt? `Are you, you?' she asked!" Vandiya Devan laughed again.
nantiṉi puṉṉakai purintaaɭ. vantiyatteevaṉuʈaiya kaɳ muṉṉaal oru miṉṉal miṉṉiyatu! atu teeṉaic corintatu. Nandini smiled. Lightning flashed in front of him. It showered honey and nectar!
"ivaɭukku appaʈittaaṉ eetaavatu canteekam tiʈiir tiʈiir eṉṟu vantuviʈum! vaacuki! eeṉ iŋkeeyee marampool niṟkiṟaay? uṉ iʈattukkup poo! yaaraavatu varum kaalaʈic cattam keeʈʈaal katavaip paʈiireṉṟu caattu!" eṉṟaaɭ nantiṉi. "She gets such sudden doubts quite often! Vasuki! Why are you standing here like a pole? Go to your place. If you hear any footsteps bang the door shut," said Nandini.
"itoo, ammaa!" eṉṟu colliviʈʈu, vaacuki lataa maɳʈapattiṉ uɭ vaɻiyaakap pirakaaca maaɭikaikkuc ceṉṟa naʈaipaataiyil naʈantu pooyc caṟṟut tuurattil maŋkalaakat terinta vaacaṟpaʈiyaɳʈai uʈkaarntu koɳʈaaɭ. "Yes my Lady!" said Vasuki as she ran out of a doorway towards the right of the Gazebo. She went down the covered walkway and sat down on some steps leading down to the lighted mansion.
nantiṉi ciṟitu kuralait taaɻttik koɳʈu, "uṉṉai mantiravaatiyillaiyeṉṟaa ivaɭ canteekittaaɭ? acaʈʈup peɳ! mantiravaatikaɭ eṉṟu collik koɭkiṟavarkaɭil mukkaalvaacip peer veṟum poyyarkaɭ. niitaaṉ uɳmai mantiravaati! eṉṉa maaya mantiram ceytu intac camayattil iŋkee vantaay?" eṉṟu keeʈʈaaɭ. Nandini lowered her voice as she spoke, "Did that foolish girl doubt you being a magician? Half the fellows who declare themselves as "sorcerers" and "magicians" are utter fools and liars. You are a true magician! What magic and sorcery did you employ to arrive here now?"
"ammaɳi! maayam mantiram ceytu naaṉ iŋku varavillai. cuvar miitu caattiyirunta eeɳi meel eeṟittaaṉ vanteeṉ!" eṉṟaaṉ vantiyatteevaṉ. "My Lady, I did not employ any magic trickery to come here. I climbed a ladder leaning against a wall to come here," declared Vandiya Devan.
"atutaaṉ terikiṟatee! intap peɳɳai eṉṉa maaya mantiram ceytu eemaaṟṟiṉaay eṉṟu keeʈʈeeṉ." "That is obvious. I asked about what magic you employed to fool my girl."
"nilaa veɭiccattil oru puṉṉakai purinteeṉ. avvaɭavu taaṉ! ataṟkuc carippaʈʈu varaaviʈʈaal taaŋkaɭ koʈutta mantira mootirattaik kaaʈʈa eɳɳiyirunteeṉ." "I smiled at her in the moonlight. That's all! If she had not been charmed, I was planning to use the signet ring you gave me."
"ataip pattiramaay vaittirukkiṟaay allavaa? anta mootiram irukkumpootu paʈʈap pakalil pakiraŋkamaaka iŋkee vantirukkalaamee? etaṟkaaka intak kuruʈʈu vaɻiyil tiruʈʈuttaṉamaaka vantaay?" "Oh! You have it safe -- don't you? You could have come here openly in broad daylight when you have that. Why this intrigue in darkness?"
"ammaɳi! taŋkaɭ maittuṉar irukkiṟaaree, ciṉṉap paɻuveeʈʈaraiyar, avaruʈaiya aaʈkaɭ cuttat tiruʈarkaɭ. mutalil eṉṉuʈaiya uʈaikaɭaiyum uʈaimaikaɭaiyum tiruʈap paarttaarkaɭ. piṟaku eṉṉaip piṉ toʈarntu orukaɳam kuuʈap piriyaamal vantu koɳʈiruntaarkaɭ. avarkaɭiʈamiruntu piriyap paʈʈapaaʈu perum paaʈaakap pooyiṟṟu. pirinta piṟaku cantu pontukaɭil pukuntu taŋkaɭuʈaiya maaɭikai matil cuvaraic cuṟṟi cuṟṟi vantu koɳʈirunteeṉ. antac camayattil cuvar meel vaitta eeɳiyaip paarttatum taaŋkaɭ taaṉ inta eeɻaiyai niṉaivu kuurntu inta eeṟpaaʈu ceytirukkiṟiirkaɭ eṉṟu eɳɳi viʈʈeeṉ. atu tavaṟu eṉṟu terintukoɳʈeeṉ. maṉṉikka veeɳʈum." "My Lady! Do you know your brother-in-law, the Younger Lord Pazluvoor? His men are complete knaves and scoundrels. First, they tried to steal my clothes and possessions. Then they followed my shadow without parting even for one second. It took great effort to part from them. After cutting loose from those fellows I entered the lanes and byways around your palace walls wandering aimlessly. At that time when I saw the ladder leaning against the garden wall I thought of you and your kindness in making this arrangement in consideration of this poor fellow. Now I realize my mistake. Please forgive me."
"maṉṉippataṟku avaciyam oṉṟum eeṟpaʈavillaiyee!" "There is nothing to forgive."
"atu eppaʈi ammaɳi!" "How is that My Lady?"
"nii niṉaittatu avvaɭavaakat tavaṟum illai. mantiravaatiyai etaṟkaaka naaṉ taruvikka niṉaitteeṉ. teriyumaa?" "Your expectations were not very wrong. Do you know why I called for the Sorcerer?"
"teriyavillai, ammaɳi! eṉakku mantiramum teriyaatu; joociyamum teriyaatu!" "I don't know, My Lady. I know no magic or sorcery; nor do I know any astrology."
"uṉṉai neeṟṟuk kaalaiyil paarttatu mutal uṉṉuʈaiya ɲaapakamaakavee iruntatu. nii eeṉ iṉṉum eṉṉaip paarkka varavillaiyeṉṟu terintu koɭɭa virumpiṉeeṉ. ataṟkaakavee taaṉ naaṉ mantiravaatiyaik kuuppiʈʈaṉuppiṉeeṉ." "I have been thinking of you since I met you yesterday morning. I wished to find out why you had not come back to see me. That's why I summoned the magician."
"mikka aaccariyamaayirukkiṟatu." "Very surprising."
"etu?" "Which?"
"ippootu niiŋkaɭ coṉṉatutaaṉ. neeṟṟu uŋkaɭaip paarttatu mutal eṉakku uŋkaɭ ɲaapakamaakaveeyiruntatu!" "What you said just now. Since meeting you yesterday, I too have been thinking about you."
"puurva jaṉma vaacaṉaiyil uṉakku nampikkai uɳʈaa?" "Do you believe in the bonds of previous incarnations?"
"appaʈiyeṉṟaal?" "Meaning ....?"
"puurva jaṉmattil iraɳʈu peerukku naʈpoo, uṟavoo iruntaal, inta jaṉmattilum attakaiya contam eeṟpaʈum eṉkiṟaarkaɭee, ataittaaṉ collukiṟeeṉ." "People say that if two persons had been friends or had some relationship in a previous birth, they would have a similar relationship in this birth also. That's what I am talking about."
"neeṟṟuvaraiyil eṉakku anta nampikkai illai. iṉṟu taaṉ atil nampikkai piṟantatu." "I had no such belief till recently. That belief was born in me yesterday!"
ivvitam vantiyatteevaṉ kuuṟiya pootu veɭippaʈaiyaakap poy coṉṉaaṉ eṉṟaalum, maṉattiṟkuɭ kuʈantai cootiʈar viiʈʈil paartta peɳɳai niṉaittuk koɳʈutaaṉ coṉṉaaṉ. aaṉaal nantiṉikku ataip paṟṟiya vivaramee teriya iʈamillaiyallavaa? taṉṉaip paṟṟic colvataakavee niṉaittuk koɳʈaaɭ. When Vandiya Devan uttered these words, outwardly he was lying. But, when he said those words his inner heart thought of the lady he had seen in the house of the Astrologer of Kudanthai. However, Nandini had no way of knowing that! She thought he was talking about her!
"aaṉaal ataṟkaaka nii eṉṉaip paarkka varavillaiyee? eetoo aaɻvaarkkaʈiyaar nampi eṉpavar ceyti colli aṉuppiyataaka..." "But, ... you have not come here to see me because of that! You talked about some message sent by Mr.Azlvar-adiyan Nambi ..."
"aam, ammaɳi! avar colli aṉuppiya ceytiyait taŋkaɭiʈam colvataṟkaakavee mutalil taŋkaɭaip paarkka virumpiṉeeṉ. taŋkaɭai orumuṟai paartta piṟaku, paɻaiya kaaraɳamellaam maṟantu pooyviʈʈatu." "Yes, My Lady. At first I wished to meet you mainly to deliver his message. After seeing you once, all those old reasons flew away."
"aaɻvaarkkaʈiyaarai nii eŋkee paarttaay? eṉṉa ceyti colliyaṉuppiṉaar?" "Where did you meet Mr. Azlvar-adiyan? What was his message?"
"viira naaraayaɳapurattukku arukil aaɻvaarkkaʈiyaar nampiyaic cantitteeṉ. avar tam kaittaʈiyiṉ caktiyaik koɳʈu viʂɳutaaṉ periya teyvam eṉṟu meyppikka muyaṉṟaar. accamayattil periya paɻuveeʈʈaraiyariṉ parivaaraŋkaɭ vantaṉa. avarait toʈarntu taŋkaɭ pallakkum vantatu. aŋkee eṉṉa rakaɭai eṉṟu paarppataṟkaakavoo eṉṉavoo, taŋkaɭuʈaiya oru poṟkaram pallakkiṉ tiraiyai vilakkiṟṟu. appootutaaṉ taaŋkaɭ eṉṟu terintukoɳʈu aaɻvaarkkaʈiyaar taŋkaɭukku oru ceyti aṉuppa virumpiṉaar. naaṉum aṉṟiravu kaʈampuur campuvaraiyar maaɭikaiyil taŋkiyapaʈiyaal eṉṉiʈam ceyti colli aṉuppiṉaar. aaṉaal kaʈampuuril taŋkaɭai naaṉ paarkka muʈiyavillai. taɲcaavuurk kooʈʈaikkarukil caalaiyiltaaṉ cantikka muʈintatu. atuvum taŋkaɭ pallakku eṉ kutiraimeel mootiyatiṉaaltaaṉ!" "I met Mr. Azlvar-adiyan Nambi near Veera-narayana-puram. He was using the powers of his wooden staff to prove that Vishnu was the greatest among Gods. At that time the retinue of the Elder Lord Pazluvoor came by in procession. Your palanquin followed him. Perhaps to see what the commotion was about, your golden hand parted the silk curtains. Recognizing you at that time Mr. Azlvar-adiyan Nambi wished to send a message to you. Since, I too was staying at Kadamboor that night, he made me his messenger. But I could not meet you in Kadamboor. I could only meet you on the road near Tanjore. Even that was possible only because your palanquin dashed against my horse!"
ivvitam vantiyatteevaṉ colli vantapootu nantiṉi meelee aɳɳaantu paarttuk koɳʈiruntaaɭ. aakaiyaal avaɭuʈaiya mukapaavattiliruntu oṉṟum kaɳʈu piʈikka muʈiyavillai. kaʈaiciyil vantiyatteevaṉ coṉṉataik keeʈʈatum, avaṉait tirumpip paarttu oru mookaṉap puṉṉakai purintaaɭ. Nandini was staring at the roof as she reclined on the couch, while Vandiya Devan talked. Therefore he could not surmise anything from her face or its expression. In the end, when she heard his words, she looked at him with a dazzling smile.
"aamaam; naaṉ eeṟum pallakku veku pollaata pallakku taaṉ!" eṉṟaaɭ. "Yes, my palanquin is a very mischievous palanquin!" she said.
