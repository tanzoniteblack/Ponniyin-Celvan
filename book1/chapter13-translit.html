patiṉmuuṉṟaam attiyaayam Chapter 13
vaɭarpiṟaic cantiraṉ The Waxing Moon
iɭavaracikaɭiṉ ratam kaɳɳukku maṟainta piṟaku, cootiʈar vantiyatteevaṉai viiʈʈukkuɭ aɻaittuc ceṉṟaar. tammuʈaiya aastaaṉa piiʈattil amarntaar. cuṟṟumuṟṟum paarttuk koɳʈirunta avvaalipaṉaiyum uʈkaarac coṉṉaar; avaṉai eeṟa iṟaŋkap paarttaar. The Astrologer led Vandiya Devan into the house after the chariot carrying the princesses had disappeared from view. He seated himself on his best seat. He asked the youth who was looking around to sit down and examined him from head to toe.
"tampi! nii yaar? eŋkee vantaay?" eṉṟu keeʈʈaar, vantiyatteevaṉ cirittaaṉ. "Thambi, who are you? Where have you come?" Vandiya Devan laughed.
"eṉṉappaa, cirikkiṟaay?" "Why are you laughing, my son?"
"illai, taaŋkaɭ ivvaɭavu pirapalamaaṉa jootiʈar eṉṉai keeɭvi keeʈkiṟiirkaɭee? naaṉ yaar, etaṟkaakat taŋkaɭiʈam vanteeṉ eṉṟu jootiʈattileeyee paarttuk koɭɭak kuuʈaataa?" "Nothing. You are such a famous astrologer. You ask me such questions! Can you not find out by your astrology about who I am and why I came here?"
"okoo! ataṟkeṉṉa? paarttuk koɭkiṟeeṉ. aaṉaal eṉakku naaṉee joociyam paarttuk koɳʈaal, taʈciɳai yaar koʈuppaarkaɭ eṉṟutaaṉ yoocikkiṟeeṉ." "Oh! Is that so? I can find out. But if I find out and predict things on my own behalf, I am wondering who will pay for my services."
vantiyatteevaṉ puṉṉakai ceytu viʈʈu, "jootiʈaree! ippootu iŋkee vantuviʈʈup pooṉaarkaɭee? avarkaɭ yaar?" eṉṟu keeʈʈaaṉ. Vandiya Devan smiled and then asked, "Sir! Those two who came here before me, who are they?"
"o! avarkaɭaa? nii yaaraip paṟṟik keeʈkiṟaay eṉṟu eṉakkut terikiṟatu. teriyum tampi, teriyum! nii eṉ ciiʈaṉaip piʈittu iɻuttuk koɳʈu uɭɭee nuɻaintapootu iŋkee iruntaarkaɭee, avarkaɭaip paṟṟittaaṉ keeʈkiṟaay, illaiyaa? ratattil eeṟik koɳʈu, piṉṉaal puɻutiyaik kiɭappi viʈʈuk koɳʈu pooṉaarkaɭee, avarkaɭaip paṟṟittaaṉee?" eṉṟu kuʈantai cootiʈar cuṟṟi vaɭaittuk keeʈʈaar. "Ah! Them? I realize whom you are asking about. I know Thambi, I know. Are you not asking about the persons who were in here when you dragged my disciple and made a sudden entry into my house? You are asking about them aren't you? Those two, who left in that swift chariot raising the road dust behind them, you are asking about them?" said the Astrologer in a rambling fashion.
"aamaam, aamaam! avarkaɭaip paṟṟit taaṉ keeʈʈeeṉ..." "Yes. Yes. I am asking about them..."
"naṉṟaakak keeɭ. keeʈka veeɳʈaam eṉṟu yaar coṉṉatu? avarkaɭ iraɳʈu peerum iraɳʈu peɳmaɳikaɭ!" "OK. Ask. Ask as much as you wish. Who said not to ask? Those two persons were two women!"
"atu eṉakkee terintu pooyviʈʈatu; jootiʈaree! naaṉ kuruʈaṉ illai. aaɳkaɭaiyum peɳkaɭaiyum naaṉ vittiyaacam kaɳʈu piʈittu viʈuveeṉa . peɳ veeʈam puuɳʈa aaɳaayiruntaal kuuʈa eṉakkut terintu pooyviʈum." "That was obvious even to me! Sir, I am not blind. I can differentiate between men and women. Even if it is a man masquerading as a woman I can find out."
"piṉṉee eṉṉa keeʈkiṟaay?.." "Then, why did you ask ..."
"peɳkaɭ eṉṟaal, avarkaɭ iṉṉaar, iṉṉa jaati.." "Women means, who are they, what kind? ..."
"okoo! ataiyaa keeʈkiṟaay? peɳkaɭil patmiṉi, cittiṉi, kaantarvi, vityaatari eṉpataaka naalu jaatikaɭ uɳʈu. uṉakkuc caamuttirikaa laʈcaɳa caastirattil koɲcam payiṟci irukkum poolirukkiṟatu. anta naalu jaatikaɭil ivarkaɭ patmiṉi, kaantarvi jaatikaɭaic ceerntavarkaɭ." "Oh ho! You are asking about that! According to the science of beauty, women are classified into four kinds: padmini, chittini, gandarvi, vidyadari. You seem to have some expertise in the study of beauty! According to that fourfold classification those two women belong to the padmini or gandarvi class."
"kaʈavuɭee!..." "Oh God!"
"eeṉ? appaṉee!" "What, my son?"
"kaʈavuɭai naaṉ kuuppiʈʈaal, niiŋkaɭ 'eeṉ?' eṉṟu keeʈkiṟiirkaɭee?" "If I call out to God, why do you answer?"
"atil eṉṉa picaku? kaʈavuɭ carvaantaryaami eṉṟu nii keeʈʈatillaiyaa? periyavarkaɭuʈaiya cakavaacam uṉakku avvaɭavaakak kiʈaiyaatu poolirukkiṟatu! eṉakkuɭɭee iruppavarum kaʈavuɭ taaṉ; uṉakkuɭɭee iruppavarum kaʈavuɭ taaṉ. nii iɻuttuk koɳʈu uɭɭee vantaayee anta eṉ ciiʈaṉukkuɭɭee iruppavarum kaʈavuɭtaaṉ..." "What is wrong in that? Haven't you heard that God is everywhere? It seems that you have not kept company with learned people. God is in me. God is in you too. My disciple, whom you dragged inside, God is in him too..."
"pootum, pootum, niṟuttuŋkaɭ." "Enough. Enough. Please stop!"
"ittaṉai neeram peecac coṉṉatum kaʈavuɭtaaṉ; ippootu niṟuttac colvatum kaʈavuɭtaaṉ!" "God asked me to talk all this time; and now God commands me to stop!"
"jootiʈaree! ippootu iŋkeeyiruntu pooṉaarkaɭee, antap peɳkaɭ yaar, enta uur, eṉṉa kulam, eṉṉa peyar, eṉṟu keeʈʈeeṉ. cuṟṟi vaɭaikkaamal maṟumoɻi coṉṉaal.." "Sir! Mr. Astrologer, those two women who left just now -- who are they? What is their family? Where do they come from? What is their name? -- that is what I asked. If you can answer me without rambling like this ..."
"coṉṉaal eṉakku nii eṉṉa taruvaay appaṉee!" "If I answer you, what will you give me, my son?"
"eṉ vantaṉattait taruveeṉ." "I shall offer you my thanks gladly."
"uṉ vantaṉattai niiyee vaittukkoɭ. eetaavatu poṉtaaṉam koʈuppataayiruntaal collu!" "You can keep the glad offering to yourself. If you are willing to give some gold offering, let me know."
"poṉtaaṉam koʈuttaal niccayamaayc colluviirkaɭaa?" "Will you answer clearly, if I make a gold offering?"
"atuvum collakkuuʈiyataayiruntaaltaaṉ colluveeṉ! tampi! itaik keeɭ. jootiʈaṉ viiʈʈukkup palarum vantu poovaarkaɭ. oruvaraip paṟṟi iṉṉoruvariʈam collak kuuʈaatu. ippootu pooṉavarkaɭaip paṟṟi uṉṉiʈam colla maaʈʈeeṉ. uṉṉaip paṟṟi va eeṟu yaaraavatu keeʈʈaal avarkaɭukkum uṉṉaip paṟṟi oru vaarttaikuuʈac colla maaʈʈeeṉ." "I will answer only if an answer is possible. Thambi, listen to this. Several persons will come and go from an astrologer's house. One should not talk about one visitor to another. I will not tell you anything about the persons who left just now. Neither will I disclose a single word about you, to any person who may come later and ask about you."
"aakaa! aaɻvaarkkaʈiyaaṉnampi taŋkaɭaip paṟṟic coṉṉatu muṟṟum uɳmaitaaṉ." "Ah! Everything that Azlvar-adiyan Nambi said about you seems to be true!"
"aaɻvaarkkaʈiyaaraa? avar yaar, appaʈi oruvar?" "Azlvar-adiyan? Who is he? A person with that name?"
"taŋkaɭukkut teriyaataa, eṉṉa? rompavum taŋkaɭait terintavarpool peeciṉaaree? aaɻvaarkkaʈiyaaṉ nampi eṉṟu keeʈʈateeyillaiyaa?" "What, don't you know him? He talked as if he knew you very well. Have you never heard of Azlvar-adiyan Nambi?"
"oruveeɭai aaɭait terintirukkum; peyar ɲaapakam iraatu koɲcam aʈaiyaaɭam collu, paarkkalaam!" "Perhaps I know the man. Don't remember the name. Describe him a little, let me see."
"kaʈʈaiyaayum kuʈʈaiyaayum iruppaar, muṉ kuʈumi vaittiruppaar. iɭantontiyil veeʈʈiyai iṟukkik kaʈʈiyiruppaar. cantaṉattaik kuɻaittu uʈampellaam kiiɻiruntu meelaaka iʈʈiruppaar. caivarkaɭaik kaɳʈaal caɳʈaikkup poovaar. atvaitikaɭaik kaɳʈaal taʈiyait tuukkuvaar. caṟṟumuṉṉaal 'niiyum kaʈavuɭ, naaṉum kaʈavuɭ' eṉṟiirkaɭee, itai aaɻvaarkkaʈiyaaṉ keeʈʈiruntaal 'kaʈavuɭaik kaʈavuɭ taakkukiṟatu!' eṉṟu collit taʈiyiṉaal aʈikka varuvaar..." "He is short and squat. He wears his hair in a topknot on his forehead. He wears his vaetti (loose lower garment of men) tightly around his young paunch. Making a paste of sandal powder, he wears it in vertical marks all over his body; readily enters into fiery fights with devout Saivas; if he sees an Advaita philosopher, he raises his wooden staff. Sometime ago you said `You are God; I am God.' If Azlvar-adiyan had heard it, he would have raised his staff and rushed over to beat you, by saying `God is hitting God.' He ..."
"tampi! nii colluvataiyellaam ceerttup paarttaal tirumalaiyappaṉaip paṟṟic collukiṟaay poolirukkiṟatu.." "Thambi, From what you say, I think you are talking about Thirumalai."
"avarukku appaʈi vevveeṟu peyarkaɭ uɳʈaa?" "Does he go by other names like that?"
"uurukku oru peyar vaittuk koɭvaar anta viira vaiʂɳavar." "That fanatic Vaishnava has a different name in different towns."
"aaɭukkut takunta veeʂamum pooʈuvaaraakkum!" "Does he also disguise himself in different garbs for different persons?"
"aakaa! camayattukkut takunta veeʂamum pooʈuvaar." "Oh yes! He will put on masquerades appropriate for the occasion and time."
"colluvatil koɲcam kaṟpaṉaiyum poyyum kalantirukkumoo?" "Will everything he utters be mixed with fancy and falsehood?"
"mukkaalee muuṉṟarai viicam poyyum kaṟpaṉaiyum irukkum; arai viicam uɳmaiyum irukkalaam." "About seven eighths of what he says will be full of imagination. The rest may be gospel truth."
"rompap pollaata maṉitar eṉṟu colluŋkaɭ!" "So, you say that he is a very wicked fellow."
"appaʈiyum colliviʈa muʈiyaatu. nallavarkku nallavar; pollaatavarkkup pollaatavar." "One cannot say that! He is good to the good; bad to the wicked."
"avaruʈaiya peeccai nampi oṉṟum ceyya ma ʈiyaatu." "That means we cannot trust his words and do anything."
"nampuvatum nampaatatum antantap peeccaip poṟuttirukkiṟatu..." "Believing or not believing depends on the words uttered."
"utaaraɳamaaka, taŋkaɭiʈam pooyc cootiʈam keeʈʈaal nallapaʈi colluviirkaɭ eṉṟu avar kuuṟiyatu..." "For example, he said that if I come to you and asked you for astrological predictions, you will give expert answers..."
"avar peeccil arai viicam uɳmaiyum irukkum eṉṟeeṉee, anta arai viicattil atu ceerntatu." "Did I not say that about one eighth of his speech will be gospel truth? That statement belongs to that category!"
"appaʈiyaaṉaal eṉakku eetaavatu jootiʈam, aaruuʈam colluŋkaɭ; neeramaakiviʈʈatu eṉakkup pookaveeɳʈum, aiyaa!" "Then predict something for me by your astrology; foretell something. It is getting late and I have to go in a hurry."
"appaʈi avacaramaaka eŋkee pooka veeɳʈum, appaṉee!" "Where do you have to go in such a great hurry, my son?"
"ataiyum taaŋkaɭ jootiʈattil paarttuc collak kuuʈaataa? eŋkee pookaveeɳʈum, eŋkee pookak kuuʈaatu, pooṉaal kaariyam cittiyaakumaa eṉpataip paṟṟiyellaantaaṉ taŋkaɭaik keeʈka vanteeṉ." "Can you not reveal that also from your astrology? Where should I go? Where should I not go? If I do go, will my endeavor be successful? -- I came to ask you all these things."
"jootiʈam, aaruuʈam colvataṟkum eetaavatu aataaram veeɳʈum, appaṉee! jaatakam veeɳʈum; jaatakam illaaviʈil, piṟantanaaɭ, naʈcattiramaavatu teriya veeɳʈum; atuvum teriyaaviʈil, uurum peerumaavatu colla veeɳʈum". "I need some basis to foretell or predict by my astrology; I need a horoscope to study; if there is no horoscope, I must at least know the day and star under which you were born. If you do not know even that, I need a name and address."
"eṉ peyar vantiyatteevaṉ!" "My name is Vandiya Devan."
"aakaa! vaaɳar kulattavaṉaa?" "Aha! Of the Vaanar clan?"
"aamaam." "Yes."
"vallavaraiyaṉ vantiyatteevaṉaa?" "Vandiya Devan Vallavarayan?"
"caaʈcaat avaṉeetaaṉ." "I am that very same."
"appaʈic collu, tampi! muṉṉamee colliyirukkak kuuʈaataa? uṉ jaatakam kuuʈa eṉṉiʈam iruntatee! teeʈip paarttaal kiʈaikkum." "Say it like that! Thambi why did you not say this before? I think, I even have your horoscope with me. If I search for it I can find it."
"ohoo! atu eppaʈi?" "How is that Sir?"
"eṉṉaip pooṉṟa jootiʈarkaɭukku veeṟu eṉṉa veelai. periya vamcattil piṟanta piɭɭaikaɭ - peɳkaɭ ivarkaɭuʈaiya jaatakaŋkaɭaiyellaam ceerttu vaittuk koɭvoom". "What other occupation do astrologer's like me have? We collect the horoscopes of men and women born in noble families..."
"naaṉ appaʈiyoṉṟum periya vamcattil piṟantavaṉ allavee..." "I am not from any such famous noble family."
"naṉṟaakac coṉṉaay! uṉṉuʈaiya kulam eppeerppaʈʈa kulam! vaaɳar kulattaip paṟṟik kavivaaɳarkaɭ evvaɭavu kavikaɭaiyellaam paaʈiyirukkiṟaarkaɭ! oruveeɭai nii keeʈʈirukka maaʈʈaay." "How can you say that? Do you not know of the fame and prestige of your clan? How many poets have sung the praises of the Vaanar clan in how many poems? Perhaps you have not heard the poems?"
"oru kavitaiyaittaaṉ colluŋkaɭeeṉ, keeʈkalaam." "Recite one such poem. Let me hear it."
jootiʈar uʈaṉee pa ṉvarum paaʈalaic coṉṉaar: The Astrologer immediately sang the following poem: 
	"vaaɳaṉ pukaɻuraiyaa vaayuɳʈoo maakatarkooṉ
	vaaɳaṉ peyareɻutaa maarpuɳʈoo - vaaɳaṉ
	koʈitaaŋki nillaata kompuɳʈoo uɳʈoo
	aʈitaaŋki nillaa aracu!"

Are there lips that do not speak of your fame: oh noble Vaana?
Are there chests that do not have your name tattooed on: oh brave Vaana?
Are there flagpoles that do not bear thy colors: oh victorious Vaana?
Are there domains that stand beyond your survey: oh majestic Vaana?
jootiʈar icaippulavar allaveṉpatu avar paaʈumpootu vauiyaayiṟṟu. aayiṉum paaʈalaip paɳɳil amaittu mika viɭakkamaakavum urukkamaakavum paaʈiṉaar. When he sang, it was quite obvious that the Astrologer was not a musical expert. Nevertheless, he sang the poem clearly and with involvement.
"kavi eppaʈiyirukkiṟatu?" eṉṟu keeʈʈaar. "How is the poem?" he asked.
"kavi kaatukku naṉṟaakattaaṉ irukkiṟatu. aaṉaal eṉṉuʈaiya koʈiyai eetaavatu oru maaʈʈiṉ kompil naaṉee kaʈʈi viʈʈaaltaaṉ uɳʈu. aracamarattuk kiɭai meel eeṟi niṉṟaaltaaṉ aracu eṉ aʈiyait taaŋkum; atukuuʈac canteekamtaaṉ. kaṉam taaŋkaamal kiɭai muṟintu eṉṉaiyum kiiɻee taɭɭiṉaalum taɭɭum!" eṉṟaaṉ vantiyatteevaṉ. "The poem is pleasing to the ear! But now, I have to tie my colors to the horns of some bull! I have to stand atop the branches of a banyan tree to survey my domain. Even that is doubtful. My weight might break the branch and throw me down!" said Vandiya Devan.
"iṉṟaikku uṉ nilaimai ippaʈi; naaɭaikku eppaʈiyirukkum eṉṟu yaar kaɳʈatu?" eṉṟaar jootiʈar. "Today your situation is like that. How do we know what can happen tomorrow?"
"taaŋkaɭ kaɳʈiruppiirkaɭ eṉṟu eɳɳiyallavaa vanteeṉ?" eṉṟaaṉ vallavaraiyaṉ. "I thought that you might know; that is why I came here."
"naaṉ eṉṉattaik kaɳʈeeṉ, tampi! ellaaraiyum pool naaṉum aṟpa aayuɭ paʈaitta maṉitaṉtaaṉee? aaṉaal kirakaŋkaɭum naʈcattiraŋkaɭum varuŋkaala nikaɻccikaɭaic collukiṉṟaṉa. avai colluvatai naaṉ ciṟitu kaɳʈaṟintu keeʈpavarkaɭukkuc colkiṟeeṉ, avvaɭavutaaṉ!" "What can I know Thambi? Like everybody I am a short lived human being. But the planets and stars foretell the future. I have learned to understand a little of what they say and then explain it to those who seek me."
"kirahaŋkaɭum naʈcattiraŋkaɭum eṉ viʂayattil eṉṉa colkiṉṟaṉa jootiʈaree?" "What do the planets and stars foretell about me, Mr. Astrologer?"
"nii naaɭukku naaɭ uyarvaay eṉṟu collukiṉṟaṉa." "They foretell that you will grow day by day."
"cariyaakap pooccu! ippootuɭɭa uyaramee atikamaayirukkiṟatu. uŋkaɭ viiʈʈil nuɻaiyumpootu kuṉiya veeɳʈiyirukkiṟatu! iṉṉum uyarntu eṉṉa ceyvatu? ippaʈiyellaam potuvaakac collaamal kuṟippaaka eetaavatu colluŋkaɭ." "Dear me! That is pretty good! The height that I have now is itself quite too much. I had to bend low as I entered your house! What can I do if I grow taller? There is no use of such generalities. Say something specific."
"nii eetaavatu kuṟippaakak keeʈʈaal, naaṉum kuṟippaakac colluveeṉ." "If you ask me about something particular, I can answer more specifically."
"naaṉ taɲcaavuurukka p pookiṟa kaariyam kaikuuʈumaa? colluŋkaɭ." "Tell me, the enterprise on which I am going to Tanjore, will it succeed?"
"nii taɲcaavuurukku uṉ contak kaariyamaakap pookiṟataaṉaal pookiṟa kaariyam kaikuuʈum. ippootu uṉakku jayakkirakaŋkaɭ uccamaayirukkiṉṟaṉa. piṟaruʈaiya kaariyamaakap poovataayiruntaal, anta maṉitarkaɭuʈaiya jaatakattaip paarttuc colla veeɳʈum!" "If you are going to Tanjore on your own behalf the endeavor will succeed; if you are going on someone else's behalf, I have to see that persons horoscope before I answer."
vantiyatteevaṉ talaiyai aaʈʈik koɳʈu muukkiṉ meel viralai vaittu, "jootiʈaree! taŋkaɭaip pooṉṟa caamarttiyacaaliyai naaṉ paarttateeyillai!" eṉṟaaṉ. Vandiya Devan shook his head in astonishment and placed a finger upon his nose. "Sir, I have never encountered as astute a fellow as you."
"mukastuti ceyyaatee, tampi!" eṉṟaar jootiʈar. "Do not flatter me, Thambi."
"irukkaʈʈum. keeʈka veeɳʈiyatait tauivaakavee keeʈʈu viʈukiṟeeṉ. taɲcaavuuril cakkaravarttiyait taricikka virumpukiṟeeṉ, atu caattiyamaakumaa?" "Ok. I shall ask what I want clearly. I wish to meet the Emperor in Tanjore. Is that possible?"
"eṉṉaiviʈap periya jootiʈarkaɭ iruvar taɲcaavuuril irukkiṟaarkaɭ avarkaɭaitaaṉ keeʈkaveeɳʈum." "There are two astrologers greater than me in Tanjore. You have to ask them."
"avarkaɭ yaar?" "Who are they?"
"periya paɻuveeʈʈaraiyar oruvar; ciṉṉa paɻuveeʈʈaraiyar oruvar." "One is the Elder Lord Pazluvoor; the other is his brother the Younger Lord Pazluvoor."
"cakkaravarttiyiṉ uʈalnilai mika moocamaakiyiruppataakac colkiṟaarkaɭee? atu uɳmaiyaa?" "It is said that the health of the Emperor is in a bad state. Is that true?"
"yaaraavatu eetaavatu colluvaarkaɭ! colluvataṟku eṉṉa? ataiyellaam nampaatee! vauiyilum collaatee!" "People will say all sorts of things. Why not? Don't believe all that they say; neither should you repeat such things."
"cakkaravarttikku eetaavatu neerntuviʈʈaal, aʈutta paʈʈam yaarukku eṉṟu colla muʈiyumaa?" "Can you tell me who has the right to succeed to the Chozla throne if something happens to the Emperor?"
"aʈutta paʈʈam uṉakkumillai; eṉakkumillai; naam eeṉ ataippaṟṟik kavalaippaʈa veeɳʈum?" "That throne is not for you nor for me. Why should we bother about it?"
"anta maʈʈil tappip piɻaittoom!" eṉṟaaṉ vantiyatteevaṉ. "From that fate we are saved!" said Vandiya Devan.
"uɳmaitaaṉ, tampi! paʈʈattukkup paattiyatai eṉpatu caataaraɳa viʂayam alla; mikka apaayakaramaaṉa viʂayam illaiyaa!" "It is true Thambi. The rights of succession to an empire is not an ordinary matter. It is a very dangerous topic. Is that not so?"
"joociyaree! taṟcamayam kaaɲciyil irukkiṟaaree, iɭavaracar aatitta karikaalar." "Sir! At present the Prince who is in Kanchi, the Crown Prince Aditya Karikala..."
"irukkiṟaar. avaruʈaiya caarpaakattaaṉee nii vantirukkiṟaay!" "Yes he is there; you have come on his behalf. Haven't you?"
"kaʈaiciyaakak kaɳʈu piʈittu viʈʈiirkaɭ; cantooʂam avaruʈaiya yookam eppaʈi ira kkiṟatu." "You have found that out in the end; no harm done. What about his fortune?"
"jaatakam kaivacam illai, tampi! paarttuttaaṉ colla veeɳʈum." "I don't have his horoscope in my hands now Thambi. I have to study it."
"iɭavaracar maturaantakariṉ yookam eppaʈi?" "What about the fortunes of Prince Madurandaka?"
"avaruʈaiyatu vicittiramaaṉa jaatakam. peɳkaɭiṉ jaatakattai ottatu. eppootum piṟaruʈaiya aatikkattukku uʈpaʈʈiruppatu..." "His, is a peculiar fortune; similar to that of womenfolk. His fortunes are always subject to the power of others."
"ippootukuuʈac cooɻa naaʈʈil peɳɳaracu naʈaipeṟuvataakac colkiṟaarkaɭee? alli raajyattaiviʈa moocam eṉkiṟaarkaɭee?" "Even now people say that the Chozla Empire is under Women's Rule. They say that it is worse than the rule of the Queen Alli?"
"eŋkee tampi appaʈic collukiṟaarkaɭ?" "Thambi, where do they say such things?"
"koɭɭiʈattukku vaʈakkee collukiṟaarkaɭ?" "To the north of the Kollidam."
"periya paɻuveeʈʈaraiyar putiyataaka maɳam purintu koɳʈa iɭaiya raaɳiyiṉ aatikkattaip paṟṟic colkiṟaarkaɭ poolirukkiṟatu." "Maybe they are talking about the power of the new bride married by the Elder Lord Pazluvoor."
"naaṉ keeɭvippaʈʈatu veeṟu." "I heard differently."
"eṉṉa keeɭvippaʈʈaay?" "What did you hear?"
"cakkaravarttiyiṉ tirukkumaari kuntavaip piraaʈʈitaaṉ avvitam peɳɳaracu celuttuvataakac colkiṟaarkaɭ!" "They said that the beloved daughter of the Emperor, the Younger Pirati Kundavai, is the one ruling like that."
jootiʈar caṟṟee vantiyatteevaṉ mukattai uṟṟup paarttaar. caṟṟumuṉ anta viiʈʈiliruntu ceṉṟatu kuntavai teevi eṉṟu terintu koɳʈutaaṉ avvitam keeʈkiṟaaṉoo eṉṟu mukattiliruntu aṟiya muyaṉṟaar. aaṉaal ataṟku aṟikuṟi oṉṟum teriyavillai. The Astrologer peered into Vandiya Devan's face carefully. He tried to read the face to find out if he was saying these words after recognizing Kundavai, who had left his house a little earlier. There was no sign to support the doubt.
"cuttat tavaṟu, tampi! cuntara cooɻa cakkaravartti taɲcaiyil irukkiṟaar, kuntavaippiraaʈʈi paɻaiyaaṟaiyil irukkiṟaar meelum..." "Totally wrong Thambi. Emperor Sundara Chozla is in Tanjore. Kundavai Pirati lives in Pazlayarai. Moreover ..."
"meelum eṉṉa? eeṉ niṟutti viʈʈiirkaɭ?" "Moreover what? Why did you stop?
"pakalil pakkam paarttup peeca veeɳʈum; iravil atuvum peecak kuuʈaatu. aaṉaalum uṉṉiʈam coṉṉaal paatakamillai. ippootu cakkaravarttikku atikaaram eetu? ellaa atikaaraŋkaɭaiyum paɻuveeʈʈaraiyarkaɭ allavaa celuttukiṟaarkaɭ!" "During the daytime one must look in all four directions before speaking; at nighttime not even that. But, there is nothing wrong in telling you, I think. What power or influence does the Emperor have these days? All the authority is held by the Lords of Pazluvoor."
ippaʈi colliviʈʈuc jootiʈar vantiyatteevaṉuʈaiya mukattai maṟupaʈiyum oru taʈavai kavaṉamaakap paarttaar. After saying this the Astrologer examined Vandiya Devan's face once again.
"jootiʈaree! naaṉ paɻuveeʈʈaraiyariṉ oṟṟaṉ alla; appaʈic cana teekappaʈa veeɳʈaam. caṟṟu muṉṉaal raajyaŋkaɭum raajavamcaŋkaɭum nilaittu nillaamai paṟṟic coṉṉiirkaɭ. naaṉ piṟanta vaaɳar kulattaiyee utaaraɳamaakac coṉṉiirkaɭ. tayavu ceytu uɳmaiyaic colluŋkaɭ; cooɻa vamcattiṉ varuŋkaalam eppaʈiyirukkum?" "Sir! I am not a spy of the Lords of Pazluvoor. You do not have to suspect me of that! A little while ago you talked about the impermanence of kingdoms and royal dynasties. You gave me the example of my own Vaanar clan. Please tell me the truth. How is the future of the Chozla Dynasty?"
"uɳmaiyaic colkiṟeeṉ; canteekam ciṟitumiṉṟic colkiṟeeṉ. aaṉi maatak kaʈaiciyil kaaveeriyilum kaaveeriyiṉ kiɭai natikaɭilum putuveɭɭam varum. appootu atu naaɭukku naaɭ perukap pookum putu veɭɭam eṉpatu kaaveeri tiirattil uɭɭavarkaɭukku naṉṟaayt teriyum. aavaɳi, puraʈʈaaci varaiyilum veɭɭam perukik koɳʈutaaṉirukkum. kaarttikai, maarkaɻiyil veɭɭam vaʈiya aarampikkum. itu vaʈikiṟa veɭɭam eṉpatum kaaveerik karaiyil uɭɭavarkaɭukkut terintu pookum. cooɻa caamraajyam ippootu naaɭukku naaɭ perukum putuveɭɭattai ottirukkiṟatu. iṉṉum pala nuuṟu varuʂam itu perukip paravik koɳʈeeyirukkum. cooɻap peeraracu ippootu vaɭarpiṟaic cantiraṉaaka iruntu varukiṟatu. paurɳamikku iṉṉum pala naaɭ irukkiṟatu. aakaiyaal meelum meelum cooɻa makaaraajyam vaɭarntu koɳʈeeyirukkum.." "I can tell truthfully without any uncertainty. Towards the end of spring the River Cauvery and its tributaries will be filled with new floods. The people living on the river banks know very well that it is a flood that will rise day by day. Till the beginning of the summer months the flood will keep rising. By the autumn months the waters start receding. The people living along the bank realize it to be a ebbing flood. The Chozla Empire is now like the fresh flood of springtime that grows day by day. For several more hundreds of years it will grow and spread. The Chozla Empire is now a Waxing Moon. The day of the Full Moon is far away. Therefore the Chozla power will expand more and more."
"ittaṉai neeram taŋkaɭuʈaṉee peeciyataṟku inta oru viʂayam tauivaakac colli viʈʈiirkaɭ. vantaṉam, iṉṉum oru viʂayam maʈʈum muʈiyumaaṉaal colluŋkaɭ. eṉakku kappal eeṟik kaʈaṟ pirayaaɳam ceyya veeɳʈum eṉṟa viruppam rompa naaɭaaka irukkiṟatu..." "After talking all this time, you have said one thing explicitly! Thanks! If possible, just tell me one more thing. I have a great desire to go on a ship and travel to distant lands..."
"anta viruppam niccayamaakak kaikuuʈum; nii cakaʈayookakkaaraṉ. uṉ kaalil cakkaram iruppatu poolavee oyaamal cuṟṟik koɳʈiruppaay. naʈantu poovaay; kutirai eeṟip poovaay; yaaṉai meel poovaay; kappal eeṟiyum poovaay; ciikkiramaakavee uṉakkuk kaʈaṟ pirayaaɳam ceyyum yookam irukkiṟatu." "That wish will definitely be fulfilled. You have the fortune of the wheel. You will be travelling constantly, as if you have wheels on your feet. You will go by walk; ride upon horses; go on elephants and you will also travel aboard ships. You have the fortune to go on a sea voyage very soon."
"aiyaa! teṉticaip paʈaiyiṉ ceeṉaapati, taṟcamayam iiɻattilee yuttam naʈattum iɭavaracar aruɭmoɻivarmaraip paṟṟit taaŋkaɭ collak kuuʈumaa? kirahaŋkaɭum naʈcattiraŋkaɭum avaraip paṟṟi eṉṉa collukiṉṟaṉa?" "Sir, about the Commander of the Southern Armies, Prince Arulmozli Varma who is now conducting a campaign in Lanka -- what do the planets and stars say about him?"
"tampi! kappalil pirayaaɳam ceyvoor ticaiyaṟivataṟku oru kaantak karuviyai upayookikkiṟaarkaɭ. kalaŋkaraiviɭakkaŋkaɭum upayookappaʈukiṉṟaṉa. aaṉaal ivaṟṟaiyellaamviʈa, naʈukkaʈalil kappal viʈum maalumikaɭukku uṟutuɳaiyaayiruppatu etu teriyumaa? vaʈaticaiyil aʈivaaṉattil uɭɭa turuva naʈcattirantaaṉ. maṟṟa naʈcattiraŋkaɭ - kirahaŋkaɭ ellaam iʈampeyarntu pooyk koɳʈeeyirukkum. saptariʂi maɳʈalamum ticaimaaṟip pirayaaɳam ceyyum. aaṉaal turuva naʈcattiram maʈʈum iʈattaiviʈʈu acaiyaamal irunta iʈattileeyee irukkum. antat turuva naʈcattirattaip pooṉṟavar cuntara cooɻa cakkaravarttiyiṉ kaʈaikkuʈʈip putalvaraaṉa iɭavaracar aruɭmoɻivarmar. etaṟkum nilaikalaŋkaata tiʈa cittamuʈaiyavar. tiyaakam, oɻukkam mutaliya kuɳaŋkaɭil poolavee viirapuruʂattilum ciṟantavar. kalviyaṟivaip poolavee ulaka aṟivum paʈaittavar. paarttaalee paci tiirum eṉṟu collak kuuʈiya paalvaʈiyum kaɭaimukam paʈaittavar; atirʂʈa teevataiyiṉ celvap putalvar. maalumikaɭ turuva naʈcattirattaik kuṟikoɭvatu pool, vaaɻkkaik kaʈalil iṟaŋkum uṉ pooṉṟa vaaliparkaɭ aruɭmoɻivarmaraik kuṟiyaaka vaittukkoɭvatu mikka palaṉ aɭikkum." "Thambi, those travelling by ship use an instrument made of the magnetic stone to find their directions. Lighthouses also help these sailors. But do you know what aids the ship in the midst of the wide open seas? What is the constant friend of the sailors? It is the pole star seen in the low northern skies. The other stars and planets all keep moving from their positions. Even the group of the `seven seers' (little dipper) travels in a circle. But the pole star is never shaken from its fixed position. The younger son of the Emperor, Prince Arulmozli, is like that pole star. He has the firmness of mind unshaken by anything. In addition to the virtues of selflessness and morality, he possess the goodness of bravery and chivalry. He excels in worldly knowledge as much as in learning. He has a charming face, like that of a young babe which calms the aches of those who gaze upon it. He is the darling son of the angel of good luck. Just as sailors take direction from the pole star, it will be very useful, if youths like you who embark on the adventure of life, take direction from Prince Arulmozli."
"appappaa! iɭavaracar aruɭmoɻivarmaraip paṟṟi evvaɭavellaam colkiṟiirkaɭ? kaatalaṉaik kaatali varɳippatu pool allavaa varɳikkiṟiirkaɭ?" "Dear me! What is all this that you describe about Prince Arulmozli? It appears like a lover describing his beloved?"
"tampi! kaaviri tiirattiluɭɭa cooɻa naaʈʈil yaaraik keeʈʈaalum eṉṉaip poolattaaṉ colvaaṉ." "Thambi, if you ask any fellow of the Chozla country on the banks of the River Ponni, he will say the same."
"mikka vantaṉam jootiʈaree! camayam na eerntaal uŋkaɭ puttimatiyiṉpaʈiyee naʈappeeṉ." "Many thanks Mr. Astrologer. If the need arises I shall follow your advice."
"uṉṉuʈaiya atirʂʈak kirakamum uccattukku vantirukkiṟatu eṉṟu aṟintu taaṉ coṉṉeeṉ." "Because I saw that your lucky planets are also on the rise, I said this."
"pooy varukiṟeeṉ jootiʈaree. eṉ maṉamaarnta vantaṉattuʈaṉ eṉṉaal iyaṉṟa poṉ taṉamum koɲcam camarppikkiṟeeṉ; tayavu ceytu peṟṟuk koɭɭa veeɳum." "I shall take leave of you Sir! Along with my heartfelt thanks, offered gladly, please accept this small tribute of gold offered to you."
ivvitam kuuṟi, aintu kaɻaɲcu poṉ naaɳayaŋkaɭai vantiyatteevaṉ camarppittaaṉ. Saying this, Vandiya Devan placed five gold coins in the palms of the Astrologer.
"vaaɳar kulattiṉ koʈaittaṉmai iṉṉamum pookavillai!" eṉṟu collik koɳʈu jootiʈar poṉṉai eʈuttu koɳʈaar. "The benevolence of the Vaanar clan has not died even now!" said the Astrologer as he hid the coins in his waistband.
