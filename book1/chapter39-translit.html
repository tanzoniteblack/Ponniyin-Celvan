39. ulakam cuɻaṉṟatu! Chapter 39 --The World Swirled
mutiya piraayattil taam kaliyaaɳam ceytu koɳʈatu paṟṟip palar palavitamaakap peecik koɭkiṟaarkaɭ eṉpataip paɻuveeʈʈaraiyar aṟintirukkiṟaar. appaʈi nintaṉaiyaakap peeciyavarkaɭil kuntavaip piraaʈʈiyum orutti eṉpatu avar kaatukku eʈʈiyiruntatu. aaṉaal kuntavai eṉṉa coṉṉaaɭ eṉpatai ituvarai yaarum avariʈam paccaiyaaka eʈuttuc collavillai. ippootu nantiṉiyiṉ vaayiṉaal ataik keeʈʈatum avaruʈaiya uɭɭam kollar ulaik kaɭattai ottatu. kup, kup eṉṟu aṉal kalanta perumuuccu vantatu. nantiṉiyiṉ kaɳɳiir avaruʈaiya uɭɭattiiyai meelum koɻuntu viʈʈeriyac ceyya neyyaaka utaviṟṟu. Lord Pazluvoor had heard that people talked with derision about his marrying a young girl in his old age. That Kundavai was one such person had reached his ears. But no one had told him explicitly till now, about what exactly Kundavai had said. Now, when he heard the words from Nandini's lips, his heart burned like the furnace in a smithy. "Puff, puff," came fiery sighs from his nostrils. Nandini's tears added further fuel to the fire in his heart.
"eṉ kaɳɳee! antac caɳʈaaɭap paataki appaʈiyaa coṉṉaaɭ? eṉṉaik kiɻa erumai maaʈu eṉṟaa coṉṉaaɭ? irukkaʈʈum; avaɭai...avaɭai...eṉṉa ceykiṟeeṉ, paar? erumai maaʈu allik koʈiyaik kaalil vaittu nacukkuvatu pool nacukki eṟikiṟeeṉ, paar! iṉṉum,...avaɭai...avaɭai" eṉṟu paɻuveeʈʈaraiyar, koopaaveecattiṉaal peecamuʈiyaatu tattaɭittaar. avar mukam aʈainta koora coruupattai varɳikkamuʈiyaatu. "My dearest! Is that what that wretched fiend said? Did she call me an aged buffalo? Wait -- let her, let her ... See what I shall do to her! I will trample her and throw her out just like the water-buffalo which tramples a lotus vine! And then, watch ... she ... I will, I will ..." Lord Pazluvoor could not talk because of his rage.
nantiṉi avaraic caantap paʈutta muyaṉṟaaɭ. avaruʈaiya irumpuk kaiyait taṉ puuvaiyotta karattiṉaal paṟṟi viralkaɭooʈu viralkaɭ iɳaittuk koottuk koɳʈaaɭ. Nandini tried to calm him down. She picked up his iron-hard palm and twined her flower-soft fingers with his.
"naataa! eṉakku neernta avamaaṉattait taaŋkaɭ poṟukka maaʈʈiirkaɭ eṉpatu eṉakkut teriyum. aaṉaal mattakajattiṉ maɳʈaiyaip piɭantu irattattaik kuʈikkum valimaiyuɭɭa ciŋkam, keevalam oru puuṉaiyiṉ miitu paaya muʈiyaatu kuntavai oru peɳ puuṉai; aaṉaal periya mantirakkaari. maayamum mantiramum ceytutaaṉ ellooraiyum avaɭ iʂʈam pool aaʈʈi vaittuk koɳʈirukkiṟaaɭ! intac cooɻa raajyattaiyee aaʈʈi vaittuk koɳʈirukkiṟaaɭ! avaɭuʈaiya mantirattai maaṟṟu mantirattaal taaṉ vella veeɳʈum. taŋkaɭukku viruppamillaa viʈʈaal colliviʈuŋkaɭ. iṉṟaikkee naaṉ inta maaɭikaiyai viʈʈu veɭiyeeṟukiṟeeṉ..." eṉṟu kuuṟi miiɳʈum vimmiṉaaɭ. "My dear Lord! I knew that you could not tolerate any insult offered to me. But the powerful lion which is capable of breaking the pate of a rogue elephant and drinking its blood, cannot pounce on a mere cat. Kundavai is a female cat and a powerful witch. She controls and orders everyone with her witchcraft and sorcery. She makes this Chozla Empire dance. Her sorcery can be overcome only by sorcery. If you don't like it, tell me. I shall leave this palace this very day." Her voice was still sob-laden.
paɻuveeʈʈaraiyariṉ koopa veṟi taɳintatu; mooka veṟi mikuntatu. Lord Pazluvoor's angry rage subsided and passion took its place.
"veeɳʈaam, veeɳʈaam! aayiram mantiravaatikaɭai veeɳʈumaaṉaalum aɻaittu vaittukkoɭ. nii pooka veeɳʈaam! eṉ uyir aṉaiyavaɭ nii! aṉaiyavaɭ eṉṉa? eṉ uyiree niitaaṉ! uyir pooyviʈʈaal appuram inta uʈampu eṉṉa ceyyum?... ippootee eṉṉai nii vilakki vaittiruppatu eṉṉai uyirooʈu vaittuk kolkiṟatu! ittaṉai mantiram terintu vaittirukkiṟaayee? eṉakku oru mantiram collit tarakkuuʈaataa?" eṉṟaar. "No, no! Never. Call a thousand sorcerers if you wish and talk to them, but don't go! You are all my life! Why `all'? You are my life! And if life departs what can this body do? ... Even now, your keeping me away, is killing me alive! You know so many magic spells -- can you not teach me one such spell?"
"naataa! taŋkaɭ kaiyil vaaɭum veelum irukkumpootu mantiram etaṟku? peetaip peɳɳaakiya eṉṉiʈam viʈʈuviʈuŋkaɭ maayamantiraŋkaɭai! taŋkaɭukku etaṟku maayamum mantiramum?" eṉṟaaɭ nantiṉi. "My dearest Lord! When you have a sword and spear in your hands what need for magic spells? Leave the spells and sorcery to a hapless female like me. Why do you need witchcraft or sorcery?"
"kaɳɳee! nii uṉ pavaɭa vaay tiṟantu 'naataa' eṉṟu aɻaikkumpootee eṉ uʈampu cilirkkiṟatu uṉ poṉ mukattaip paarttaal eṉ mati cuɻalkiṟatu! eṉ kaiyil vaaɭum veelum iruppatu uɳmaitaaṉ. ataiyellaam poorkkaɭattil pakaivarkaɭait taakkuvataṟku upayookippeeṉ. aaṉaal anta aayutaŋkaɭai vaittuk koɳʈu intak koʈi maɳʈapattil eṉṉa ceyveeṉ? maṉmataṉuʈaiya paaɳaŋkaɭukku etirppaaɳam eṉṉiʈam oṉṟumillaiyee? uṉṉiʈam allavaa irukkiṟatu? eṉakku mantiram etaṟkaaka eṉṟu keeʈkiṟaay! eṉ uʈalaiyum uyiraiyum oyaamal erittuk koɳʈirukkiṟatee, antat tiiyait taɳippataṟkaakattaaṉ! ataṟku eetaavatu mantiram uṉakkut terintiruntaal collu! illaiyeṉṟaal, uṉ puu meeṉiyait toʈʈu makiɻum paakkiyattai eṉakkuk koʈu! eppaʈiyaavatu eṉ uyiraik kaappaaṟṟu! kaɳmaɳi! ulakam aṟiyac caastira vitippaʈi niiyum naaṉum maɳantu iraɳʈarai aaɳʈukaɭ aakiṉṟaṉa! aayiṉum naam ulaka vaɻakkappaʈi ilvaaɻkkai naʈatta aarampikkavillai. viratam eṉṟum, nooṉpu eṉṟum colli eṉṉai otukkiyee vaittuk koɳʈirukkiṟaay. karam piʈittu maɳantukoɳʈa kaɳavaṉai vaaʈʈi vataikkiṟaay! allatu oru vaɻiyaaka eṉakku uṉ kaiyiṉaal viʂattaik koʈuttuk koṉṟuviʈu!..." "Darling, when you open your coral lips and call me `Dearest', my very being is filled with happiness. Your golden face makes my head swim. Yes, I do have a sword and spear in my hands. But, I can use them only against my enemies in the battlefield. What can I do with such weapons in this flower laden bower? I have no arrows against Cupids's darts! You have them! You ask me why I need magic spells! This fire of passion that burns constantly, consuming my very life and body -- I need spells to quench it. Do you have any spells for that? Teach me, if you know them? If not, let me have the fortune of touching your flower like body and enjoy that happiness. Save my life, darling! It is more than two and a half years since we have been man and wife as per the laws of the land and the sacred texts. But still, we do not lead a normal wedded life. You put me away with excuses of vows and penances. You are torturing your husband who took your hand in marriage. At least, once and for all, give me some poison with your own hands...."
nantiṉi taṉ cevikaɭaip pottikkoɳʈu "aiyaiyoo! immaatiri koʈiya vaarttaikaɭaic collaatiirkaɭ! iṉṉoru muṟai ippaʈic coṉṉaal, niiŋkaɭ collukiṟapaʈiyee ceytuviʈuveeṉ. viʂattaik kuʈittuc cettup pooveeṉ. appuṟam taaŋkaɭ kavalaiyaṟṟu nimmatiyaaka irukkalaam!" eṉṟaaɭ. Nandini covered her ears, "Dear dear me! Don't utter such cruel words! If you say such things again I will do as you say! I will drink poison and die. Then you can live without worry!"
"illai, illai; iṉi appaʈic collavillai, eṉṉai maṉṉittu viʈu! nii viʂaŋ kuʈittu iṟantaal eṉakku maṉanimmati uɳʈaakumaa? ippootu araip paittiyamaayirukkiṟeeṉ. appootu muɻup paittiyamaakiviʈuveeṉ!..." "No, no! I won't say anything like that again. Forgive me! How can I be at peace if you drink poison and die? Now I am half mad, then I will become totally deranged."
"naataa! etaṟkaakat taaŋkaɭ paittiyamaaka veeɳʈum? eṉṟaikku naam kaippiʈittu maɳantu koɳʈoo moo, aṉṟaikkee naam iraɳʈu uʈampum or uyirum aakiviʈʈoo m. uyirum uyirum kalantuviʈʈaṉa; uɭɭamum uɭɭamum ceerntu viʈʈaṉa; taŋkaɭ itayattiṉ ovvoru tuʈippum iŋkee eṉ itayattil etiroliyai uɳʈaakkukiṟatu. taŋkaɭ neɲcil utikkum ovvoru eɳɳamum iŋkee eṉ akak kaɳɳaaʈiyil piratipalikkiṟatu. taŋkaɭ puruvam nerintaal eṉ kaɳkalaŋkukiṟatu. taŋkaɭ miicai tuʈittaal eṉ kuʈal tuʈikkiṟatu. ippaʈi naam uyirkkuyiraaṉa piṟaku, keevalam inta uʈalaip paṟṟi eeṉ cintaṉai? maɳɳiṉaal aaṉa uʈampu itu. orunaaɭ erintu caampalaaki maɳɳooʈu maɳ aakap pookiṟa uʈampu itu..." "Dear Lord, why should you become mad? On the very day we wed, we became one soul in two bodies. Your soul and mine became one; heart and heart mingled. Every heartbeat of yours echo's in my heart. Every thought that rises in your mind is reflected here in my vision. When you frown, my eyes fill with tears. Thus, when we have become one heart and soul, why bother about mere bodies? This body is made of dust for one day it will burn to ashes, turn to dust again..."
"niṟuttu! niṟuttu! uṉ koʈumaiyaaṉa vaarttaikaɭaik keeʈʈu eṉ kaatu koppaɭikkiṟatu!" eṉṟu paɻuveeʈʈaraiyar alaṟiṉaar. meelum avaɭ peeca iʈaŋkoʈaamal peeciṉaar; "maɳɳiṉaal aaṉa uʈampu eṉṟaa coṉṉaay? poy! poy! teeṉ maɳam kamaɻum uṉ kaṉi vaayiṉaal attakaiya perum poyyaic collaatee! uṉ uʈampai maɳɳiṉaal ceytataakavaa coṉṉaay? oru naaɭum illai. ulakil ettaṉaiyoo peɳkaɭ irukkiṟaarkaɭ. avarkaɭaiyellaam pirammateevaṉ maɳɳiṉaalum ceytirukkalaam, kalliṉaalum ceytirukkalaam, cuɳɳaampiṉaalum ceytirukkalaam. kariyaiyum caampalaiyum kalantum ceytirukkalaam. aaṉaal uṉṉuʈaiya tirumeeṉiyaip pirammaa eppaʈic ceytaaṉ teriyumaa? teevalookattu mantaara maraŋkaɭiliruntu utirnta malarkaɭaic ceekarittaaṉ; tamiɻakattukku vantu centaamarai malarkaɭaip paṟittuc ceekarittaaṉ; ceekaritta malarkaɭait teevalookattil teevaamirtam vaittirukkum taŋkak kalacattil pooʈʈaaṉ. amutamum malarkaɭum uuṟik kalantu oree kuɻampaaṉa piṟaku eʈuttaaṉ. antak kuɻampil veɳɳilaak kiraɳaŋkaɭai uuʈʈiṉaaṉ. paɳʈait tamiɻakattup paaɳarkaɭai aɻaittu vantu yaaɻ vaacikkac coṉṉaaṉ. anta yaaɻiṉ icaiyaiyum kalantaaṉ. appaʈi eeṟpaʈʈa aṟputamaaṉa kalavaiyiṉaal uṉ tirumeeṉiyaip paʈaittaaṉ pirammateevaṉ..." "Stop! Stop! My ears burn with your cruel words." Lord Pazluvoor continued, without letting her talk: "Are you saying it is made of dust? A lie! An untruth! It can never be so! There are several women in this world -- the creator might have made them of dust or dirt, stone or lime; coal or ashes. But, do you know how Brahma, the creator, fashioned your divine form? He collected delicate mandara flowers from the gardens of heaven and came to this Tamil land and collected the blushing lotus from our creeks. He threw the flowers in a golden bowl full of nectar from paradise. He then took that mixture and added moon-beams to it. He called for the bards of ancient times and asked them to play the veena, blending their melody into his mixture. With that unique mixture he created your divine form..."
"naataa! eetoo pirammaavukkup pakkattil iruntu paarttavaraip pool peecukiṟiirkaɭee! inta varɳaṉaikaɭukkellaam naaṉ oruttitaaṉaa akappaʈʈeeṉ? taŋkaɭuʈaiya antappurattil evvaɭavoo peɳɳaracikaɭ irukkiṟaarkaɭ. iraaja kulaŋkaɭil piṟantavarkaɭ. ettaṉaiyoo niiɳʈa kaalamaaka avarkaɭuʈaṉum illaṟam naʈattiyirukkiṟiirkaɭ. eṉṉait taaŋkaɭ paarttu iraɳʈarai aaɳʈutaaṉ aakiṟatu!..." "My Lord! You talk as if you stood besides the creator and watched all this! Am I the only person available to you for such descriptions? You have several other queens in your palace -- nobly born women. You have lived with them for several years. It is merely two and a half years since you met me..."
eṉṟu nantiṉi colvataṟkuɭ, paɻuveeʈʈaraiyar kuṟukkiʈʈaar. avaruʈaiya uɭɭattil poŋkik koɳʈirunta uɳarcci veɭɭattai vaarttaikaɭiṉ muulamaakavaavatu veɭippaʈuttiviʈa virumpiṉaar poolum. avaraip paṟṟi eritta taapattiiyaic col maariyiṉaal naṉaintu aɳaikka muyaṉṟaar poolum. Before Nandini could finish, he intervened. Perhaps he wished to express his desire at least with words. He perhaps wished to quench the fire of passion at least with a shower of words!
"nantiṉi eṉ antappurattu maatarkaɭaip paṟṟic coṉṉaay. paɻamaiyaaṉa paɻuvuur maṉṉar kulam niiʈittu vaɭara veeɳʈum eṉpataṟkaakavee avarkaɭai naaṉ maɳanteeṉ. avarkaɭil cilar malaʈikaɭaakit tolaintaarkaɭ. veeṟu cilar peɳkaɭaiyee peṟṟaɭittaarkaɭ. 'kaʈavuɭ aruɭ avvaɭavutaaṉ' eṉṟu maṉa nimmatiyaʈainteeṉ. peɳkaɭiṉ niṉaivaiyee vekukaalam viʈʈoɻittirunteeṉ. iraajaaŋka kaariyaŋkaɭee eṉ kavaṉam muɻuvataiyum kavarntiruntaṉa. cooɻa raajyattiṉ meeṉmaiyait tavira veeṟu enta niṉaivukkum inta neɲcil iʈamirukkavillai. ippaʈi irukkumpootutaaṉ paaɳʈiyarkaɭooʈu iṟutip perum yuttam vantatu. vaalipap piraayattut taɭapatikaɭ palar iruntapootilum eṉṉaal piṉ taŋki irukka muʈiyavillai. naaṉ poorkkaɭam ceṉṟiraaviʈʈaal, attakaiya maaperum veṟṟi kiʈaittum iraatu. paaɳʈiyar paʈaiyai aʈiyooʈu naacam ceytu maturaiyil veṟṟik koʈi naaʈʈiya piṟaku koŋku naaʈu ceṉṟeeṉ. aŋkiruntu akaɳʈa kaaveerik karaiyooʈu tirumpi vantu koɳʈirunteeṉ. "Nandini, you talk about those other women in my palace. I married them only to establish the ancient line of the Pazluvoor clan. Some of them remained barren and the others merely gave birth to girls. I accepted it as God's decree and remained tranquil. I had forgotten a desire for women for a long time. Affairs of the state occupied all my attention and time. There was no place in my heart for anything but the welfare of this Chozla Empire. Then we fought the final battle with the Pandiyas. Even though the campaign was led by several younger generals, I could not remain behind. Anyway, if I had not entered that battlefield, our forces would not have won such a decisive victory. My skill and strength helped. After destroying the Pandiya forces completely and occupying Madurai, I went to the Kongu Kingdom. I was returning from there along the banks of the Cauvery.
"vaɻiyil kaaʈu aʈarnta or iʈattil uṉṉaik kaɳʈeeṉ. mutalil nii aŋku niṟkiṟaay eṉpatai eṉṉaal nampavee muʈiyavillai. kaɳɳai muuʈi muuʈit tiṟantu paartteeṉ. appootum nii niṉṟaay. 'nii vaṉateevataiyaaka irukka veeɳʈum; arukil ceṉṟatum maṟaintuviʈuvaay!" eṉṟu eɳɳik koɳʈu neruŋkiṉeeṉ. appootum nii maṟaintuviʈavillai. 'puraaɳak kataikaɭilee colliyiruppatu pool, corkkattiliruntu caapam peṟṟup puumikku vanta teevakaṉṉikai allatu kantarvap peɳɳaayirukka veeɳʈum; maṉita paaʂai uṉakkut terintiraatu!' eṉṟu eɳɳikkoɳʈu, 'peɳɳee! nii yaar?' eṉṟu keeʈʈeeṉ. nii nalla tamiɻil maṟumoɻi kuuṟiṉaay. 'naaṉ anaataip peɳ; uŋkaɭiʈam aʈaikkalam pukunteeṉ; eṉṉaik kaappaaṟṟuŋkaɭ' eṉṟaay. uṉṉaip pallakkil eeṟṟi aɻaittuk koɳʈu vantapootu eṉ maṉam eɳɳaatatellaam eɳɳiyatu. uṉṉai eŋkeeyoo, eppootoo, muṉṉam paarttiruppatu poolat tooṉṟiyatu. aaṉaal niṉaittu niṉaittup paarttum eŋkee eṉṟu teriyavillai. caʈʈeṉṟu eṉ maṉattai muuʈiyirunta maayattirai vilakiyatu; uɳmai utayamaayiṟṟu. uṉṉai inta jaṉmattil naaṉ muṉṉaal paarttatillai. aaṉaal muntaiya pala piṟavikaɭil paarttirukkiṟeeṉ eṉpatu terintatu. antap puurva jaṉmattu niṉaivukaɭ ellaam mootikkoɳʈu vantaṉa. "On the way, beside a forest path, I saw you. At first I could not believe that I saw you; I blinked my eyes to make sure. You were still there. `She must be a forest nymph; she will disappear if I go closer,' thought I. But, I came closer and you did not go away. I decided that like in myths, you must be some heavenly nymph who came down to this earth because of some curse or misdeed. `She may not know our language,' I thought as I asked, `Girl, who are you?' You answered in sweet Tamil: `I am an orphan; I seek sanctuary with you; please protect me.' I carried you back in my palanquin, with all sorts of wild thoughts. I felt that I had seen you somewhere, sometime long ago. But the more I thought about it, the less I could remember. Suddenly the veil of deception that covered my mind was lifted. Truth dawned. I realized that I had never seen you before in this birth, but I had seen you in earlier births several times! I recalled all those previous births easily.
"nii akalikaiyaaka inta ulakil piṟantiruntaay; appootu naaṉ teeveentiraṉaaka irunteeṉ. corkkalooka aaʈciyait tuṟantu riʂi caapattukkum tuɳintu uṉṉait teeʈik koɳʈu vanteeṉ. piṟaku naaṉ cantaṉu makaaraajaṉaakap piṟantirunteeṉ. kaŋkaik karaiyooʈu veeʈʈaiyaaʈac cellukaiyil uṉṉaik kaɳʈeeṉ; puulooka peɳɳappool urukkoɳʈirunta kaŋkaiyaakiya uṉṉaik kaatalitteeṉ. piṟaku oru kaalattil kaaveerip puumpaʈʈaɳattil naaṉ koovalaṉaayp piṟantirunteeṉ. nii kaɳɳakiyaaka avatarittiruntaay. eṉ aṟivai maṟaitta maayaiyiṉaal uṉṉaic cila kaalam maṟantirunteeṉ. piṟaku maayait tirai vilakiṟṟu. uṉ arumaiyai aṟinteeṉ. maturai nakarukku aɻaittuc ceṉṟeeṉ. vaɻiyil uṉṉai aayar kuʈiyil viʈʈu viʈʈuc cilampu viṟkac ceṉṟeeṉ. vaɲcakattiṉaal uyirai iɻanteeṉ. ataṟkup paɻikku paɻiyaaka intap piṟaviyil maturaip paaɳʈiyaṉ kulattai naacam ceytu viʈʈut tirumpi varum pootu uṉṉaik kaɳʈeeṉ. pala nuuṟu aaɳʈukaɭukku muṉpu pirinta kaɳɳaki niitaaṉ eṉpatai uɳarnteeṉ!..." "You were born in this world as Ahalya; I was Indra. I gave up my kingship over paradise and braving the curse of the learned ascetics, I came down in search of you. Much later, I was born as the Emperor Santhanu. I went along the banks of the Ganga on a hunt. I saw you -- you were divine Ganga in the guise of a earthly woman. I loved you then. Much later, several eons later, I was born as Kovalan in Kaviri-pattinam. You were Kannagi. For some time I had forgotten you, because of the cloak of maya that covered my eyes. The cloak fell and I realized your worth. I took you to Madurai and left you in the farmhouse to go into the city, to sell your anklet. I lost my life because of treachery. In revenge for that, in this birth, I destroyed the Pandiya clan and came back to find you once again. You were that same Kannagi whom I had seen several hundreds of years ago ...."
(Note: The myth of Ahalya and Indra is from the epic Ramayana. The story of Santhanu and Ganga is from the epic Mahabarata. Kannagi and Kovalan are from the romance Silappadikaram.)
ippaʈip paɻuveeʈʈaraiyar muṟpiṟavik kataikaɭaic collikkoɳʈu vantapootu nantiṉi, avar mukattaip paarkkaamal veeṟu ticaiyai nookkik koɳʈiruntaaɭ. ataṉaal avaɭ mukattil appootu tooṉṟiya paava veeṟupaaʈukaɭaip paɻuveeʈʈaraiyar kavaṉikkavillai, kavaṉittiruntaal, avar toʈarntu peeciyiruppaaraa eṉpatu canteekantaaṉ. As the Elder Lord Pazluvoor was recounting the tales of his past births, Nandini had turned her face away looking into the darkness. Because of this, Lord Pazluvoor did not notice the changing expressions on her face. It is doubtful if he would have continued talking in the same fashion if he had seen them.
muuccu viʈuvataṟkaaka avar caṟṟu niṟuttiyapootu, nantiṉi avarait tirumpip paarttu, "naataa! taaŋkaɭ kuuṟiya utaaraɳaŋkaɭ avvaɭavu poruttamaayillai. ellaam koɲcam apacakuṉamaakavee irukkiṉṟaṉa. veeɳʈumaaṉaal, taŋkaɭai maṉmataṉ eṉṟum eṉṉai rati eṉṟum colluŋaɭ!" eṉṟu muṉpool mukamalarntu puṉṉakai ceytaaɭ. When he stopped to take a breath, Nandini turned back to him, "My Lord! Your examples are not very appropriate. Everything is slightly inauspicious. If you want, call yourself Manmatha (Lord of Beauty and Love) and me Rathi (Goddess of Beauty and Passion)!" she smiled once again.
paɻuveeʈʈaraiyariṉ mukamum appootu makiɻcciyiṉaalum perumaiyiṉaalum malarntu viɭaŋkiyatu. evvaɭavu avalaʈcaɳamaaṉa maṉitaṉaayiṉum, taaṉ kaatalitta peɳɳiṉaal 'maṉmataṉ' eṉṟu aɻaikkappaʈʈaal kutuukalappaʈaatavaṉ yaar? eṉṟaalum, taṟperumaiyai virumpaatavar pool peeciṉaar. Lord Pazluvoor's face brightened with pride and happiness. Which man will not feel happy, however ugly he may be, if his sweetheart calls him Manmatha, the God of Love and Beauty? But still, he spoke as if he did not wish to praise himself.
"kaɳmaɳi! uṉṉai rati eṉpatu mikavum poruttamaaṉatutaaṉ. aaṉaal eṉṉai 'maṉmataṉ' eṉṟu colluvatu poruntumaa! uṉ aṉpu mikutiyiṉaal colkiṟaay!" eṉṟaar. "My dearest, it is quite appropriate to call you Rathi. But can we compare me to Manmatha? You call me so because of your love for me."
"naataa! eṉ kaɳkaɭukkut taaŋkaɭ taaṉ maṉmataṉ. aaɳ piɭɭaikaɭukku aɻaku viiram. taŋkaɭaip pooṉṟa viiraati viirar intat teṉṉaaʈʈil yaarum illai eṉpatai ulakamee collum. aʈuttapaʈiyaaka, aaɳmai paʈaittavarkaɭukku aɻaku taruvatu apalaikaɭiʈam irakkam. anta irakkam taŋkaɭiʈam iruppataṟku naaṉee attaaʈci. iṉṉa uur, iṉṉa kulam eṉṟu teriyaata inta eeɻai anaataip peɳɳait taaŋkaɭ aɻaittu vantu aʈaikkalam aɭittiirkaɭ. iɳaiyillaata aṉpaiyum aataravaiyum eṉpeeril corintiirkaɭ. appaʈippaʈʈa taŋkaɭai naaṉ vekukaalam kaattirukkumpaʈi ceyyamaaʈʈeeṉ. eṉṉuʈaiya viratamum nooṉpum muʈiyum kaalam neruŋkiviʈʈatu..." eṉṟaaɭ. "My Lord, in my eyes you are Manmatha! Bravery is beauty for menfolk. The world knows that there is no warrior who is your match in this land. Men acquire allure because of their kindness to waifs like me. You gave sanctuary to me, an orphan girl, without questions about my past. You protected me and showered all your love on me. I will not make you wait for long. The time for ending my penance and fulfilling my vow is drawing close," spoke Nandini.
"kaɳmaɳi! eṉṉa viratam, eṉṉa nooṉpu eṉpatai maʈʈum teɭivaakac colliviʈu! evvaɭavu ciikkiram muʈittut taralaamoo avvaɭavu ciikkirattil muʈittut taruveeṉ!" eṉṟaar paɻuvuur aracar. "Dearest, tell me clearly what that vow is and what your penance is about. I will complete it for you as quickly as possible."
"taṉṉaik kaaʈʈilum maṉmataṉ veeṟu illai eṉṟu eɳɳiyirukkum intac cuntara cooɻaruʈaiya cantatikaɭ taɲcaic cimmaacaṉattil eeṟakkuuʈaatu. taṟperumai koɳʈa antak kuntavaiyiṉ karvattai oʈukka veeɳʈum..." "None of the descendants of this Sundara Chozla who thinks he is the handsomest man in this world, should ascend the Chozla throne. The pride of that prejudiced female, Kundavai, must be wounded ...."
"nantiṉi! anta iraɳʈu kaariyaŋkaɭum niṟaiveeṟiviʈʈataakavee nii vaittuk koɭɭalaam. aatittaṉukkum aruɭmoɻivarmaṉukkum paʈʈam kiʈaiyaatu; maturaantakaṉukkee paʈʈam kaʈʈa veeɳʈum eṉṟu inta raajyattiṉ talaivarkaɭ ellaarum cammatittu viʈʈaarkaɭ..." "Nandini, think them done. Aditya and Arulmozli will not get the throne. All the chieftains of this nation have agreed to crown Madurandaka as the next Crown Prince."
"ellaarum cammatittu viʈʈaarkaɭaa? uɳmaitaaṉaa?" eṉṟa nantiṉi aɻuttamaakak keeʈʈaaɭ. "Have all agreed? Truly?" asked Nandini sharply.
"iraɳʈu muuṉṟu peerait tavira maṟṟavarkaɭ ellaarum cammatittu viʈʈaarkaɭ. koʈumpaaɭuuraaṉum, malaiyamaaṉum, paarttipeentiraṉum nammuʈaṉ eṉṟum iɳaŋka maaʈʈaarkaɭ. avarkaɭaip paṟṟik kavalaiyillai..." "Except two or three, all have agreed. That fellow of Kodumbalur, Malayaman and Parthiban will never join us. I have no worry about them."
"aayiṉum kaariyam muʈiyum varaiyil jaakkirataiyaaka irukka veeɳʈiyatutaaṉee?" "Nevertheless, we must be careful till the deed is done."
"ataṟkuc canteekam illai. ellaa jaakkirataiyum naaṉ ceytu koɳʈu taaṉ varukiṟeeṉ. maṟṟavarkaɭiṉ muʈʈaaɭ taṉattiṉaal picaku neerntaaltaaṉ neerntatu. iṉṟaikkuk kuuʈa attakaiya picaku oṉṟu neerntirukkiṟatu. kaaɲciyiliruntu vanta oru vaalipaṉ kaalaantakaṉai eemaaṟṟiviʈʈuc cakkaravarttiyaic cantittu olai koʈuttirukkiṟaaṉ..." "No doubt about that. I am taking care of everything. Mistakes may occur because of the foolishness of others. Even today such a mistake happened. A young messenger from Kanchi who arrived today, duped Kalanthaka and met the Emperor to deliver a letter and message."
"aakaa! taŋkaɭ tampiyaip paṟṟit taaŋkaɭ oyaamal pukaɻntu koɳʈirukkiṟiirkaɭ. avarukkuc caamaarttiyam pootaatu eṉṟu naaṉ collavillaiyaa?" "Ah! You keep praising your brother all the time. I always maintain that he is not capable enough."
"inta viʂayattil acaʈʈuttaṉamaakattaaṉ pooy viʈʈaaṉ! eetoo nam araɳmaṉai muttirai mootirattai anta vaalipaṉ kaaʈʈiyataakac colkiṟaaṉ!" "Yes, he was mislead in this affair. He was saying something about that youth showing our signet ring."
"eemaantu pooṉavarkaɭ ippaʈittaaṉ eetaavatu kaaraɳam colluvaarkaɭ! eemaaṟṟiya anta vaalipaṉaip piʈikka muyaṟci eetum ceyyavillaiyaa?" "Those who have been duped will try and find some excuse like that! Haven't they made any efforts to arrest that youth?"
"muyaṟci ceyyaamal eṉṉa? kooʈʈaikku uɭɭeeyum veɭiyeeyum veeʈʈai aarampamaakiviʈʈatu! eppaʈiyum piʈittu viʈuvaarkaɭ. itaṉaalellaam nammuʈaiya kaariyattukku oṉṟum paŋkam vantuviʈaatu. cakkaravartti kaalamaaṉatum maturaantakaṉ cimmaacaṉam eeṟuvatu niccayam..." "Why no effort? the search for him has begun -- inside and outside the fort. They will find him. This will not hinder our plans in any way. After the Emperor, the throne is surely for Madurandaka."
"naataa! eṉṉuʈaiya viratam eṉṉaveṉpatait taŋkaɭukkut teriyappaʈuttum kaalam ippootu neruŋki vantuviʈʈatu..." "My Lord, the time has come for me to reveal the aim of my penance..."
"kaɳɳee! ataic collumpaʈitaaṉ naaṉum keeʈkiṟeeṉ..." "Dearest, I have been asking you to tell me that ..."
"maturaantakaṉ - anta acaʈʈup piɭɭai - peɳ eṉṟaal pallai iɭippavaṉ - avaṉ paʈʈattukku varuvatiṉaal eṉṉuʈaiya viratam niṟaiveeṟiviʈaatu..." "My vows will not be fulfilled by placing that foolish youth, Madurandaka -- he who shows his teeth to smile at every woman -- that idiot, on the throne."
"veeṟu etaṉaal niṟaiveeṟum? uṉ viruppattaic collu! niṟaiveeṟṟi vaikka naaṉ irukkiṟeeṉ..." "What will fulfil your vow? Tell me your desire. I will accomplish it."
"aracee! eṉ ciṟu piraayattil oru pirapala cootiʈaṉ eṉ jaatakattaip paarttaaṉ. patiṉeʈʈup piraayam varaiyil naaṉ paṟpala iṉṉalkaɭukku uɭɭaaveeṉ eṉṟu coṉṉaaṉ..." "My King! A famous astrologer read my horoscope when I was very young. He said that I would be troubled by various incidents till my eighteenth year."
"iṉṉum eṉṉa coṉṉaaṉ?" "And then what did he say?"
"patiṉeʈʈup piraayattukkup piṟaku tacai maaṟum eṉṟaaṉ. iɳaiyillaata uṉṉata pataviyai aʈaiveeṉ eṉṟu coṉṉaaṉ." "He said that my luck would change after my eighteenth year. He said that I would achieve an incomparable position of prestige."
"avaṉ coṉṉatu uɳmaitaaṉ! antac cootiʈaṉ yaar eṉṟu collu! avaṉukkuk kaṉakaapiʂeekam ceytu vaikkiṟeeṉ." "Ah! He said the truth. Who is that astrologer? I will shower him with gold coins."
"naataa!" "My Lord!"
"kaɳɳee!" "Dearest!"
"iṉṉum antac cootiʈaṉ kuuṟiyatu oṉṟu irukkiṟatu. ataic collaʈʈumaa?" "He said one more thing. Shall I say it?"
"kaʈʈaayam collu! colliyee tiiraveeɳʈum!" "Surely, you must tell me!"
"eṉṉaik kaippiʈittu maɳantu koɭɭum kaɳavar, maɳimakuʈam tarittu oru makaa caamraajyattiṉ cimmaacaṉattil aimpattaaṟu teecattu raajaakkaɭum vantu aʈipaɳintu eettum cakkaravarttiyaaka viiṟṟiruppaar eṉṟu antac cootiʈaṉ coṉṉaaṉ. atai niṟaiveeṟṟuviirkaɭaa?" "That astrologer said, that the man who takes my hand and weds me will sit upon the throne of a large empire and kings of all the fifty-six nations will come and bow to him as he sits on the throne of an emperor. That's what the astrologer said. Can you fulfill that?"
paɻuveeʈʈaraiyariṉ cevikaɭil ivvaarttaikaɭ viɻuntatum, avarukku muṉṉaalirunta nantiṉiyum avaɭ viiṟṟirunta maɲcamum cuɻaṉṟaṉa. lataa maɳʈapam cuɻaṉṟatu. anta maɳʈapattiṉ tuuɳkaɭ cuɻaṉṟaṉa. etiree irunta iruɭaʈarnta tooppuc cuɻaṉṟatu. nilaak katiril oɭirnta mara uccikaɭ cuɻaṉṟaṉa. vaaṉattu naʈcattiraŋkaɭ cuɻaṉṟaṉa. irupuṟattu maaɭikaikaɭum cuɻaṉṟaṉa. ulakamee cuɻaṉṟatu! When Lord Pazluvoor heard these words -- Nandini, the couch on which she was sitting, everything, swam before his eyes. The flower laden gazebo swirled; its pillars twirled. The dark garden in front began revolving. Treetops bathed in the moonlight began turning. The mansions on both sides revolved. The world swirled!
