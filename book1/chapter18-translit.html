patiṉeʈʈaam attiyaayam Chapter 18
iʈumpaṉkaari Idumban Kari
koɭɭiʈattup paricil tuṟaiyil aaɻvaarkkaʈiyaaṉnampi eṉṉum tirumalaiyappaṉai viʈʈu viʈʈu vantuviʈʈoom. anta viira vaiʂɳavarai ippootu koɲcam kavaṉikkalaam. We left Azlvar-adiyan, also known as Thirumalai, at the ferry landing near Kollidam. Let us consider that Vaishnava zealot once again.
vantiyatteevaṉ kutirai eeṟik kuʈantai nakar nookkic ceṉṟatum, tirumalai avaṉ pooṉa ticaiyaip paarttuk koɳʈee taṉakkuɭ collik koɳʈaaṉ. When Vandiya Devan sat upon his horse and rode away towards Kudanthai, Mr. Thirumalai started mumbling these words to himself:
"inta vaalipaṉ mikap pollaatavaṉaayirukkiṟaaṉ.naam taʈʈiyil nuɻaintaal ivaṉ koolattil nuɻaikiṟaaṉ. ivaṉ uɳmaiyil yaaruʈaiya aaɭ, etaṟkaaka, eŋkee pookiṟaaṉ eṉpatai nammaal kaɳʈupiʈikka muʈiyavillai. kaʈampuur maaɭikaiyil naʈanta catik kuuʈʈattil ivaṉ kalantu koɳʈaaṉaa eṉṟum teriyavillai. nalla veeɭaiyaakak kuʈantai cootiʈaraip paṟṟi ivaṉiʈam colli vaittoom. nammaal aṟiya muʈiyaatataik kuʈantai cootiʈaraavatu terintu koɭɭukiṟaaraa paarkkalaam!..." "This youth is very sharp. If I enter through the sieve he squeezes in through the floor decoration. I have not been able to find the truth about whose man he is, or why and where he is going. I don't know if he was part of the treasonable meeting at Kadamboor Fort. Luckily, I mentioned the Astrologer of Kudanthai to him! Let us see if that old man can find out what I could not ..."
"eṉṉa, cuvaami! aracamarattooʈu peecaṟiiŋkaɭaa? uŋkaɭukku niiŋkaɭee peecikkiṟiiŋkaɭaa?" eṉṟa kuralaik keeʈʈu tirumalaiyappaṉ tirumpip paarttaaṉ. "Hello Sir! Are you talking to that ficus tree -- or, are you talking to yourself?" Hearing a voice behind him, Mr. Thirumalai turned around.
kaʈampuuriliruntu vanta vantiyatteevaṉukkuk kutirai piʈittuk koɳʈu vanta paɳiyaaɭ pakkattil niṉṟaaṉ. The servant who had come from Kadamboor and procured the horse for Vandiya Devan, was standing there.
"appaṉee! niiyaa keeʈʈaay? naaṉ eṉakku naaṉee peecik koɭɭavum illai; araca marattooʈu peecavum illai. inta marattiṉ meelee oru veetaaɭam irukkiṟatu; ataṉooʈu ciṟitu callaapam ceyteeṉ!" eṉṟaaṉ tirumalaiyappaṉ. "My good man! Are you asking me? I wasn't talking to myself; neither was I talking to the tree. I had a little discussion with a vampire sitting upon this tree," said Mr. Thirumalai.
"ohoo! appaʈiŋkaɭaa! anta veetaaɭam caivamaa? vaiʂɳavamaa?" eṉṟaaṉ anta aaɭ. "Oh! Is that so, Sir! Is the vampire of the Saiva sect or of the Vaishnava sect?" asked that good man.
"ataittaaṉ naaṉum keeʈʈuk koɳʈirunteeṉ. ataṟkuɭɭee nii vantu kuṟukkiʈʈaay. veetaaɭam maṟaintu viʈʈatu; pooṉaal pookaʈʈum! uṉ peyar eṉṉa appaṉee?" "That's what I was trying to find out. You interrupted and now the vampire has disappeared. ... Let him go! ... What is your name, my good man?"
"etaṟkaaka keeʈkiṟiiŋka, cuvaami!" "Why do you ask, Sir?"
"naʈuk koɭɭiʈattil paʈaku kaviɻaamal kaappaaṟṟiṉaayee! appaʈippaʈʈa puɳɳiyavaaṉaakiya uṉṉai naaṉ ɲaapakattil vaittuk koɭɭa veeɳʈaamaa?" "You saved us in the middle of the Kollidam by preventing the ferry-boat from overturning. Shouldn't I remember a commendable fellow like you?
"eṉ peyar..eṉ peyar..iʈumpaṉkaari, cuvaami!" eṉṟu iɻuttaaṟpool coṉṉaaṉ. "My name ... My name ... is Idumban Kari, Sir." He said it with some hesitation.
"o! iʈumpaṉkaariyaa? eppootoo keeʈʈa ɲaapakamaayirukkiṟatee!" "Oh! Idumban Kari? I have heard it somewhere ... before..."
iʈumpaṉkaari appootu oru vicittiramaaṉa kaariyam ceytaaṉ.taṉṉuʈaiya viritta kaikaɭ iraɳʈaiyum oṉṟiṉ meel oṉṟaik kuppuṟutti vaittuk koɳʈu, iru orattuk kaʈʈai viralkaɭaiyum aaʈʈiṉaaṉ; aaʈʈik koɳʈee tirumalaiyappariṉ mukattaip paarttaaṉ. At that time Idumban Kari did something very peculiar! He placed the spreading palms of both his hands one upon the other, and wriggled his thumbs. As he did this, he looked at Azlvar-adiyan's face.
"appaṉee! itu eṉṉa camikɲai? eṉakku viɭaŋkavillaiyee?" eṉṟaaṉ tirumalai. "My dear man! What is this sign? I don't understand ...?"
appootu iʈumpaṉkaariyiṉ kariya mukam meelum ciṟitu karuttatu; kaɳ puruvaŋkaɭ nerintaṉa. Idumban Kari's dark face darkened further. His eyebrows drew close in exasperation.
"naaṉaa? naaṉ oṉṟum camikɲai ceyyavillaiyee?" eṉṟaaṉ. "I? I didn't make any sign," he said.
"ceytaay ceytaay! naaṉtaaṉ paartteeṉee? paratanaaʈʈiya caastirattil tirumaaliṉ mutal avataarattukku oru astam piʈippatuɳʈu atumaatiri ceytaayee?" "You did. You did do so. I saw it. Exponents of the classical dance Bharata Natyam, hold a certain posture when they represent the first incarnation of God Vishnu; you did something like that."
"tirumaaliṉ mutal avataaram eṉṟaal? atu eṉṉa? eṉakkut teriyavillai cuvaami!" "The first incarnation of Vishnu? What is that, Sir? I don't know?"
"viʂɳuviṉ mutal avataaram teriyaataa? maccaavataaram." "Don't you know of Thirumal's first incarnation? The Pisces!"
"miiṉaic colluṟiiŋkaɭaa!" "Are you talking about fish?"
"aamaam, appaṉee, aamaam!" "Yes. My dear man! yes!"
"nallaveeɭai, caami! uŋkaɭ kaɳɳee vicittiramaaṉa kaɳɳaayirukkiṟatee! veṟum marattiṉ meelee veetaaɭam terikiṟatu. eṉ veṟuŋkaiyilee maccaavataaram terikiṟatu! oruveeɭai miiṉ peerilee caamiyaarukkuk koɲcam aacai atika oo?" "Very good Sir! Your eyes are something special! On an ordinary tree you can see a vampire and empty hands look like the fish incarnation. Perhaps, Sir, you have an extra fondness for fish!"
"ceeccee! anta maatiriyellaam collaatee appaṉee! atu pooṉaal pookaʈʈum. nammooʈu paʈakilee oru viira caivar vantaaree, avar entap pakkam pooṉaar paarttaayaa?" "No. No. No such thing my dear man! Don't sidetrack me. If you wish, let us drop the matter. But tell me this: do you remember that radical Saiva fellow who came with us in the boat? Do you know where he went?"
"paarkkaamaleṉṉa? paartteeṉ naaṉ kutirai vaaŋkap pooṉa pakkantaaṉ avaru vantaar; uŋkaɭaip paṟṟit tiʈʈik koɳʈee vantaar..." "Do I know? Oh yes. I saw him; he came along the same road when I went to buy the horse. He kept cursing you all along the way ..."
"eṉṉaveṉṟu eṉṉait tiʈʈiṉaar?" "What did he say?"
"uŋkaɭai maṟupaʈiyum anta viira caivar paarttaal uŋkaɭ muṉ kuʈumiyaic ciraittut talaiyai moʈʈaiyaʈittu..." "He said that if he sees you again he would cut off your topknot and shave your head and ..."
"okoo! anta veelai kuuʈa avarukkut teriyumaa?" "Ah! Does he know the barber's trade?"
"uŋkaɭ tirumeeṉiyiluɭɭa naamattaiyellaam aɻittu viʈʈut tiruniiṟṟaip puuci viʈuvaaraam!" "He said that he would wipe out all the namam marks of your sect from your body and cover you with ashes!"
"appaʈiyaaṉaal avaraik kaʈʈaayam naaṉ paartteeyaaka veeɳʈum; avarukku enta uur eṉṟu uṉakkut teriyumaa?" "Is that so? Then, I must surely meet him. Do you know the name of his home-town?"
"avarukku puɭɭirukkum veeɭuur eṉṟu avaree coṉṉaaruŋka!" "He said that it was Pullirukum Vellur, Sir."
"anta viira caivaraip pooyp paarttuviʈʈuttaaṉ maṟu kaariyam.appaṉee! nii eŋkee pookap pookiṟaay? oruveeɭai niiyum anta vaɻi varap pookiṟaayoo?" "Everything else must wait till I meet that zealot! My good man, where are you going now? Will you be coming in that direction?"
"illai, illai naaṉ etaṟkaaka aŋkee varukiṟeeṉ?. tirumpik koɭɭiʈattait taaɳʈik kaʈampuurukkuttaaṉ pookiṟeeṉ. illaaviʈʈaal ejamaaṉar eṉ kaɳɳaip piʈuŋki viʈa maaʈʈaaraa?" "No. No. Why should I go there? I have to cross the Kollidam and go back to Kadamboor. Won't the master pluck my eyes out, if I don't show up?"
"appaʈiyaaṉaal, uʈaṉee tirumpu atoo paʈaku puṟappaʈap pookiṟatu!" "If that is so, go quickly my man! See, the ferry-boat is about to leave."
iʈumpaṉkaari tirumpip paarttapootu, aaɻvaarkkaʈiyaaṉ kuuṟiyatu uɳmai eṉṟu terintatu; paʈaku puṟappaʈum taruvaayil iruntatu. Idumban Kari looked back; what Azlvar-adiyan said was true. The ferry-boat was about to leave.
"cari, caamiyaaree! naaṉ pookiṟeeṉ" eṉṟu colliviʈʈup paʈakut tuṟaiyai nookki viraintu ceṉṟaaṉ iʈumpaṉkaari. "That's fine Sir. I shall leave immediately." After uttering these words he started walking quickly down the embankment, towards the ferry landing.
paati vaɻiyil oru taʈavai tirumpip paarttaaṉ. ataṟkuɭ aaɻvaarkkaʈiyaaṉ oru vintaiyaaṉa kaariyam ceytiruntaaṉ. maɭamaɭaveṉṟu anta araca marattiṉ miitu paayntu eeṟik kiɭaikaɭ aʈarntirukkum iʈata tukkup pooy viʈʈaaṉ. aakaiyaal iʈumpaṉkaariyiṉ kaɳɳooʈʈattil avaṉ viɻavillai. He looked back once, when he was half way down. By then Azlvar-adiyan had done a strange thing: he quickly climbed the ficus tree on the shore and reached the topmost branches of that huge tree. Idumban Kari could not see him.
iʈumpaṉkaari natiyiṉ paricil tuṟaiyai aʈaintaaṉ. paʈakooʈʈikaɭil oruvaṉ, "akkaraikku varukiṟaayaa, appaa?" eṉṟu keeʈʈaaṉ. Idumban reached the ferry landing and stopped. "Are you coming back to the other shore?" asked a boatman.
"illai, aʈutta paʈakil varap pookiṟeeṉ; nii poo!" eṉṟaaṉ iʈumpaṉkaari. "No. I shall come in the next ferry. You can go," said Idumban Kari.
"aʈee! ivvaɭavutaaṉaa? nii varukiṟa veekattaip paarttuviʈʈu allavaa paʈakai niṟuttiṉeeṉ!" eṉṟu colli oʈakkaaraṉ kool pooʈʈu oʈattai natiyil celuttiṉaaṉ. "Ah! Is that all? The way you came running down, I thought you wanted to catch this boat!" The boatman pushed his long pole into the water; the ferry slid into the deeps currents.
itaṟkuɭ aracamarattiṉ naʈumatti varaiyil eeṟi naṉṟaaka maṟaintu uʈkaarntu koɳʈa tirumalai, "okoo! naaṉ niṉaittatu cariyaakap pooyiṟṟu. ivaṉ paʈakil eeṟavillai; tirumpittaaṉ varappookiṟaaṉ. vanta piṟaku entap pakkam pookiṟaaṉ eṉṟu paarkka veeɳʈum. ivaṉuʈaiya kaikaɭ maccahasta muttirai kaaʈʈiyatai naaṉ naṉṟaakap paartteeṉ. ataṉ poruɭ eṉṉa? miiṉ! miiṉ! miiṉ ciṉṉam etaik kuṟikkiṟatu? aa! miiṉ paaɳʈiyaṉuʈaiya koʈiyil poṟittatallavaa? oruveeɭai, aahaahaa!.. appaʈiyum irukkumoo! paarkkalaam! ciṟitu poṟumaiyuʈaṉee iruntu paarkkalaam. poṟuttavar puumi aaɭvaar, poŋkiyavar kaaʈaaɭvaar... aaṉaal, intak kaalattil puumi aaɭvataik kaaʈʈilum kaaʈu aaɭvatee meelaaṉatu eṉṟu tooṉṟukiṟatu! aaṉaalum poṟuttup paarkkalaam!.." ivvitam aracamarattilirunta aruvamaaṉa veetaaɭattiṉiʈam tirumalai collik koɳʈiruntaaṉ. By now Azlvar-adiyan had climbed into the thickest branches of the tree and completely concealed himself. Aha! I thought correctly! This fellow did not go in that boat. He is going to come back. I must see where he goes and what he does next. I clearly saw his hands make the sign of the fish. What does it mean? Fish! Fish! Fish ...! What is represented by the sign of the fish? Ah! isn't the fish a symbol on the Pandiya flag? Perhaps, ha! ... Could it be so? Let us wait ... Let me wait with some patience. The patient ones shall inherit the fields; the hasty will get the forest! But these days it seems better to inherit the forest than the field. Anyway let me be patient... Azlvar-adiyan shared these thoughts with the invisible vampire in the tree.
viraivil avaṉ etirpaarttapaʈiyee naʈantatu; paʈaku iʈumpaṉkaariyai eeṟṟik koɭɭaamalee ceṉṟatu.iʈumpaṉkaari natikkaraiyiliruntapaʈi araca marattaʈiyai uṟṟu uṟṟup paarttaaṉ. piṟaku naalaa ticaikaɭilum tuɭaavip paarttaaṉ. aaɻvaarkkaʈiyaaṉ eŋkumillaiyeṉpatai naṉku terintu koɳʈu tirumpi atee aracamarattaʈikku vantu ceerntaaṉ. iṉṉuma oru taʈavai cuṟṟumuṟṟum naṉṟaayp paarttu viʈʈu anta marattaʈiyileeyee uʈkaarntu koɳʈaaṉ. etaiyoo, allatu yaaraiyoo etirpaarppavaṉ pool avaṉuʈaiya kaɳkaɭ naalaapuṟamum cuɻaṉṟu nookkik koɳʈiruntaṉa. aaṉaal, marattiṉ meelee maʈʈum avaṉ aɳɳaantu paarkkavillai. paarttiruntaalum tirumalai naṉṟaakat tam tirumeeṉiyai maṟaittuk koɳʈiruntapaʈiyaal marattiṉ meel avaṉ uʈkaarntiruppatu iʈumpaṉkaarikkut terintiraatu. Soon, his expectations came true. The ferry-boat left without Idumban Kari. Idumban looked up at the ficus tree from where he stood. His eyes searched in all directions. After making sure that Azlvar-adiyan was nowhere in sight he came back to the same spot under the tree. His eyes searched around once again before he sat down. His eyes kept looking hither and thither as if searching for someone or something. But, he did not look up into the branches of the tree. Even if he had looked up he would not have spotted Azlvar-adiyan easily for Thirumalai had hidden himself well.
cumaar oru naaɻikai neeram ivvitam ceṉṟatu. tirumalaikkuk kaalkaɭ maratup pookat toʈaŋkiṉa. iṉi veku neeram marattiṉ meel irukka muʈiyaateṉṟu tooṉṟiyatu. iʈumpaṉoo, marattaʈiyiliruntu eɻuntirukkum vaɻiyaakat tooṉṟavillai. tappittup poovatu eppaʈi! evvaɭavu jaakkirataiyaaka marattiṉ maṟupakkattil iṟaŋkiṉaalum eetaavatu cattam keeɭaamal iraatu! keeʈʈaal iʈumpaṉkaari uʈaṉee paarttu viʈuvaaṉ. avaṉoo iʈuppil oru kuuriya koʈuvaaɭaic cerukkik koɳʈiruntaaṉ. atait taṉ peeril avaṉ pirayookikka maaʈʈaaṉ eṉpatu eṉṉa niccayam? About an hour passed in this fashion. Azlvar-adiyan's legs began turning numb. He could not remain hidden amidst the tree branches much longer. Idumban Kari did not appear as if he was about to leave soon. How to escape? However carefully he descended on the other side of the tree, he was sure to make some noise. Idumban Kari would surely see him. He had a sharp knife thrust in his waistband. What guarantee that he would not use it?
veeṟu eṉṉataaṉ ceyvatu? peey picaacaip pool payaŋkaramaakac cattamiʈʈuk koɳʈu iʈumpaṉiṉ meeleeyee kutikkalaamaa? kutittaal taṉṉai veetaaɭam eṉṟu niṉaittuk koɳʈu avaṉ payattiṉaal muurccaiyaʈaintu viɻalaam allavaa? allatu tappittu oʈap paarkkalaam allavaa! accamayam taaṉum tappi oʈiviʈalaam!... ivvitam tirumalai eɳɳiya camayattil, avaṉuʈaiya cootaṉai muʈivaʈaiyum eṉat tooṉṟiyatu. What can I do? Shall I make horrible noises like a ghost or demon and jump down on him? If I jump like that he may think that the vampire is attacking him and fall down in a faint or run away. I can then run away and escape! ... When Thirumalai Nambi Azlvar-adiyan was considering these options, it seemed as if his patience would be rewarded.
or aaɭ teṉmeeṟkiliruntu, ataavatu kuʈantaic caalai vaɻiyaaka vantu koɳʈiruntaaṉ. avaṉukkaakattaaṉ iʈumpaṉkaari ittaṉai neeramaayk kaattirukkiṟaaṉ eṉṟu tirumalaiyiṉ uɭɭuɳarcci kuuṟiyatu. A man could be seen walking up from the south west i.e., the Kudanthai road. Thirumalai's intuition warned that Idumban Kari was waiting for that man. Soon, his intuition proved to be right.
putu aaɭ vantataip paarttatum a camarattaʈiyil uʈkaarntirunta iʈumpaṉ eɻuntu niṉṟaaṉ. vantavaṉ, muṉṉaal iʈumpaṉ ceyta camikɲaiyaic ceytaaṉ. ataavatu oru viritta puṟaŋkaiyiṉ meel iṉṉoru viritta kaiyai vaittu, iraɳʈu kaʈʈai viralkaɭai aaʈʈi, macca camikɲai piʈittuk kaaʈʈiṉaaṉ; ataip paartta iʈumpaṉum atee maatiri ceytu kaaʈʈiṉaaṉ. Upon seeing the new man, Idumban Kari stood up. The newcomer made a sign like the one Idumban had made before. That is, he placed the palm of one hand upon the other and wriggled his thumbs, making the sign of the fish. On seeing it, Idumban made a similar sign with his hands.
"uṉ peyar eṉṉa?" eṉṟu vantavaṉ keeʈʈaaṉ. "What is your name?" asked the newcomer.
"eṉ peyar iʈumpaṉkaari; uŋkaɭ peyar?" "My name is Idumban Kari. What is yours, Sir?"
"coomaṉ caampavaṉ!" "Soman Samban."
"uŋkaɭaittaaṉ etirpaarttuk koɳʈirunteeṉ." "I was expecting you, Sir!"
"naaṉum uṉṉait teeʈik koɳʈu taaṉ vanteeṉ." "I too came in search of you."
"naam entat ticaiyil pooka veeɳʈum?" "Where should we go?"
"meeṟkut ticaiyiltaaṉ!" "Westward!"
"evviʈattukku?" "Where?"
"pakaivaṉiṉ paɭɭippaʈaikku!" "To the enemy memorial."
"tiruppuṟampayam arukil..." "Near Thiru-puram-biyam ... "
"iraintu peecaatee! yaar kaatilaavatu viɻap pookiṟatu" eṉṟu collic coomaṉ caampavaṉ naalaapakkamum paarttaaṉ. "Don't talk so loudly. If someone hears it?"
"iŋkee oruvarum illai; muṉṉaaleeyee naaṉ paarttu viʈʈeeṉ." "There is no one here. I looked."
"pakkattil eŋkum oɭintirukkavum iʈamillaiyee?" "If someone is hiding near-abouts?"
"kiʈaiyavee kiʈaiyaatu!" "Impossible."
"appaʈiyaaṉaal puṟappaʈu eṉakku avvaɭavu naṉṟaaka vaɻi teriyaatu. nii muṉṉaal poo! naaṉ caṟṟup piṉṉaal varukiṟeeṉ. aʈikkaʈi niṉṟu naaṉ piṉṉaal varukiṟeeṉaa eṉṟu paarttup poo!" "OK. Let us leave. I don't know the way very well. You go first. I shall follow you at a little distance. Stop and make sure that I am following you as you go on."
"aakaʈʈum. vaɻi nalla vaɻiyalla; kaaʈum meeʈum muɭɭum kallumaayirukkum, jaakkirataiyaakap paarttu naʈantu vara veeɳʈum!" "Fine. It is not a good road. The path is full of stones and thorns. We have to walk through forest and thicket. Watch and walk carefully."
"cari, cari, nii puṟappaʈʈup poo! kaaʈʈu vaɻiyaayiruntaalum yaaraavatu etirppaʈʈaal maṟaintu koɭɭa veeɳʈum terintataa?" "That's alright. You leave now. Even if it is a forest path, hide if you see anyone. Understand?"
"terintatu, terintatu!" "Yes. I know."
iʈumpaṉkaari koɭɭiʈak karaiyooʈu meeṟkut ticaiyai nookkip pooṉaaṉ. avaṉukkuc caṟṟup piṉṉaal coomaṉ caampavaṉum toʈarntu ceṉṟaaṉ. iruvarum kaɳɳukku maṟaiyum varaiyil aaɻ aarkkaʈiyaaṉ marattiṉ meeleeyee iruntaaṉ. ellaavaṟṟaiyum paarttuk koɳʈum keeʈʈuk koɳʈum iruntaaṉ. Idumban Kari started walking westward along the banks of the Kollidam. Soman Samban followed him a little later. Azlvar-adiyan waited on the tree till both were hidden from his sight. He had watched and heard everything!
aahaa! kaalam pollaata kaalam! etirpaaraata kaariyaŋkaɭ ellaam naʈaipeṟukiṉṟaṉa. eetoo oru periya marmamaaṉa kaariyattait terintu koɭɭak kaʈavuɭ aruɭaal cantarppam kiʈaittirukkiṟatu.iṉi nammuʈaiya caamarttiyattaip poṟuttatu viʂayattai aṟivatu. kaʈampuur maaɭikaiyil aṟaikuṟaiyaakattaaṉ terintu koɭɭa muʈintatu. iŋkee appaʈi eemaantu pookak kuuʈaatu. tiruppuṟampayam - paɭɭippaʈaiyeṉṟaal, kaŋka maṉṉaṉ piritiviipatiyiṉ paɭɭippaʈaiyaittaaṉ colliyirukka veeɳʈum. antap paɭɭippaʈaiyaik kaʈʈi nuuṟu varuʂam aakiṟatu. aakaiyaal paaɻaʈaintu kiʈakkiṟatu; cuṟṟilum kaaʈu maɳʈik kiʈakkiṟatu; kiraamamoo caṟṟut tuurattil irukkiṟatu aŋkee etaṟkaaka ivarkaɭ pookiṟaarkaɭ! "Ah ha! These are bad times! All sorts of unexpected things are happening. I think I am going to find out about some mysterious activity. God's grace has given me the opportunity to find out. Now, getting details depends on my resource-fulness. I could not get all the details at Kadamboor. I should not be thwarted like that again. Thiru-puram-biyam Memorial means they are talking about the memorial temple for the Ganga King Prithvi-pathi. It is more than a hundred years since that memorial was built. It is now dilapidated! The forest has encroached and surrounded that area. The village is quite away from the memorial.
inta iraɳʈu peerum maʈʈum peeca veeɳʈiya viʂayamaayiruntaal, iŋkeeyee peecikkoɭɭuvaarkaɭ. kaaʈʈu vaɻiyil oru kaata tuuram pooka veeɳʈiyatillaiyee? aakaiyaal, aŋkee iṉṉum cilarum varap pookiṟaarkaɭ eṉpatu niccayam. etaṟkaaka? piritiviipatiyiṉ paɭɭippaʈaiyaip 'pakaivaṉiṉ paɭɭippaʈai'yeṉṟu ivarkaɭil oruvaṉ colvaaṉeeṉ? piritiviipati yaarukkup pakaivaṉ? aakaa! naam niṉaittatu uɳmaiyaakum poolirukkiṟatee! etaṟkum paarttut terintu koɭɭalaam. ivarkaɭ koɭɭiʈak karaiyooʈu pookiṟaarkaɭ. naam maɳɳik karaiyooʈu pookalaam. maɳɳik karaiyil kaaʈu atika aʈarttiyaayiruntaalum paatakamillai. kaaʈum meeʈum muɭɭum kallum namakku eṉṉa ilaʈciyam? avaitaam nammaik kaɳʈu payappaʈa veeɳʈum!... "Why are these men going there? If the matter is to be discussed between these two, they would have talked about it here itself. There is no need to walk a league along a forest path. I am sure some others are likely to come to that spot. Why? Why did one of them refer to the memorial of King Prithvi-pathi as `The enemy memorial'? Whose enemy was Ganga Prithvi-pathi? Yes! My surmise is likely to be true. Anyway let me find out. They have walked along the banks of the Kollidam. I shall walk along the River Manni. It doesn't matter if the forest is thicker along the banks of the Manni. Why should I bother about the forest and hills or the stones and thorns. I am used to them -- They have to be afraid of me!"
ivvaaṟu eɳɳik koɳʈum vaayooʈu muɳumuɳuttuk koɳʈum tirumalai aracamarattiliruntu iṟaŋkic caṟṟut teṟku nookkipa pooṉaaṉ. maɳɳiyaaṟu vantatu ataṉ karaiyooʈu meeṟku nookki naʈaiyaik kaʈʈiṉaaṉ. jaṉa caɲcaaramillaata aʈarnta kaaʈukaɭiṉ vaɻiyaaka aaɻvaarkkaʈiyaaṉ pukuntu ceṉṟu cuuriyaṉ astamikkum camayattil tiruppuṟampayam paɭɭippaʈaik kooyilai aʈaintaaṉ. Muttering these words and thinking such thoughts, Azlvar-adiyan descended the ficus tree and went a little southward. He came to the banks of the River Manni and started walking west. He walked through forests meeting no one on the way and by about sunset he reached the memorial temple near Thiru-puram-biyam.
