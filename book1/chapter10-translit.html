pattaam attiyaayam Chapter 10
kuʈantai cootiʈar The Astrologer Of Kudanthai
kuʈaku naaʈʈil piṟantu vaɭarnta poṉṉi nati kaṉṉip paruvam kaʈantatum taṉ maɳaaɭaṉaakiya camuttira raajaṉiʈam ceṉṟaʈaiya virumpiṉaaɭ. kaaʈum meeʈum kaʈantu paaṟaikaɭaiyum paɭɭaŋkaɭaiyum taaɳʈik koɳʈu viraintu ceṉṟaaɭ. camuttira raajaṉai neruŋka neruŋka, naayakaṉaik kaaɳap pookiṟoom eṉṟa kutuukalattiṉaal avaɭ uɭɭam vimmi uʈal puurittatu. iṉṉum caṟṟut tuuram ceṉṟaaɭ, kaatalaṉai aɳaittuk koɭɭak karaŋkaɭ iraɳʈu uɳʈaayiṉa. iru karaŋkaɭai virittavaaṟu taavip paayntu ceṉṟaaɭ. aaṉaal uɭɭattil poŋkiya aarva mikutikku iru karaŋkaɭ pootumeṉṟu tooṉṟavillai; avaɭuʈaiya aacaik karaŋkaɭ pattu, irupatu, nuuṟu eṉṟu vaɭarntaṉa. avvaɭavu karaŋkaɭaiyum aavaluʈaṉ niiʈʈik koɳʈu camuttira raajaṉai aɳukiṉaaɭ. River Ponni, born and raised in the Kudagu Hills, after her childhood was past, wished to meet the Ocean King, her chosen husband. She went swiftly, crossing hill and dale, rocky mountain and canyon. As she came closer and closer, the joyous anticipation of meeting her beloved Lord, the Ocean King, made her thrive and grow. She went even further. Two arms grew to embrace the lover. Spreading her arms wide, she leaped and surged forward. Two arms were not sufficient for her ardent enthusiasm. Her loving arms grew into ten, twenty, hundred! Stretching out all these arms in eagerness she neared the Ocean King.
ivvitam aacaik kaɳavaṉai aʈaivataṟkuc ceṉṟa maɳap peɳɳukkuc cooɻa naaʈʈuc cevilit taaymaar ceyta alaŋkaaraŋkaɭtaaṉ eṉṉa? aʈaʈaa! ettaṉai aɻakiya paccaip puʈaivaikaɭai uʈuttiṉaarkaɭ? eppaʈiyellaam vaɳɳa malarkaɭaic cuuʈʈiṉaarkaɭ? evvitamellaam parimaɭa cukantaŋkaɭait tuuviṉaarkaɭ? aahaa! iru karaiyilum vaɭarntirunta puṉṉai maraŋkaɭum kaʈampa maraŋkaɭum rattiṉap puukkaɭaiyum vaaric corinta arumaiyai evvitam varɳippatu? teevarkaɭ poɻiyum puumaariyum itaṟku iɳaiyaakumaa? She was the bride meeting her beloved. Chozla women, her bridesmaids, dressed her in such wondrous ways. They clothed her in the beautiful greens of rice-fields. They decorated her with colorful flowers; and showered her with fragrant woods. How can we describe the enchanting kadamba and punnai trees on both her banks: they covered her with pearls and rubies of flowers.
poṉṉi natiyee! uṉṉaip paarttuk kaɭippaʈaiyaata kaṉṉip peɳ yaartaaṉ irukka muʈiyum! uṉ maɳakkoola aaʈai alaŋkaaraŋkaɭaik kaɳʈu uɭɭam poŋkaata maŋkai yaar irukka muʈiyum? Who would not be overjoyed to cast their eyes upon you, dear Ponni? Which maid will not be beguiled by this, your bridal attire?
kaliyaaɳap peɳɳaic cuṟṟi uuriluɭɭa kaṉṉip peɳkaɭ elloorum cuuɻntu koɭvatupool uṉṉai naaʈip peɳkaɭ vantu kuuʈuvatum iyaṟkaiyee allavaa! It is but natural that attractive young girls should gather around you just as bridesmaids surround a ravishing bride.
poṉṉi taṉ maɳaaɭaṉait taɻuvik koɭɭa aacaiyuʈaṉ niiʈʈum poṟkaraŋkaɭil oṉṟukkuttaaṉ aricilaaṟu eṉṟu peyar! kaaveerikkut teṉ puṟattil mika nerukkattil aricilaaṟu eṉṉum aɻakiya nati amaintirukkiṟatu. appaʈi oru nati iruppatu caṟṟut tuurattil iruntu varukiṟavarkaɭukkuc collit taaṉ teriya veeɳʈum. irupuṟamum aʈarttiyaaka vaɭarntirukkum iṉiya pacumaraŋkaɭ appaʈi annatiyai maṟaittu viʈukiṉṟaṉa. piṟantatu mutalaavatu antappurattai viʈʈu vauiyeeṟi aṟiyaata aracakulak kaṉṉiyeṉṟee aricilaaṟṟaic collalaam. antak kaṉṉi natiyiṉ aɻakukku inta ulakil uvamaiyee kiʈaiyaatu. One of the arms stretched out by Ponni or Cauvery, to reach out for her Lord the Ocean King, is known by the name River Arisil. The beautiful Arisil flows to the south of and very close to River Cauvery. One could not easily view this petite river from a little distance. The groves of trees, growing thickly on both its banks hide the river. One could consider the Arisil as a princess who never stepped out of the enclosed courtyards of a royal palace. There is no comparison to this beautiful virgin river.
nallatu; antappuram eṉṉum eɳɳattai maṟantuviʈʈu neeyarkaɭ nammuʈaṉ aricilaaṟṟai neruŋki varuvaarkaɭaaka. coolaiyaaka neruŋki vaɭarntirunta maraŋkaɭukkiʈaiyee pukuntu varuvaarkaɭaaka! aʈaʈaa itu eṉṉa arumaiyaaṉa kaaʈci? aɻakukku aɻaku ceyvatu poolum amutattukku iṉippu uuʈʈuvatu poolum allavaa irukkiṟatu? Let our readers forget that it is the inner courtyard of a palace and come with us to the banks of the Arisil river. Let them enter these woody groves on its banks. Oh! What an enchanting sight meets our eyes! It seems to add radiance to beauty; to add sweetness to nectar.
cittira vicittiramaakac ceyta aṉṉa vaʈivamaaṉa vaɳɳap paʈakil viiṟṟirukkum inta vaṉitaamaɳikaɭ yaar? avarkaɭil naʈu naayakamaaka, naʈcattiraŋkaɭukkiʈaiyil puuraɳa cantiraṉaip pool eeɻulakaŋkaɭaiyum aaɭap piṟanta raaɳiyaip pool, kaantiyuʈaṉ viɭaŋkum inta naariimaɳi yaar? avaɭukku arukil kaiyil viiɳaiyuʈaṉ viiṟṟirukkum caantacuntari yaar? iṉiya kuralkaɭil icai paaʈi nati veɭɭattuʈaṉ kiita veɭɭamum kalantu perukac ceytu koɳʈu varum intak kantarvap peɳkaɭ yaar? Who are these divine women, seated on the beautifully carved barge, constructed like a floating swan? Who is the radiant jewel among women, seated in their midst, like the full moon among twinkling stars, like a queen born to rule all seven worlds? Who is the gentle damsel seated next to her, playing the veena (lute like instrument)? Who are these heavenly beauties coming down the stream, mixing the pleasant melody of their music with the gurgling noises of the river?
avarkaɭil orutti miiṉaloocaṉi; iṉṉorutti niilaloocaṉi; orutti taamarai mukattaaɭ; iṉṉorutti kamala itaɻ nayaṉattaaɭ; aahaa viiɳaiyai miiʈʈukiṟaaɭee, avaɭuʈaiya kaantaɭai otta viralkaɭ viiɳait tantikaɭil aŋkumiŋkum caɲcarikkum aɻakaip paarttuk koɳʈeeyirukkalaam. One of them had large eyes shaped like darting fish; the other was blue eyed; yet another had a face like the blossoming lotus; another girl had wide dark eyes shaped like the petal of a blue lily. Ah! The girl playing the musical instrument, one could watch forever, her soft fingers dancing over the taunt strings of the veena.
avarkaɭ icaikkum kiitattiṉ iṉimaiyaittaaṉ eṉṉa eṉṟu colluvatu? ataik keeʈpataṟkaaka natiyiṉ veɭɭam kuuʈa allavaa taṉ ocaiyai niṟuttiyirukkiṟatu? natikkarai maraŋkaɭil vaaɻum kiɭikaɭum kuyilkaɭum kuuʈa vaaytiṟavaa mooṉattil aaɻntirukkiṉṟaṉavee! maṉitarkaɭaayp piṟantavarkaɭ, keeʈkum cevi paʈaitta paakkiyacaalikaɭ anta amuta kaaṉattai keeʈʈup paravacam aʈaivatil viyappu eṉṉa? paʈakil varum appeɳkaɭ eṉṉa paaʈukiṟaarkaɭ keeʈkalaam:- What can we say about the sweetness of the song they sang? Even the water in the river seemed to be still as it listened to their melodious music. Even the cuckoos and parrots on the trees listened quietly. We humans, blessed with the fortune of being able to listen, why should we not be enchanted by their song? Let us listen to them: 
	maruŋku vaɳʈu ciṟantu aarppa,
	    maɳippuu aaʈai atupoorttuk
	karuŋkayaṟkaɳ viɻittu olki
	    naʈantaay vaaɻi! kaaveeri !

Walk slowly, walk shyly,
Praise to you, dear Cauvery;
Dress yourself in beautiful clothes:
Fragrant gardens full of humming bees;

	karuŋkayaṟkaɳ viɻittu olki
	    naʈanta ellaam niṉkaɳavaṉ
	tiruntu ceŋkool vaɭaiyaamai
	    aṟinteeṉ vaaɻi kaaveeri !

Open wide, those large dark eyes:
Dancing fish of deep dark seas;
Dancing does not melt a lovers heart:
Like a good monarch's mace of justice;

	puuvar coolai mayilaaʈap
	    purintu kuyilkaɭ icaipaaʈak
	kaamar maalai arukacaiya
	    naʈantaay vaaɻi kaaveeri !

When nightingales sing in deep woods;
And peacocks dance amidst flower groves;
Dance gently, dear Cauvery:
You disturb the garlands on Kama's shoulders.

		kaamar maalai arukacaiya
	     naʈanta vellaam, niṉ kaɳavaṉ
	naama veeliṉ tiṟaŋkaɳʈee
	     aṟinteeṉ vaaɻi! kaaveeri!

The garlands at his waist are all asway;
What caused it? The very name of your dear Lord:
An expert spear like dart;
Walk slowly, walk shyly,
Praise to you, dear Cauvery.
inta amutat tamiɻp paaʈalkaɭai eŋkeeyoo keeʈʈirukkiṟoomallavaa? aam, cilappatikaarattil uɭɭa varip paaʈalkaɭ ivai. eṉiṉum, intap peɳkaɭ paaʈumpootu muṉ eppootumillaata vaṉappum kavarcciyum peṟṟu viɭaŋkukiṉṟaṉa. ivarkaɭ poṉṉi natiyiṉ arumait tooɻikaɭ poolum! ataṉaaleetaaṉ ivvaɭavu paravacamaaka uɳarcci tatumpap paaʈukiṟaarkaɭ. aʈaʈaa! paaʈalum paɳɳum paavamum eppaʈik kalantu iɻaintu kuɻaintu ivarkaɭuʈaiya kuraliliruntu amuta veɭɭamaakap poɻikiṉṟaṉa? paaʈʈaavatu, paɳɳaavatu, kaaṉamaavatu, icaiyaavatu! atellaam oṉṟumillai. itu eetoo maayak kalai! paaʈukiṟavarkaɭ, keeʈpavarkaɭ ellaaraiyum pittup piʈikkac ceyyum mantira vittai! Where have we heard this delightful Tamil poem? Oh yes, is it not from The Silappadikaram (an ancient poetical work)? The poem seems even more enchanting and delightful when these maids sing it. Perhaps they are the maids of honor in River Arisil's court? Or is this the magic of some sorcerer? It entices every beholder.
paʈaku mitantu koɳʈee vantu, maraŋkaɭ ciṟitu iʈaivaui tanta oʈattuṟaiyil otuŋki niṟkiṟatu. iraɳʈu peɳkaɭ iṟaŋkukiṟaarkaɭ; avarkaɭil orutti eeɻulakattukkum raaɳi eṉat takum kampiirat tooṟṟamuʈaiya peɳmaɳi. iṉṉorutti viiɳait tantikaɭil viralkaɭai oʈʈi iṉṉicai eɻuppiya naŋkai. iruvarum aɻakikaɭ eṉṟaalum oruvaruʈaiya aɻakukkum iṉṉoruvaruʈaiya aɻakukkum mikka veeṟṟumai iruntatu. orutti centaamarai malariṉ kampiira cauntariyam uʈaiyavaɭ. iṉṉorutti kumuta malariṉ iṉiya aɻakai uʈaiyavaɭ. orutti puuraɳa cantiraṉ; iṉṉorutti kaalaip piṟai. orutti aaʈum mayil; iṉṉorutti paaʈum kuyil. orutti intiraaɳi; iṉṉorutti maṉmataṉiṉ kaatali. orutti veekavaahiṉiyaaṉa kaŋkaanati; iṉṉorutti kuɻaintu nauintu cellum kaaveeri. The barge floated down gently till it stopped near a landing amidst the woods. Two maidens are getting down. One is that dignified lady, comparable to a queen of the seven worlds; the other is her companion who was creating the enchanting harmony while playing the veena. Though both are beautiful, there was a difference between the two of them. One has the dignity of a lotus blossom in full bloom. The other has the pleasantness of the night-time water lily. One is the radiant full moon; the other is the young crescent of late evening. One is the dancing peacock; the other is a singing nightingale. One is a queen of the Gods; her friend is Rathi, the beloved of Cupid. She is the fast flowing turbulent Ganga. Her companion is the gentle Cauvery.
vaacakarkaɭai meelum canteeka aaraaycci nilaiyil viʈʈu vaikkaamal ivarkaɭ iruvarum yaar eṉṟu colli viʈukiṟoom. kampiirat tooṟṟamuʈaiya kaŋkaitaaṉ cuntara cooɻa maṉṉariṉ celvap putalvi kuntavai. carittirattil raajaraajaṉ eṉṟu pukaɻ peṟṟa aruɭmoɻivarmaṉiṉ cakootari. araciɭaŋ kumari eṉṟum iɭaiya piraaʈʈi eṉṟum makkaɭaal pooṟṟappaʈʈa maataraci. cooɻa raajyattiṉ makooṉṉatattiṟku aʈikooliya tamiɻp perum celvi. raajaraajaṉuʈaiya putalvaṉ raajeentiraṉai eʈuttu vaɭarttu viiraati viiraṉaayum maṉṉaati maṉṉaṉaayum aakkiya tiirap peɳmaɳi. Without further ado, without leaving our readers in further suspense, let me introduce these two ladies. The lady with a certain dignity in her posture is Kundavai, the beloved daughter of Emperor Sundara Chozla. She is the elder sister to Arulmozli Varma who would later attain historic fame as Rajaraja I. She is the lady revered by the people as the young royal princess, the Younger Pirati. A distinguished daughter of the Tamils, she laid the foundations for the greatness of the Chozla Empire. She is the capable lady who will raise and mould the ambitions of Rajaraja's son Rajendra and make him the greatest of monarchs in South Indian history.
iṉṉorutti, kuntavaip piraaʈʈiyuʈaṉ irukkum paakkiyattai naaʈi vanta koʈumpaaɭuurc ciṟṟaracar kulap peɳ. piṟkaalattil, carittirattileeyee iɳaiyillaata paakkiyavatiyaakap pookiṟavaɭ. iṉṟu aʈakkamum iṉimaiyum caantamum uruveʈuttu viɭaŋkukiṟavaɭ. Her friend is Vanathi, a noblewoman from the clan of Kodumbalur chieftains. She came to enjoy the fortune of being a part of Kundavai's household. In the future she would attain greatness unparalleled in history. She is now a young girl, full of modesty and a pleasant gentleness.
inta iru maŋkaimaarkaɭum paʈakiliruntu karaiyil iṟaŋkiṉaarkaɭ. kuntavai maṟṟa tooɻip peɳkaɭaip paarttu, "niiŋkaɭ iŋkeeyee iruŋkaɭ. oru naaɻikai neerattil tirumpi vantu viʈukiṟoom!" eṉṟaaɭ. After both had alighted from the barge, Kundavai turned to her other companions and said, "You can all wait here. We will be back in an hour."
antat tooɻip peɳkaɭ aṉaivarum teyvat tamiɻnaaʈʈil paṟpala ciṟṟaracarkaɭiṉ araɳmaṉaiyil piṟanta aracakumaarikaɭ. kuntavai teevikkut tooɻiyaaka iruppataip peṟaṟkarum peeṟaakak karutip paɻaiyaaṟai araɳmaṉaikku vantavarkaɭ.ippootu taŋkaɭil oruttiyai maʈʈum aɻaittuk koɳʈu kuntavaip piraaʈʈi karaiyil iṟaŋki 'pooyviʈʈu viraivil varukiṟeeṉ' eṉṟatum avarkaɭuʈaiya kaɳkaɭil eemaaṟṟamum acuuyaiyum tooṉṟiṉa. All those maids, who were her companions, were noblewomen from the households of the aristocrats and chieftains in the Chozla nation. They had come to the Pazlayarai Palace, considering it a fortune to be included as maids of honor to Kundavai. Now, when Kundavai went ashore with just one girl from among them, saying, "I'll come after a while," a certain envy and disappointment could be discerned in their eyes.
karaiyil kutirai puuʈʈiya ratam oṉṟu cittamaayiruntatu. "vaaṉati! ratattil eeṟikkoɭ!" eṉṟaaɭ kuntavai taṉ tooɻiyaip paarttu. vaaṉati eeṟiyatum taaṉum eeṟi koɳʈaaɭ ratam veekamaayc ceṉṟatu. A horse drawn chariot awaited their pleasure on the shore. "Vanathi! Get into the chariot," said Kundavai. When both were seated, the chariot moved swiftly.
"akkaa! naam eŋkee pookiṟoom? eṉakkuc collalaamaa?" eṉṟu vaaṉati keeʈʈaaɭ. "Akka! Where are we going? Can you tell me?" asked Vanathi.
"collaamal eṉṉa? kuʈantai cootiʈar viiʈʈukkup pookiṟoom!" eṉṟaaɭ kuntavai. "Why not? We are going to the house of the Kudanthai Astrologer," said Kundavai.
"cootiʈar viiʈʈukku etaṟkaakap pookiṟoom, akkaa? eṉṉattaip paṟṟik keeʈpataṟkaaka?" "Why are we going to an astrologer's house, Akka? What do we have to ask him?"
"veeṟu etaṟku? uṉṉaip paṟṟik keeʈpataṟkaakattaaṉ. cila maata kaalamaaka nii ippaʈip piramai piʈittavaɭ poolum, uʈal melintum varukiṟaayaa? uṉakku eppootu piramai niiŋki uʈampu teeṟum eṉṟu keeʈpataṟkaakattaaṉ!" "What else? To ask about you! For some months now you seem to be a girl possessed, lost in some dream world, losing weight. We are going to ask him if you will be cured of these fantasies and become healthy once again. We are going to ask him all these things!"
"akkaa! taŋkaɭukku rompap puɳɳiyamuɳʈu; eṉakku uʈampukku oṉṟumillai. eṉṉaip paṟṟik keeʈpataṟkaakap pooka veeɳʈaam tirumpi viʈuvoom!" "Akka! Let good fortune be showered upon you! There is nothing wrong with my health. We don't have to ask him anything about me. Let's go back."
"illaiyaʈi, ammaa, illai! uṉṉaip paṟṟik keeʈpataṟkaaka illai; eṉṉaip paṟṟik keeʈpataṟkaakattaaṉ pookiṟeeṉ." "No, my dear no! I am not going to ask him anything about you. I shall ask him about me!"
"taŋkaɭaip paṟṟi eṉṉa keeʈʈut terintu koɭɭap pookiṟiirkaɭ? joociyariʈam keeʈʈu eṉṉa terintu koɭɭap pookiṟiirkaɭ." "What do you have to ask about yourself? What is there to ask an astrologer about yourself?"
"eṉakkuk kaliyaaɳam aakumaa? allatu kaʈaici varaiyil kaṉṉip peɳɳaakavee iruntu kaalam kaɻippeeṉaa eṉṟu keeʈkap pookiṟeeṉ." "I am going to ask him if I would ever be married; or am I going to spend all my life a virgin maid!"
"akkaa!itaṟku joociyariʈam pooyk keeʈpaaṉeeṉ! taŋkaɭuʈaiya maṉataiyee allavaa keeʈka veeɳʈum? taaŋkaɭ talaiyai acaikka veeɳʈiyatutaaṉ! imaya malai mutalaavatu kumari muṉai varaiyil uɭɭa aimpattaaṟu teecattu raajaakkaɭum pooʈʈi pooʈʈuk koɳʈu oʈi varamaaʈʈaarkaɭaa? eeṉ, kaʈal kaʈanta teecaŋkaɭileeyiruntellaamkuuʈa varuvaarkaɭee! taŋkaɭaik kai piʈikkum peeṟu enta viira raajakumaaraṉukkuk koʈuttu vaittirukkiṟatoo? atait taaŋkaɭ allavaa tiirmaaṉikka veeɳʈum!" "Akka! Why ask the astrologer about it? You have to ask your own heart! Just nod you gracious head: princes from all the fifty-six kingdoms from Cape Comorin to the Himalayas would come running, vying with each other. Why, even princes from across the seas from Yavana and Kadaram will come. I wonder which of these princes will have the fortune of receiving your hand. You have to determine that!"
"vaaṉati nii colvatellaam uɳmai eṉṟu vaittuk koɳʈaalum ataṟku oru taʈai irukkiṟatu. entat teecattu araca kumaaraṉaiyaavatu maɳam purintu koɳʈaal naaṉ avaṉuʈaiya naaʈʈukkup pooka veeɳʈi varumallavaa? eṉakku intap poṉṉi nati paayum cooɻa naaʈʈiliruntu veeṟoru naaʈʈukkup pookap piʈikkaveeyillaiyaʈi! veeṟu naaʈʈukkup poovatillai eṉṟu naaṉ capatam eʈuttuk koɳʈirukkiṟeeṉ..." "Vanathi, even if I accept all that you say as truth, there is one obstacle. If I marry any one of these princes from an alien nation, I will have to go with him to his kingdom. My dear, I have a great dislike to go away from this delightful Chozla Kingdom where the Ponni flows! I have taken an oath that I will not go to another kingdom from here.. ..."
"atu oru taʈaiyaakaatu; taŋkaɭai maɳam purintu koɭɭum enta raajakumaaraṉum taŋkaɭ kaalil viɻuntu kiʈakkum aʈimaiyaakavee iruppaaṉ. iŋkeeyee irukka veeɳʈum eṉṟaalum iruntu viʈʈup pookiṟaaṉ." "That is no obstacle! Any prince that marries you will stay at your feet as your slave. If you order him to stay here he will remain here."
"aakaa! eliyaip piʈittu maʈiyil vaittuk kaʈʈik koɭvatupool veeṟu teecattu raajakumaaraṉai nam uurileeyee koɳʈu vaittuk koɭɭavaa colkiṟaay? ataṉaal eṉṉeṉṉa tollaikaɭ ellaam viɭaiyum teriyumaa?" "Oh dear Lord! That will be like picking up a rat and tieing it up in your waistband! How can we retain an alien prince in our lands? Do you know the consequences of such activity?"
"eppaʈiyum peɳɳaayp piṟantavarkaɭ oru naaɭ kaliyaaɳam ceytu koɳʈutaaṉee tiira veeɳʈum?" "Anyway, whatever you say, a person born as a woman will one day have to be married. Is that not so?"
"appaʈi oru caastirattilum colliyirukkavillaiyaʈi, vaaṉati! auvaiyaaraip paar! avaɭ eṉṟum kaṉṉi aɻiyaata kaviicuvariyaakap pala kaalam jiivittirukkavillaiyaa?" "No law says that my dear. Vanathi, think of the Lady Avvaiyar. Did she not live for a long time as a queen among poets but a virgin maid?"
"auvaiyaar iɭam piraayattileeyee kaʈavuɭiṉ varattiṉaal kiɻaviyaakap pooṉavaɭ taaŋkaɭ ataippool aakavillaiyee?" "Because of a boon from the Gods Avvaiyar became an old maid in her young age. You are not like that."
"cari appaʈik kaliyaaɳam ceytu koɭvatu eṉṟu puṟappaʈʈaal anaataiyaaṉa cooɻa naaʈʈu viiraṉ oruvaṉaiyee naaṉ maɳantu koɭveeṉ. attakaiyavaṉukku raajyam iraatu. eṉṉai aɻaittuk koɳʈu veeṟu oru teecattukkup pooka veeɳʈum eṉṟu colla maaʈʈaaṉ. iŋkeeyee cooɻa naaʈʈileeyee iruntu viʈuvaaṉ..." "Well if I do have to be married, I think I shall choose an orphan Chozla warrior for my consort. That fellow will not have any kingdom. He will not demand that I go to another nation. He will remain here itself, in this Chozla country."
"akkaa! appaʈiyaaṉaal intac cooɻa naaʈʈai viʈʈup pookamaaʈʈiirkaɭee?" "Akka, does it mean that you will never leave these Chozla Territories?"
"oru naaɭum pooka maaʈʈeeṉ corkka lookattukku eṉṉai araciyaakkuvataakac coṉṉaalum pookamaaʈʈeeṉ." "Never will I leave. Even if they offer to crown me as a queen of the heavens, I will not leave!"
"iṉṟaikkuttaaṉ eṉ maɳam nimmati aʈaintatu." "Now I am quite happy."
"atu eṉṉaʈi?" "How is that?"
"niiŋkaɭ veeṟu naaʈʈukkup pooṉaal, naaṉum uŋkaɭooʈu vantee tiira veeɳʈum. uŋkaɭai viʈʈup pirintirukka eṉṉaal muʈiyaatu. atee camayattil intac cooɻa vaɭanaaʈʈaip pirintu pookavum eṉakku maṉamillai." "If you move to another kingdom, I will have to follow you. I cannot be parted from you. At the same time I have no heart to leave this bountiful Chozla land!"
"kaliyaaɳam aaṉaal nii pirintu pooyttaaṉee tiira veeɳʈum?" "If you are married, won't you have to leave?"
"naaṉ kaliyaaɳamee ceytu koɭɭap poovatillai, akkaa!" "I am not going to marry anyone, Akka."
"aʈiyee! eṉakku ceyta upateecamellaam eŋkee pooyiṟṟu?" "My dear girl! What happened to all the advise you were giving me just now?"
"taŋkaɭai poolavaa naaṉ?" "Am I like you?"
"aʈi kaɭɭi! eṉakku ellaam teriyum. eṉ kaɳɳil maɳɳait tuuvalaam eṉṟaa paarkkiṟaay? uṉakkuc cooɻa naaʈʈiṉ miitu apimaaṉam oṉṟum kiʈaiyaatu. nii aacai vaittirukkum cooɻa naaʈu, vaaɭum veelum taaŋki iiɻanaaʈʈukku yuttam ceyya allavaa pooyirukkiṟatu? uṉ antaraŋkam eṉakkut teriyaatu eṉaṟaa niṉaittaay?" "You deceiver! I know everything! Are you trying to throw dust in my eyes? You have no great love for this Chozla land. The Chozla nation you love is bearing sword and spear and has gone to Lanka to conduct a campaign! You think that I do not know your secrets?"
"akkaa! akkaa! naaṉ avvaɭavu maʈamati uʈaiyavaɭaa? cuuriyaṉ eŋkee? kaalaip paṉittuɭi eŋkee? cuuriyaṉuʈaiya naʈpukkup paṉittuɭi aacaippaʈʈaal eṉṉa payaṉ?" "Akka! Akka! Am I so foolish? Where is the sun? and where is the morning dewdrop? What is the use, if a dewdrop wishes for the friendship of the fiery sun?"
"paṉittuɭi ciṟiyatu taaṉ! cuuriyaṉ periyatu, pirakaacamaaṉatu taaṉ! aaṉaalum paṉittuɭi appaʈippaʈʈa cuuriyaṉaic ciṟaippaʈuttit taṉakkuɭ vaittirukkiṟatoo, illaiyoo?" "The dewdrop is small! And the sun is mighty and bright! Still, does not the dewdrop impound the bright sunshine in itself? Tell me?"
vaaṉati uṟcaakamum aarvamum niṟainta kuralil, "appaʈiyaa colkiṟiirkaɭ! paṉittuɭikuuʈac cuuriyaṉai aʈaiyalaam eṉṟu colkiṟiirkaɭaa?" eṉṟaaɭ. piṟaku tiʈiireṉṟu maṉac coorvu vantu viʈʈatu. "paṉittuɭi aacaippaʈukiṟatu; cuuriyaṉaiyum ciṟaippiʈikkiṟatu. aaṉaal palaṉ eṉṉa? ciṟitu neerattukkellaam cariyaaṉa taɳʈaṉai aʈaikiṟatu.veyilil ularntu, irunta iʈam teriyaamal maṟaikiṟatu!" Vanathi now replied in a voice filled will cheer and eagerness: "Are you saying that? Can a mere dewdrop attain the sun?" Then suddenly she felt depressed. "The dewdrop wishes to do so; in fact it enslaves the sunray! But what use? Within a short time it is severely punished. It dries up in the bright sunlight and disappears without a trace!"
"atu tavaṟu, vaaṉati! paṉittuɭiyiṉ aacaiyaik kaɳʈu cuuriyaṉ taṉṉuʈaṉ paṉittuɭiyai aikkiyappaʈuttik koɭkiṟaaṉ. taṉ aacaikkukanta paṉittuɭip peɳ piṟa puruʂar kaɳɳil paʈak kuuʈaatu eṉṟu avaṉ eɳɳam. iravu vantatum maṟupaʈiyum vauiyee viʈʈu viʈukiṟaaṉ. maṟainta paṉittuɭi maṟupaʈiyum vantu utikkiṟatu allavaa?" "That is wrong, Vanathi. The sun recognizes the love of the dewdrop and merges her into himself. He thinks that his beloved maid, the dewdrop, should not be seen by other men. He lets her out again after darkness. Doesn't the hidden dewdrop appear again every morning?"
"akkaa! itellaam eṉṉait teeṟṟuvataṟkaakac colkiṟiirkaɭ." "Akka, you are telling me all these stories to cheer me up."
"appaʈiyaaṉaal uṉ maṉatil oru kuṟai irukkiṟatu eṉṟu collu. ittaṉai naaɭ 'illavee illai' eṉṟu caatittaayee? ataṉaaltaaṉ kuʈantai joociyariʈam pookiṟeeṉ." "So! You do say that you need cheering up. There is some sadness in your heart. All these days you maintained that `Nothing is wrong'. That is why I am going to the Astrologer of Kudanthai."
"eṉ maṉatil kuṟaiyiruntaal, ataip paṟṟik keeʈkac cootiʈariʈam pooy eṉṉa payaṉ?" eṉṟu kuuṟi vaaṉati perumuucceṟintaaɭ. "If there is a sadness in my heart, what is the use of asking an astrologer about it?" sighed Vanathi.
kuʈantai cootiʈariṉ viiʈu anta nakariṉ oru muulaiyil kaaɭi kooyilukku arukil oru taṉitta iʈattil iruntatu. kuʈantai nakarukkuɭ pukaamaleeyee nakaraic cuṟṟik koɳʈu ratam anta viiʈu ceṉṟu aʈaintatu. ratacaarati ratattait taŋku taʈaiyiṉṟi aŋkee oʈʈik koɳʈu pooyc ceerttataip paarttaal, avaṉ ataṟku muṉ palamuṟai aŋkee ratam oʈʈik koɳʈu ceṉṟirukka veeɳum eṉṟu tooṉṟiyatu. The house of the Kudanthai Astrologer was situated in the outskirts of that city, in an isolated spot, besides a Kali temple. The chariot did not enter the city, but went around and reached that house. From the way the charioteer drove his chariot without any hesitation or doubt, it appeared that he had driven there several times before.
viiʈʈu vaacalil cootiʈarum avaruʈaiya ciiʈar oruvarum aayattamaakak kaattiruntaarkaɭ. cootiʈar mikka pakti mariyaataiyuʈaṉ vantavarkaɭai varaveeṟṟu upacarittaar. The Astrologer and one of his disciples waited readily outside the house. The Astrologer welcomed his visitors with great reverence and courtesy.
"perumaaʈʈi! kalaimakaɭum tirumakaɭum oruruvaay vanta taayee! varaveeɳum! varaveeɳum! inta eeɻaiyiṉ kuʈicai ceyta paakkiyam, maṟumuṟaiyum taaŋkaɭ ikkuʈicaiyait teeʈi vantiirkaɭ!" eṉṟaar. "Great lady! A reincarnation of both Kalaimagal (Goddess of Learning) and Thirumagal (Goddess of Wealth)! Welcome. Welcome. It is the fortune of my humble abode that you have come in search of me once again!"
"cootiʈaree! inta veeɭaiyil taŋkaɭait teeʈik koɳʈu veeṟu yaarum iŋku varamaaʈʈaarkaɭ allavaa?" eṉṟaaɭ kuntavai. "Mr. Astrologer! I hope that nobody else will come in search of you at this time?" asked Kundavai.
"varamaaʈʈaarkaɭ, taayee! ippootellaam eṉṉait teeʈi atikam peer varuvatee illai. ulakattil kaʂʈaŋkaɭ atikamaakum pootu taaṉ cootiʈarkaɭait teeʈi makkaɭ atikamaaka varuvaarkaɭ. ippootu taŋkaɭuʈaiya tirut tantai cuntarac cooɻariṉ aaʈciyil, kuʈikaɭukkuk kaʂʈam eṉpatee kiʈaiyaatu. elloorum cuka caukkiyaŋkaɭuʈaṉ cakala campattukkaɭaiyum peṟṟuc cantooʂamaaka vaaɻkiṟaarkaɭ. eṉṉait teeʈi eeṉ varukiṟaarkaɭ?" eṉṟaar cootiʈar. "No one will come Thaye! Not many people come in search of me these days. Only when troubles in the world increase will people seek the astrologer. Nowadays, under the rule of your esteemed father -- The Emperor Sundara Chozla -- the people have no troubles at all! They possess all the comforts and luxuries and live life with happiness. Who will come in search of me?"
"appaʈiyaaṉaal eṉakku eetoo kaʂʈam vantiruppataṉaal taaṉ ummait teeʈi vantirukkiṟeeṉ eṉṟu collukiṟiiraakkum!" "Do you mean to say that I have some troubles and therefore, I have come to consult you?" asked Kundavai.
"illai, perumaaʈʈi! illavee illai! navanitiyum koɻikkum paɻaiyaaṟai maṉṉariṉ tiruk kumaarikkuk kaʂʈam vantatu eṉṟu entak kuruʈaṉtaaṉ colluvaaṉ! ulakattil makkaɭukkuk kaʂʈamee illaamaṟ pooyviʈʈapaʈiyaal, inta eeɻaic cootiʈaṉukku maʈʈum kaʂʈam vantirukkiṟatu; ivaṉai maʈʈum kavaṉippaar illai. aakaiyaal, inta eeɻaiyiṉ kaʂʈattait tiirppataṟkaaka ampikaiyaip pool vantirukkiṟiirkaɭ. taayee! kuʈicaikkuɭɭee vantaruɭa veeɳʈum. iŋkeeyee taŋkaɭai niṟutti vaittiruppatu naaṉ ceyyum apacaaram!" eṉṟu joociyar camatkaaramaakap peeciṉaar. "No. Great lady, no. Which blind fool will say that the beloved daughter of Sundara Chozla who possesses immense wealth, has any troubles? Since the people have no troubles, this poor astrologer is cast into misfortune. Nobody takes care of him. Therefore you have come here like a Goddess with solicitude for my troubles. Please enter and grace my humble house. Look at me, talking while I detain you here on the doorstep!" said the resourceful Astrologer.
ratacaaratiyaip paarttuk kuntavai, "ratattaik kooyilukkuc camiipam koɳʈu pooy aalamarattiṉ niɻalil niṟutti vai!" eṉṟaaɭ. Kundavai turned to the charioteer and said, "Take the chariot to the shade of the banyan tree behind the temple and wait."
piṟaku cootiʈar vaɻikaaʈʈi muṉ cella, kuntavaiyum vaaṉatiyum avviiʈʈukkuɭɭee ceṉṟaarkaɭ. Kundavai and Vanathi entered the Astrologer's house as he led them inside.
cootiʈar tam ciiʈaṉaip paarttu, "appaṉee! vaacalil jaakkirataiyaaka niṉṟu koɳʈiru; tappit tavaṟi yaaraavatu vantaalum uɭɭee viʈaatee!" eṉṟu eccarittaar. The Astrologer turned to his disciple and said warningly, "Wait outside and guard the entrance carefully. Even if accidentally someone happens to come by, do not let them enter."
aracakumaariyai varaveeṟpataṟku ukantataakac cootiʈariṉ kuuʈam aɻaku ceyyappaʈʈiruntatu. cuvaril oru maaʈattil ampikaiyiṉ paʈam alaŋkarikkappaʈʈu viɭaŋkiyatu. amaruvataṟku iraɳʈu piiʈaŋkaɭ cittamaayiruntaṉa. kuttuviɭakku erintatu, aŋkumiŋkum koolaŋkaɭ polintaṉa. raacic cakkaraŋkaɭ pooʈʈa palakaikaɭum olaiccuvaʈikaɭum cuṟṟilum iraintu kiʈantaṉa. The parlor in the Astrologer's house was decorated to receive his royal visitors. In an alcove in the wall was a decorated figure of the Goddess Devi. Two seats were made ready for seating the visitors. A tall metal lamp was lit. The floor gleamed with intricate kolam decorations. Several tablets and palm leaf charts with the scribbles and symbols of the astrologer's trade were scattered about.
peɳmaɳikaɭ iruvarum piiʈaŋkaɭil amarnta piṟaku, cootiʈarum uʈkaarntaar. After both ladies were seated, the Astrologer sat down.
"ammaɳi! vanta kaariyam iṉṉateṉpatait tayavu ceytu colli aruɭa veeɳum!" eṉṟaar. "Madam, please tell me why you have come?"
"joociyaree! ataiyum taŋkaɭ jootiʈattileeyee paarttut terintu koɭɭak kuuʈaataa?" eṉṟaaɭ kuntavai. "Sir! Can you not find out even that by your astrology?" asked Kundavai.
"aakaʈʈum taayee!" eṉṟu kuuṟic jootiʈar kaɳɳai muuʈik koɳʈu ciṟitu neeram eetoo mantiram japittuk koɳʈiruntaar. "So be it, Thaye!" said the Astrologer and closed his eyes. He muttered some incantations.
piṟaku kaɳɳait tiṟantu paarttu, "koomaaʈʈi, intak kaṉṉip peɳɳiṉ jaatakam paṟṟik keeʈpataṟkaakavee iṉṟu mukkiyamaaka vantirukkiṟiirkaɭ. avvitam teevi paraacaktiyiṉ aruɭ colkiṟatu uɳmaitaaṉaa?" eṉṟaar. After a while looked at them saying "Lady, you have come here today, mainly to ask about the horoscope of this young girl. That is what the grace of the Goddess reveals to me."
"aahaa! piramaatam! uŋkaɭuʈaiya caktiyai eṉṉaveṉṟu colvatu? aam joociyaree! intap peɳɳaip paṟṟik keeʈkat taaṉ vanteeṉ. oru varuʂattukku muṉpu ivaɭ paɻaiyaaṟai araɳmaṉaikku vantaaɭ. vantu eʈʈu maata kaalam mikak kutuukalamaay iruntu vantaaɭ. eṉ tooɻiyarukkuɭɭee ivaɭtaaṉ cirippum viɭaiyaaʈʈum kalakalappumaaka iruntu vantaaɭ. naalumaatamaaka ivaɭukku eṉṉavoo neerntirukkiṟatu. aʈikkaʈi coorntu pookiṟaaɭ.piramai piʈittaaṟpool irukkiṟaaɭ; cirippaiyee maṟantu viʈʈaaɭ. uʈampukku oṉṟumillai eṉkiṟaaɭ. ivaɭ peṟṟoorkaɭ naaɭaikku vantu keeʈʈaal, eṉṉa maṟumoɻi colvateṉṟee teriyavillai..." "Aha! Remarkable! How can we praise your powers? Yes sir! I have come to ask about this girl. She came to the Pazlayarai Palace about a year ago. For the first eight months she was very cheerful and happy. Amongst my companions she was the most joyous, most filled with playful laughter. For the last four months, something has happened to her. Often, she seems depressed. She seems to live in some abstract world. She has forgotten her laughter. She says that nothing is wrong with her health. If her parents come and ask me tomorrow, what reply can I give?"
"taayee! koʈumpaaɭuur koomakaɭiṉ celvap putalvi taaṉee ivar? ivaruʈaiya peyar vaaṉati taaṉee?" eṉṟaar jootiʈar. "Thaye! Is she not the beloved daughter of the Lord of Kodumbalur? Is her name Vanathi Devi?" asked the Atrologer.
"aamaam; umakku ellaam terintirukkiṟatee!" "Yes; you seem to know everything."
"inta araciɭaŋkumariyiṉ jaatakam kuuʈa eṉṉiʈam irukkiṟatu. ceerttu vaittirukkiṟeeṉ! caṟṟup poṟukka veeɳum!" eṉṟu colliviʈʈu, jootiʈar pakkattilirunta oru paɻaiya peʈʈiyait tiṟantu ciṟitu neeram puraʈʈiṉaar. piṟaku, atiliruntu oru jaatakak kuṟippai eʈuttuk kavaṉamaayp paarttaar. "I even have the horoscope of this young Lady. I have it in my collection. Please wait a little." The Astrologer opened an old casket by his side and searched awhile. He then picked up a palm leaf notation of a horoscope and peered at it.
