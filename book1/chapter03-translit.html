muuṉṟaam attiyaayam Chapter 3
viɳɳakarak kooyil Vinnagara Temple
cila camayam ciṟiya nikaɻcciyiliruntu periya campavaŋkaɭ viɭaikiṉṟaṉa.vantiyatteevaṉ vaaɻkkaiyil attakaiya oru ciṟiya nikaɻcci ippootu neerntatu. caalaiyoorattilee niṉṟu paɻuveeʈʈaraiyariṉ parivaaraŋkaɭ poovatai vantiyatteevaṉ paarttuk koɳʈiruntaaṉ allavaa? avaṉ niṉṟa iʈattukkuc caṟṟut tuurattileeyee avaṉuʈaiya kutirai niṉṟu koɳʈiruntatu. Sometimes trivial incidents lead to events of great significance. One such incident now occurred to Vallavarayan Vandiya Devan. Remember, he was standing by the roadside to watch the procession of the Pazluvoor Lord and his entourage? His horse stood a little away form him.
paɻuveeʈʈaraiyariṉ aaʈkaɭilee kaʈaiciyaakac ceṉṟa cilariṉ paarvai akkutirai miitu ceṉṟatu. Some of the men walking towards the end of the Pazluvoor retinue sighted the horse.
"aʈee! intak kurutaiyaip paaraʈaa!" eṉṟaaṉ oruvaṉ. "Dear chap! Look at this mule!" said one of them.
"kurutai eṉṟu collaateeʈaa! kutirai eṉ col!" eṉṟaaṉ iṉṉoruvaṉ. "Don't say mule. Say mare," corrected the other.
"uŋkaɭ ilakkooɳa aaraaycci irukkaʈʈum; mutalil atu kurutaiyaa allatu kaɻutaiyaa eṉṟu terintu koɭɭuŋkaɭ!" eṉṟaaṉ iṉṉoruvaṉ veeʈikkaip piriyaṉ. "Set the semantic research aside! First of all find out if it is a donkey or a mule!" said a third man.
"ataiyum paarttu viʈalaamaʈaa!" eṉṟu collik koɳʈu, anta aaʈkaɭil oruvaṉ kutiraiyai aɳuki vantaaṉ. ataṉ meel taavi eeṟa muyaṉṟaaṉ. eeṟap paarkkiṟavaṉ taṉ ejamaaṉaṉ alla eṉpatai anta aṟivuk kuurmaiyuɭɭa kutirai terintu koɳʈatu. anta veeṟṟu maṉitaṉai eeṟṟik koɭɭa maaʈʈeeṉ eṉṟu muraɳʈu piʈittatu! "Let's check it out!" said yet another as he approached the horse. He tried to jump on its back, but the intelligent horse realized that this was not its master; it refused to let him mount.
"itu pollaata kutiraiyaʈaa! itaṉ peeril naaṉ eeṟak kuuʈaataam! paramparaiyaaṉa aracakulattavaṉtaaṉ itaṉ meel eeṟalaamaam. appaʈiyeṉṟaal taɲcaavuur muttaraiyaṉ tirumpi vantutaaṉ itaṉ meel eeṟaveeɳʈum!" eṉṟu avaṉ camatkaaramaayp peeciyataik keeʈʈu maṟṟa viirarkaɭ nakaittaarkaɭ. "Hey! This is a troublesome horse. It will not let me mount! Perhaps only a prince of well established lineage can ride it! Well then, Tanjore Muthuaraya will have to come back!" His friends laughed at this witticism.
eeṉeṉṟaal, taɲcaavuur muttaraiyar kulam nacittup pooy nuuṟu aaɳʈukaɭ aakiviʈʈaṉa. ippootu cooɻarkaɭiṉ pulikkoʈi taɲcaavuuril paṟantu koɳʈiruntatu. Tanjore Muthuaraya's dynasty had vanished a hundred years ago! Now, a flag bearing the tiger symbol of the Chozlas flew over Tanjore.
"kutiraiyiṉ eɳɳam avvitam irukkalaam. aaṉaal, eṉṉaik keeʈʈaal, cettup pooṉa taɲcaavuur muttaraiyaṉaik kaaʈʈilum uyirooʈu irukkiṟa taaɳʈavaraayaṉee meel eṉpeeṉ!" eṉṟaaṉ maṟṟoru viiraṉ. "The horse may think so. However, if you ask me I would say that a fully alive Thandavaraya is better than a dead Muthuaraya from Tanjore," said another undaunted man named Thandavaraya.
"taaɳʈavaraayaa! uṉṉai eeṟṟik koɭɭa maṟukkum kutirai nijak kutiraitaaṉaa eṉṟu paarttuviʈu! oruveeɭai, perumaaɭiṉ tirunaaɭukku vanta poykkaal kutiraiyaayiruntaalum irukkalaam!" eṉṟaaṉ maṟṟoru parikaacap piriyaṉ. "Thandavaraya, find out if this pesky horse that wont let you come near is really a horse! Perhaps it is a dummy dance-horse come in for the temple festival," said another prankster.
"ataiyum cootittup paarttu viʈukiṟeeṉ" eṉṟu collik koɳʈu kutirai miitu eeṟappooṉa taaɳʈavaraayaṉ ataṉuʈaiya vaalai muṟukkiṉaaṉ. rooʂamuɭɭa akkutirai uʈaṉee piṉṉaŋkaalkaɭai naalu taʈavai viciṟi utaittuviʈʈu oʈʈam piʈittatu. "Ok, let's examine it," saying this Thandavaraya twisted the horse's tail. The proud horse kicked its hind legs several times and began running amok.
"kurutai oʈukiṟataʈaa! nijak kurutai taaṉaʈaa!" eṉṟu avviirarkaɭ kuuccaliʈʈu, "uy! uy!" eṉṟu kooʂittu, oʈukiṟa kutiraiyai meelum viraʈʈiṉaarkaɭ!. "Hey, the mule is running away! It really is a mare!" shouted the man and with cries of "Ooi, ooi," they began to chase the horse even further.
kutirai, tirunaaɭ kuuʈʈattukkiʈaiyee pukuntu oʈiṟṟu. jaṉaŋkaɭ ataṉ kaalaʈiyil mitipaʈaamaliruppataṟkaakap paraparappuʈaṉ aŋkum iŋkum nakarntu koɳʈaarkaɭ. appaʈiyum avarkaɭil cilar utaipaʈʈu viɻuntaarkaɭ. kutirai neṟikeʈʈu veṟi koɳʈu oʈiyatu. The horse began running helter skelter amidst the festive crowd. People trying to avoid its hooves moved away in a hurry. Even so, some of them were kicked down. The horse ran wild.
ivvaɭavum vantiyatteevaṉ kaɳɳetiree ati ciikkirattil naʈantu viʈʈatu. avaṉuʈaiya mukat tooṟṟattiliruntu kutirai avaṉuʈaiya kutirai eṉpatai aaɻvaarkkaʈiyaaṉ kaɳʈu koɳʈaaṉ. All this happened within a few seconds right in front of Vandiya Devan. From the expression on Vallavarayan's face, Azlvar-adiyan, the Vaishnava, surmised it to be his horse.
"paarttaayaa, tampi! antap paɻuvuurt taʈiyarkaɭ ceyta veelaiyai! eṉṉiʈam nii kaaʈʈa vanta viirattai avarkaɭiʈam kaaʈʈuvatutaaṉee!" eṉṟu kuttik kaaʈʈiṉaaṉ. "Thambi, did you see the work of those Pazluvoor brutes? What happened to the boldness that you showed in front of me? Could you not show your valor against them?" he pointed out.
vantiyatteevaṉukku aattiram pottuk koɳʈu vantatu. eṉiṉum pallaik kaʈittuk koɳʈu poṟumaiyaik kaʈaippiʈittaaṉ. paɻuvuur viirarkaɭ peruŋkuuʈʈamaayiruntaṉar. avvaɭavu peeruʈaṉ oree camayattil caɳʈaikkup poovatil poruɭ illai. avarkaɭ ivaṉuʈaṉ caɳʈai pooʈuvataṟkaakak kaattirukkavum illai. kutirai oʈiyataip paarttuc cirittu viʈʈu, avarkaɭum viraintu meelee naʈantaarkaɭ. Vandiya Devan felt a piercing outrage. Yet, he grit his teeth and remained patient. The Pazluvoor men were a large group. There was no point in confronting the whole lot. Neither did those men wait for him to pick a fight. Laughing over their pranks with the horse they had quickly marched ahead.
kutirai pooṉa ticaiyai nookki vantiyatteevaṉ ceṉṟaaṉ. atu koɲca tuuram oʈiviʈʈut taaṉaakavee niṉṟu viʈum eṉṟu avaṉukkut teriyum. aakaiyaal ataip paṟṟi avaṉ kavalaippaʈavillai. paɻuveeʈʈaraiyariṉ akampaavam piʈitta aaʈkaɭukku putti kaṟpikka veeɳʈum eṉṟa eɳɳam avaṉ uɭɭattil aɻuttamaakap patintatu. Vandiya Devan started walking in the direction in which the horse had gone. He knew that his horse would run for a while and then stop. So he did not worry about it. He felt that one day he should settle the score with those arrogant men of Pazluvoor and the idea became firmly etched in his mind.
puɭiyantooppukku appaal, jaṉa caɲcaaramillaata iʈattil kutirai cookamee vaʈivaaka niṉṟu koɳʈiruntatu. vantiyatteevaṉ ataṉ arukil ceṉṟatum, kutirai kaṉaittatu.'eeṉ eṉṉai viʈʈup pirintu ceṉṟu, intac caŋkaʈattukku uɭɭaakkiṉaay?' eṉṟu anta vaayillaap piraaɳi kuṟaikuuṟuvatu pool ataṉ kaɭaipput toṉittatu. vantiyatteevaṉ ataṉ mutukait taʈʈic caantappaʈuttalaaṉaaṉ. piṟaku atait tiruppi aɻaittuk koɳʈu caalaip pakkam nookki vantaaṉ. His horse stood in a clearing beyond the tamarind grove, wearing a saddened expression. As he approached it, the horse neighed; the speechless creature seemed to say, "Why did you part from me and subject me to these travails?" He quietened the horse by patting its back and then led it back towards the road.
tiruviɻaak kuuʈʈattil iruntavarkaɭ palarum avaṉaip paarttu, "inta muraʈʈuk kutiraiyai eeṉ kuuʈʈattil koɳʈu vantaay, tampi! ettaṉai peerai atu utaittut taɭɭiviʈʈatu?" eṉṟaarkaɭ. Several persons of the festive crowd asked, "Thambi why did you bring this unruly horse here? See how it has kicked us?"
"intap piɭɭai eṉṉa ceyvaaṉ? kutiraitaaṉ eṉṉa ceyyum? antap paɻuveeʈʈaraiyariṉ muraʈʈu aaʈkaɭ allavaa ippaʈic ceytuviʈʈaarkaɭ?" eṉṟu iraɳʈoruvar camaataaṉam coṉṉaarkaɭ. Others pacified them with words like "What will this young man do? What could the horse have done? We have to blame those Pazluvoor rogues for this mishap."
aaɻvaarkkaʈiyaaṉ iṉṉamum caalaiyil kaattuk koɳʈu niṉṟaaṉ. "iteetaʈaa caṉiyaṉ? ivaṉ nammai viʈamaaʈʈaaṉ poolirukkiṟatee!" eṉṟu eɳɳi vantiyatteevaṉ mukattaic cuɭukkiṉaaṉ. Azlvar-adiyan waited at the roadside. Vandiya Devan frowned. What nuisance is this... It seems as if this fellow will not let go.
"tampi! nii entap pakkam pookap pookiṟaay?" eṉṟu aaɻvaarkkaʈiyaaṉ keeʈʈaaṉ. "Thambi, which way are you going?" asked Azlvar-adiyan.
"naaṉaa? koɲcam meeṟkup pakkam ceṉṟu, piṟaku teṟkup pakkam tirumpi, ciṟitu kiɻakkup pakkam vaɭaittuk koɳʈu pooy appuṟam teṉ meeṟkup pakkam pooveeṉ!" eṉṟaaṉ vantiyatteevaṉ. "Me? I plan to go a little ways west and then a bit to the south; then go around east to travel southwest."
"ataiyellaam naaṉ keeʈkavillai iṉṟu raattiri eŋkee taŋkuvaay eṉṟu keeʈʈeeṉ." "I am not asking all that. I meant, where do you plan to stay tonight?"
"nii etaṟkaaka ataik keeʈkiṟaay?" "Why do you ask?"
"oruveeɭai kaʈampuurc campuvaraiyar araɳmaṉaiyil nii taŋkuvataayiruntaal, eṉakku aŋkee oru veelai irukkiṟatu.." "Perhaps, if you were planning to stay at the fort of Kadamboor Sambuvaraya, I have an assignment to be done there ..."
"uṉakku mantiratantiram teriyumaa, eṉṉa? naaṉ kaʈampuur araɳmaṉaikkup pookiṟeeṉ eṉpatai eppaʈi aṟintaay?" "What! Do you know magic or witchcraft? How did you know that I was going to Kadamboor Palace?" asked Vandiya Devan.
"itil eṉṉa aticayam? iṉṟaikkup pala uurkaɭiliruntum pala viruntaaɭikaɭ aŋkee varukiṟaarkaɭ. paɻuveeʈʈaraiyarum avar parivaaramum aŋkeetaaṉ pookiṟaarkaɭ." "What is so surprising about it? Tonight, several dignitaries from various places are going there. Lord Pazluvoor and his retainers are also going there."
"meyyaakavaa?" eṉṟu vantiyatteevaṉ taṉ viyappai vauiyiʈʈaaṉ. "Is that true?" said a surprised Vandiya Devan.
"meyyaakattaaṉ! atu uṉakkut teriyaataa, eṉṉa? yaaṉai, kutirai, pallakku, parivaʈʈam, ellaam kaʈampuur araɳmaṉaiyaic ceerntavaitaaṉ. paɻuveeʈʈaraiyarai etirkoɳʈu aɻaittup pookiṉṟaṉa. paɻuveeʈʈaraiyar eŋkee pooṉaalum inta mariyaataiyellaam avarukku naʈaipeṟṟee aaka veeɳʈum." "Yes, it is true. The elephant, the horses, liveried footmen and other honors were from Kadamboor. They came to receive Lord Pazluvoor; all such protocols are always observed wherever he goes."
vantiyatteevaṉ mauṉa yoocaṉaiyil aaɻntaaṉ. paɻuveeʈʈaraiyar taŋkumiʈattil taaṉum taŋkuvateṉpatu eɭitil kiʈaikkak kuuʈiya vaayppu alla. anta maaperum viiraruʈaṉ paɻakkam ceytukoɭɭa oru cantarppam kiʈaittaalum kiʈaikkalaam. aaṉaal avaruʈaiya muraʈʈup parivaaraŋkaɭuʈaṉ eeṟpaʈʈa aṉupavam iṉṉum avaṉukkuk kacantu koɳʈiruntatu. Vandiya Devan became thoughtful. The opportunity to stay in the same lodgings as Lord Pazluvoor was not easy to come by. He may even chance to make the acquaintance of that esteemed warrior. However, the experience with the unruly retainers of the Lord still rankled bitterly.
"tampi! eṉakku oru utavi ceyvaayaa?" eṉṟu aaɻvaarkkaʈiyaaṉ irakkamaaṉa kuralil keeʈʈaaṉ. Azlvar-adiyan interrupted these thoughts in a begging voice, "Thambi, will you do me a favor?"
"uṉakku naaṉ ceyyakkuuʈiya utavi eṉṉa irukka muʈiyum? intap pakkattukkee naaṉ putiyavaṉ." "How can I help you? I am new to these parts."
"uṉṉaal muʈiyakkuuʈiya kaariyattaiyee colveeṉ. iṉṟiravu eṉṉaik kaʈampuur araɳmaṉaikku aɻaittuk koɳʈu poo!" "It is a task that is possible for you. Take me along to Kadamboor Fort tonight."
"etaṟkaaka? aŋkee yaaraavatu viiracaivar varukiṟaaraa? civaṉ periya teyvamaa? tirumaal periya teyvamaa? eṉṟu vivaatittu muʈivu kaʈʈap pookiṟiirkaɭaa?" "Why? Is some fanatic Saiva coming there? Are you going to debate about the greatness of Shiva and Thirumal to conclude who is the greater God?"
"illai, illai caɳʈai piʈippatee eṉ veelai eṉṟu niṉaikka veeɳʈaam. iṉṟiravu kaʈampuur maaɭikaiyil periya viruntu naʈaipeṟum. viruntukku piṟaku kaɭiyaaʈʈam, caamiyaaʈʈam, kuravaik kuuttu ellaam naʈaipeṟum. kuravaik kuuttup paarkka veeɳʈum eṉṟu eṉakku aacai! "No. No. Did you think that getting into arguments was my only occupation? Tonight there will be a lavish banquet at Kadamboor. After the feasting there would be several entertainments: music, pantomimes, miracle plays, gypsy dancers and mystic oracles. I wish to see the gypsy dance and hear the oracle."
"appaʈiyiruntaalum naaṉ uṉṉai eppaʈi aɻaittup pooka muʈiyum?" "Even then, how can I take you along?"
"eṉṉai uṉ paɳiyaaɭ eṉṟu coṉṉaal pookiṟatu." "Say that I am your servant."
vantiyatteevaṉukku muṉṉaal eeṟpaʈʈa canteekam valuppaʈʈatu. Vandiya Devan felt that his earlier doubts were confirmed.
"anta maatiri eemaaṟṟu moocaʈikkellaam nii veeṟu yaaraiyaavatu paarkka veeɳʈum. uṉṉaip pooṉṟa paɳiyaaɭaṉ eṉakkut teevaiyillai, coṉṉaal nampavum maaʈʈaarkaɭ. meelum, nii coṉṉataiyellaam yoocittup paarttaal eṉṉaiyee iṉṟu kooʈʈaikkuɭ viʈuvaarkaɭoo eṉṟa canteekam uɳʈaakiṟatu." "Find someone else for such deception and fraud. I do not need a servant like you. Anyway no one will believe it; from what you say, I doubt if they would even let me into the fort tonight."
"appaʈiyaaṉaal, nii kaʈampuurukku aɻaippup peṟṟu pookavillaiyeṉṟu collu!" "Aha! That means you are not going to Kadamboor upon invitation!"
"oruvakaiyil aɻaippu irukkiṟatu, campuvaraiyar makaṉ kantamaaṟaveeɭ eṉṉuʈaiya uṟṟa naɳpaṉ. intap pakkam vantaal avarkaɭuʈaiya araɳmaṉaikku avaciyam varaveeɳumeṉṟu eṉṉaip palamuṟai aɻaittirukkiṟaaṉ." "Well, I have an invitation of sorts. Kandamaran, the son of Lord Sambuvaraya, is my close friend. He has invited me several times to come and stay at his palace if I come to these parts."
"ivvaɭavutaaṉaa? appaʈiyaaṉaal uṉ paaʈee iṉṟaikkuk koɲcam tiɳʈaaʈʈamaattaaṉ irukkum!" "Is that all! Then even your situation is kind of doubtful tonight!"
iruvarum ciṟitu neeram mauṉamaakap pooyk koɳʈiruntaarkaɭ. Both kept walking onwards, silently, for a while.
"eeṉ eṉṉai iṉṉum toʈarntu varukiṟaay?" eṉṟu vantiyatteevaṉ keeʈʈaaṉ. "Why are you still following me?" asked Vandiya Devan.
"antak keeɭviyaiyee naaṉum tiruppik keeʈkalaam; nii eeṉ eṉṉait toʈarkiṟaay? uṉ vaɻiyee poovatutaaṉee?" "I was about to ask you the same question. why are you following me? Why don't you go your own way?"
"vaɻi teriyaata kuṟṟattiṉaal taaṉ. nampi! nii eŋkee pookiṟaay? oruveelai kaʈampuurukkuttaaṉaa?" "Well it is because of not knowing which way to go. Where are you going? Perhaps to Kadamboor?"
"illai; niitaaṉ eṉṉai aŋku aɻaittup pooka muʈiyaatu eṉṟu colliviʈʈaayee? naaṉ viɳɳakarak kooyilukkup pookiṟeeṉ." "No. You said you cannot take me there. I am going to the Vinnagara temple."
"viiranaaraayaɳap perumaaɭ cannitikkuttaaṉee?" "To the presence of the deity Veera-narayana-perumal?"
"aam." "Yes."
"naaṉum anta aalayattukku vantu perumaaɭaic ceevippataṟku virumpukiṟeeṉ." "I too would like to go to that temple and worship that perumal."
"oruveeɭai viʂɳu aalayattukku nii vara maaʈʈaayoo eṉṟu paartteeṉ. paarkka veeɳʈiya kooyil; taricikka veeɳʈiya canniti. iŋkee iicuvara muṉikaɭ eṉṟa paʈʈar, perumaaɭukkuk kaiŋkariyam ceytu varukiṟaar avar periya makaaṉ." "I thought that you would perhaps not come to a Vishnu temple. It is a temple that should be seen; a deity worthy to behold. The priest Eshwara Munigal offers services at the temple. He is a venerable man."
"naaṉum keeɭvippaʈʈirukkiṟeeṉ oree kuuʈʈamaayirukkiṟatee! kooyilil eetaavatu viceeʂa uṟcavam uɳʈoo?" "I too have heard this. But, it is very crowded. Is there some special celebration at the temple today?"
"aam; iṉṟu aaɳʈaaɭ tirunaʈcattiram.aaʈip patiṉeʈʈaam perukkooʈu aaɳʈaaɭiṉ tirunaʈcattiramum ceerntu koɳʈatu; ataṉaaltaaṉ ivvaɭavu koolaakalam. tampi! aaɳʈaaɭ paacuram eetaavatu nii keeʈʈirukkiṟaayaa?" "Yes, today is the day of the Saint Andal. It is also the Aadi Festival of Padhinettam Perukku. All these festivities are because of this. Thambi, have you heard any of the invocative poems of Andal?"
"keeʈʈatillai." "I have not heard any."
"keeʈkaatee! ataik kaatiṉaaleeyee keeʈkaatee!" "Don't. Don't ever listen to her poems!"
"eeṉ avvaɭavu vaiʂamyam?" "Why this hatred?"
"vaiʂamyamum illai; virootamum illai; uṉṉuʈaiya naṉmaikkuc coṉṉeeṉ. aaɳʈaaɭiṉ iṉiya paacurattaik keeʈʈu viʈʈaayaaṉaal, appuṟam vaaɭaiyum veelaiyum viʈʈeṟintu viʈʈu eṉṉaip pool niiyum kaɳɳaṉ meel kaatal koɳʈu viɳɳakara yaattirai kiɭampi viʈuvaay!" "It is not hatred. Nor is it enmity. It is for your benefit that I say it. If you ever happen to hear any of the melodious psalms of Andal, you will drop your swords and spears; like me, you too will fall in love with Kannan and start upon a pilgrimage to all the Vishnu temples."
"uṉakku aaɳʈaaɭ paacuraŋkaɭ teriyumaa? paaʈuvaayaa?" "Do you know any of these psalms of Andal? Can you sing them?"
"cila teriyum; veetam tamiɻ ceyta nammaaɻvaar paacuraŋkaɭil cila teriyum. perumaaɭ cannitiyil paaʈap pookiṟeeṉ veeɳumaaṉaal keeʈʈuk koɭ! itoo koovilum vantu viʈʈatu!" itaṟkuɭ uɳmaiyileeyee viiranaaraayaɳap perumaaɭ kooyilai avarkaɭ neruŋki vantuviʈʈaarkaɭ. "I know some. I also know some of the poems of Nammazlvar who translated the Vedas (scriptures) into Tamil. I am going to sing some of them at the shrine. If you wish, you can listen. Here comes the temple." By now they had reached the Vinnagara temple.

Paranthaka Chozla I, a grandson of Vijayala Chozla, had won the title Conqueror of Madurai and Lanka. He was the monarch who established the foundations of the Chozla Empire. He achieved historical fame because he covered the roof of the temple at Chidambaram with gold. Besides titles like Jewel among Chozlas, Greatest of Warriors, he also had the honored name of Veera Narayana.
paraantakaṉuʈaiya kaalattil vaʈakkee iraʈʈai maɳʈalattu raaʂʈirakuuʈa maṉṉarkaɭ valimai peṟṟu viɭaŋkiṉaarkaɭ. maaṉiya keeʈattiliruntu avarkaɭ paʈaiyeʈuttu varak kuuʈumeṉṟu paraantakaṉ etirpaarttaaṉ. eṉavee, taṉatu mutaṟputalvaṉaakiya iɭavaracaṉ iraajaatittaṉai oru periya caiṉyattuʈaṉ tirumuṉaippaaʈi naaʈʈil irukkac ceytaaṉ. During Paranthaka's times, the Rashtrakutas in the north were powerful monarchs. He expected them to come from Manyaketa (roughly present day Maharashtra) and invade the territories of the Tamils. Therefore he stationed a huge army, led by his eldest son Prince Raja-aditya, in the Thirumunaipadi Territories north of the Cauvery.

Prince Raja-aditya had a brilliant idea while his large numbers of soldiers waited in idleness, with nothing to do. Using them he wished to fulfil a major undertaking for the benefit of the population. Enormous quantities of flood waters flowing in the Kollidam river were going wastefully into the sea. Wishing to make use of these waters, he made his soldiers build a huge, spreading reservoir. In honor of his distinguished father he named it the Veera Narayana Lake. He then established the town of Veera-narayana-puram on its shores and also built a Vinnagara temple. The Sanskrit term Vishnu Griha meaning Abode of Vishnu, became Vinnagara in Tamil. Does not Sri Narayana, i.e., Vishnu recline upon the primeval waters in sublime meditation? Therefore, it was customary in those days to build Vinnagaras on lake shores so that the deity would protect the reservoir. Therefore, the Prince constructed a temple for Veera-Narayana at Veera-narayana-puram.
