patiṉooṟaam attiyaayam Chapter 11
tiʈumpiraveecam Sudden Entry
innaaɭil kumpakooɳam eṉṟa peyaraal aaŋkila akaraatiyileekuuʈa iʈam peṟṟirukkum nakaram, nammuʈaiya katai naʈanta kaalattil kuʈantai eṉṟum kuʈamuukku eṉṟu vaɻaŋkappaʈʈu vantatu. puɳɳiya stala makimaiyaiyaṉṟi, 'kuʈantai cootiʈaraalum atu pukaɻpeṟṟiruntatu. kuʈantaikkuc caṟṟut tuurattil teṉ meeṟkut ticaiyil cooɻarkaɭiṉ iʈaikkaalat talainakaramaaṉa paɻaiyaaṟai, vaaṉai aɭaaviya araɳmaṉai maaʈaŋkaɭuʈaṉum aalaya koopuraŋkaɭuʈaṉum kampiiramaakak kaaʈci aɭittuk koɳʈiruntatu. The town known as Kumbakonam, was known as Kudanthai or Kudamooku during the times of our story. Besides the virtue of being a pilgrimage center, it was also famous because of the Astrologer of Kudanthai. A little distance to the south of Kudanthai, a majestic view of the interim capital of the Chozla's, Pazlayarai, with its temple towers and palace turrets reaching the skies, could be glimpsed.
paɻaiyaaṟai araɳmaṉaikaɭil vacitta araca kulattiṉar aṉaivaruʈaiya jaatakaŋkaɭaiyum kuʈantai cootiʈar ceekarittu vaittiruntaar. appaʈic ceekarittu vaittirunta jaatakaŋkaɭaip puraʈʈittaaṉ koʈumpaaɭuur iɭavaraci vaaṉatiyiṉ jaatakattai avar kaɳʈeʈuttaar. ciṟitu neeram jaatakattai uṟṟup paarttuk koɳʈirunta piṟaku, cootiʈara vaaṉatiyiṉ mukattai eeṟiʈʈup paarttaar. tirumpa jaatakattaip paarttaar. ippaʈi maaṟṟi maaṟṟip paarttuk koɳʈiruntaaree tavira, vaayait tiṟantu oṉṟum collukiṟa vaɻiyaik kaaɳavillai. The Astrologer of Kudanthai had collected the horoscopes of all the royal family living at the Pazlayarai Palace. He had searched amidst this collection and found the horoscope of Lady Vanathi of Kodumbalur. After peering at the palm leaf notation of the horoscope for a while, he stared at the face of Vanathi; then again looked at the notation. He was peering and staring one after the other, but did not open his mouth to utter a single word!
"eṉṉa, joociyaree! eetaavatu collap pookiṟiiraa, illaiyaa?" eṉṟu kuntavai teevi keeʈʈaaɭ. With a little impatience Kundavai asked, "What Sir? Are you going to say something or not?"
"taayee! eṉṉattaic colvatu? eppaʈic colvatu? muṉ oru taʈavai taṟceyalaaka inta jaatakattai eʈuttup paartteeṉ. eṉṉaaleeyee nampa muʈiyavillai; ippaʈiyum irukka muʈiyumaa eṉṟu canteekappaʈʈu vaittu viʈʈeeṉ. ippootu intap peɳɳiṉ tirumukattaiyum inta jaatakattaiyum ceerttup paarkkumpootu, tikaikka veeɳʈiyirukkiṟatu!" "Thaye! What can I say? How can I say it? I had studied this horoscope once before and could not believe it myself. I doubted if anything could be like it and kept it aside. Now, when I behold the divine face of this Lady and see her horoscope at the same time, I am astounded!"
"tikaiyum! tikaiyum! pootumaaṉavarai tikaittuviʈʈu piṟaku eetaavatu kuṟippaakac collum!" "Be amazed! Be astounded! After you have been astounded enough, say something in particular."
"itu mikavum atirʂʈa jaatakam taayee! taaŋkaɭ etuvum vittiyaacamaaka niṉaittuk koɭɭa maaʈʈiirkaɭ eṉṟu colkiṟeeṉ. taŋkaɭuʈaiya jaatakattaik kaaʈʈilum kuuʈa, itu orupaʈi meelaaṉatu. immaatiri atirʂʈa jaatakattai naaṉ ituvarai paarttateeyillai!" "Madam, this is a very fortunate horoscope. I am saying it with the belief that you will not mistake my words. This is one step better than even your horoscope! I have never, till now, seen such a lucky horoscope."
kuntavai puṉṉakai purintaaɭ; vaaṉatiyoo veʈkappaʈʈavaɭaay, "akkaa! inta turatirʂʈakkaariyaip pooy ivar ulakattileeyee illaata atirʂʈakkaari eṉkiṟaaree! ippaʈittaaṉ irukkum ivar colluvatellaam!" eṉṟaaɭ. Kundavai smiled. Vanathi said with some shyness, "Akka! He is calling this most unfortunate girl the luckiest person in this world! Everything he utters will be like this."
"ammaa! eṉṉa coṉṉiirkaɭ? naaṉ colvatu tavaṟaaṉaal eṉṉuʈaiya toɻilaiyee viʈʈuviʈukiṟeeṉ" eṉṟaar jootiʈar. "Amma, what are you saying? If my statements are incorrect, I will give up my practice!" said the Astrologer.
"veeɳʈaam, jootiʈaree! veeɳʈaam appaʈiyellaam ceytuviʈaatiir. eetoo naalupeerukku nalla vaarttaiyaakac collik koɳʈirum. aaṉaal veṟumaṉee potuppaʈaiyaakac colkiṟiiree tavira, kuṟippaaka oṉṟum collavillaiyee? ataṉaaleetaaṉ ivaɭ canteekappaʈukiṟaaɭ!" "No. Mr. Astrologer, no. Don't do anything like that. Keep predicting such good things to the people. But you are uttering generalities; you have said nothing in particular. That is why she doubts you."
"kuṟippaakac colla veeɳʈumaa? itoo collukiṟeeṉ! naalu maatatta ṟku muṉṉaal apacakuṉam maatiri tooṉṟak kuuʈiya oru kaariyam naʈantatu. eetoo oṉṟu tavaṟi viɻuntatu; aaṉaal atu uɳmaiyil apacakuṉam illai. atiliruntutaaṉ intak koomakaɭukku ellaa atirʂʈaŋkaɭum varappookiṉṟaṉa!" "You want me to say something specific? Here, I will say it. Four months ago, something that appeared to be an ill omen occurred. Something slipped and fell. But in truth, that is not an ill omen. It is from that incident that this Lady will obtain all her good fortune."
"vaaṉati! naaṉ eṉṉaʈi coṉṉeeṉ? paarttaayaa?" eṉṟaaɭ kuntavai teevi. "Vanathi, remember what I said? See?" said Kundavai Devi.
"muṉṉaaleeyee ivarukku niiŋkaɭ colli vaittirukiṟiirkaɭ poolirukkiṟatu!" eṉṟaaɭ vaaṉati. "You must have told him about it before!" said Vanathi.
"paarttiiraa cootiʈaree, intap peɳɳiṉ peeccai!" "Sir, look at the way this girl talks?"
"peecaʈʈum taayee! ippootu etu veeɳʈumaaṉaalum peecaʈʈum! naaɭaikku maṉṉar maṉṉaṉai maɳantu koɳʈu..." "Let her talk Thaye, let her say whatever she wants now. Tomorrow when she marries the king of kings ...."
"appaʈic colluŋkaɭ. iɭam peɳkaɭiʈam kaliyaaɳattaip paṟṟip peeciṉaal allavaa avarkaɭ cantooʂamaakak keeʈʈuk koɳʈiruppaarkaɭ?..." "Yes, talk about such things! Young girls will listen happily if you speak to them about marriage."
"ataittaaṉ naaṉum colla varukiṟeeṉ, taayee! tiʈutippeṉṟu kaliyaaɳap peeccai eʈukkak kuuʈaatu allavaa? eʈuttaal, intak kiɻavaṉukkup putti keʈʈuviʈʈatu" eṉṟu colli viʈuvaarkaɭ!" "That is what I have been saying too. If I suddenly raise the topic of marriage, people will say `This old man has lost his senses.' That is why ..."
"ivaɭukkup puruʂaṉ eŋkiruntu varuvaaṉ? eppootu varuvaaṉ? avaṉukku eṉṉa aʈaiyaaɭam? jaatakattiliruntu itaiyellaam colla muʈiyumaa, jootiʈaree!" "Where will her husband come from? When will he come? What is his identification? Can you tell us all these things from the horoscope, Sir?"
"aakaa! colla muʈiyaamal eṉṉa? naṉṟaayc colla muʈiyum!" eṉṟu kuuṟiviʈʈu, jootiʈar jaatakattai maṟupaʈiyum kavaṉittup paarttaar. "Oh yes! Why not? I can say it very well." The Astrologer peered into the horoscope once more.
kavaṉittup paarttaaroo, allatu kavaṉittup paarppatu pool avar paacaaŋkutaaṉ ceytaaroo namakkut teriyaatu. Whether he examined it carefully or pretended to do examine it, none can say!
piṟaku, talainimirntu nookki, "ammaɳi! inta iɭavaracikkuk kaɳavaṉ veku tuurattiliruntu varaveeɳʈiyatillai. camiipattil uɭɭavaṉtaaṉ; aayiṉum anta viiraati viiraṉ ippootu innaaʈʈil illai. kaʈal kaʈantu ceṉṟirukkiṟaaṉ!" eṉṟaar jootiʈar. Then looking up decisively he said, "Madam! A husband for this maid does not have to come from very far! He is quite nearby. However, that brave warrior is not in this country now. He has gone across the seas."
itaik keeʈʈatum kuntavai, vaaṉatiyaip paarttaaɭ. Upon hearing these words, Kundavai looked at Vanathi.
vaaṉatiyiṉ uɭɭattil poŋkiya uvakaiyai vaɭ aʈakkik koɭɭap paarttum muʈiyavillai, mukam kaaʈʈi viʈʈatu. Vanathi tried to contain the happiness that bubbled in her heart; but could not do so: her face showed it.
"appuṟam? avaṉ yaar? eṉṉa kulam? terintukoɭɭa eetaavatu aʈaiyaaɭam uɳʈaa?" "Then, who is he? What clan? Any signs to recognize him?"
"naṉṟaaka uɳʈu intap peɳɳai maɳantu koɭɭum paakkiyacaaliyiṉ tirukkaraŋkaɭil caŋku cakkara reekai irukkum, ammaa!" "Oh yes. Very clear signs. A prince fortunate enough to marry this girl, will have the sign of the conch and discus on his palms."
miiɳʈum kuntavai vaaṉatiyaip paarttaaɭ. vaaṉatiyiṉ mukam kavintu puumiyaip paarttuk koɳʈiruntatu. Once again Kundavai looked at Vanathi. But Vanathi was looking down, almost hiding her face.
"appaʈiyaaṉaal, ivaɭuʈaiya kaikaɭilum eeteeṉum aʈaiyaaɭa reekai illaamaṟpookumaa?" eṉṟaaɭ kuntavaip piraaʈʈi. "Won't there be some signs or significant lines on her palms also?" asked Kundavai Pirati.
"taayee! ivaɭuʈaiya paataŋkaɭai eppootaavatu taaŋkaɭ paarttatuɳʈaa?.." "Thaye! Have you ever seen the soles of this girl's feet? ..."
"eeṉ jootiʈaree! itu eṉṉa vaarttai! ivaɭuʈaiya kaalaip piʈikkumpaʈi eṉṉaic colkiṟiiraa?" "Sir! What are you saying? Are you asking me to touch her feet?" (Greeting a person by touching their feet denotes extreme humility, or servitude.)
"illai; appaʈiyellaam naaṉ collavillai aaṉaal oru kaalattil aayiramaayiram maṉṉarkulap peɳkaɭ, paʈʈa makiʂikaɭ, araciɭaŋkumarikaɭ, raaɳikaɭ, makaaraaɳikaɭ, intap peɳɳaraciyiṉ paataŋkaɭait toʈum paakkiyattukkaakat tavam kiʈappaarkaɭ taayee!" "No! Oh no! I am not saying anything like that! However, sometime in the future, thousands of princesses, queens and empresses will pray for the fortune of touching this noble lady's feet."
"akkaa! inta kiɻavar eṉṉaip parikaacam ceykiṟaar. itaṟkaakavaa eṉṉai iŋkee aɻaittu vantiirkaɭ? eɻuntiruŋkaɭ pookalaam!" eṉṟu uɳmaiyaakavee poŋki vanta poopattuʈaṉ kuuṟiṉaaɭ vaaṉati. "Akka! This old man is teasing me. Is it for this, that you brought me here? Please get up; let us go" Vanathi said this with some real anger.
"nii eṉṉattukkup pataṟukiṟaayaʈi, peɳɳee! avar eetaavatu collik koɳʈu pookaʈʈum..." "Why are you agitated my dear? Let him keep saying what he wants ..."
"naaṉ eetaavatu colli viʈavillai; ellaam inta jaatakattil kuṟippiʈʈiruppataittaaṉ collukiṟeeṉ. 'paatattaamarai' eṉṟu eetoo kavikaɭ upacaaramaaka varɳippaarkaɭ. intap peɳɳiṉ uɭɭaŋkaalaic ciṟitu kaaʈʈac colluŋkaɭ. atil centaamarai itaɻkaɭiṉ reekai kaʈʈaayam irukkum." "I am not saying anything. I am just explaining the notations in this horoscope. Poets often talk about lotus feet. Please ask this Lady to show me her feet a little bit. Her sole will surely have the line of the red-lotus."
"pootum! jootiʈaree ivaɭaip paṟṟi iṉṉum eetaavatu coṉṉaal eṉṉaik kaiyaip piʈittu iɻuttuk koɳʈu puṟappaʈʈu viʈuvaaɭ. ivaɭukku vaaykkap poo kum kaɳavaṉaip paṟṟik koɲcam colluŋkaɭ..." "Well! That's enough Sir. If you say anything more about this girl, she will get hold of my hands and drag me out. Tell us something about the man who is to be her husband."
"aakaa! colkiṟeeṉ! ivaɭaik kaippiʈikkum paakkiyavaaṉ viiraati viiraṉaayiruppaaṉ! nuuṟu nuuṟu poorkkaɭaŋkaɭil muṉṉaɳiyil niṉṟu vaakai maalai cuuʈuvaaṉ. maṉṉaati maṉṉaṉaayiruppaaṉ; aayiramaayiram aracarkaɭ pooṟṟac cakkaravarttiyiṉ cimmaacaṉattil paṉṉeʈuŋ kaalam viiṟṟiruppaaṉ. "Yes. Of course, I shall do so. The fortunate youth who will take her hand will be the bravest among brave; he will wear the victory garlands from fighting in the forefront of hundreds of campaigns. He will be a king of kings. With the praise and support of thousands of kings, he will sit upon the throne of an emperor for a long time."
"niir colvatai naaṉ nampavillai atu eppaʈi naʈakka muʈiyum?" eṉṟu keeʈʈa kuntavai teeviyiṉ mukattilee aarvamum makiɻcciyum aiyamum kavalaiyum kalantu taaɳʈavamaaʈiṉa. "I do not believe your words. How is that possible?" asked Kundavai. Several emotions -- eagerness, happiness, fear, doubt, worry -- danced on her face.
"naaṉum nampavillai. ivar etaiyoo niṉaittuk koɳʈu peecukiṟaar. ippaʈic coṉṉaal taŋkaɭukkuc cantooʂamaayirukkum eṉṟu kuuṟukiṟaar!" eṉṟaaɭ vaaṉati. "I too don't believe it. He is thinking of something else and saying all this. He is uttering such words to make you happy!" said Vanathi.
"iṉṟu niiŋkaɭ nampaaviʈʈaal paatakamillai; oru kaalattil nampuviirkaɭ appootu inta eeɻaic cootiʈaṉai maṟantu viʈaatiirkaɭ.." "If you don't believe my words today, there is no harm. One day you will believe it. Don't forget this poor astrologer on that day."
"akkaa! naam pookalaamaa?" eṉṟu maṟupaʈi keeʈʈaaɭ vaaṉati. Vanathi asked once again: "Akka, shall we go?"
avaɭuʈaiya kariya viɻikaɭiṉ oraŋkaɭil iru kaɳɳiirt tuɭikaɭ eʈʈip paarttuk koɳʈiruntaṉa. Two tear drops peeped at the corners of her dark eyes.
"iṉṉum oree oru viʂayam colli viʈukiṟeeṉ. ataik keeʈʈuviʈʈup puṟappaʈuŋkaɭ, inta iɭavaraciyai maɳantu koɭɭap pookum viiraṉukku ettaṉai ettaṉaiyoo apaayaŋkaɭum, kaɳʈaŋkaɭum eeṟpaʈum; pakaivarkaɭ palar uɳʈu..." "I have to tell you just one more thing. Please hear that also and then leave. There will be several obstacles and dangers for the young man who is to marry this Princess. He will have several enemies ..."
"aiyoo!" "Oh!"
"aaṉaal avvaɭavu apaayaŋkaɭum kaɳʈaŋkaɭum muʈivil paṟantu pookum; pakaivarkaɭ paʈunaacam aʈaivaarkaɭ. intat teeviyai aʈaiyum naayakaṉ ellaat taʈaikaɭaiyum miiṟi makooṉṉata pataviyai aʈaivaaṉ.... itaiviʈa mukkiyamaaṉa ceyti oṉṟu uɳʈu taayee! naaṉ vayataaṉavaṉ aakaiyaal uɭɭatai oɭiyaamal viʈʈuc colkiṟeeṉ. intap peɳɳiṉ vayiṟṟai niiŋkaɭ orunaaɭ paaruŋkaɭ. atil aalilaiyiṉ reekaikaɭ illaaviʈʈaal naaṉ inta jootiʈat toɻilaiyee viʈ ʈuviʈukiṟeeṉ..." "But all the dangers and obstacles will fly away in the end. Enemies will be totally destroyed; the Lord who attains this Lady will cross all impediments and achieve great positions. There is something much more important than even this ... Thaye! I am an old man. Therefore, I can reveal all without hiding anything. You should look at the stomach of this girl sometime. If you do not find the lines and sign of a banyan (ficus) leaf on her stomach, I will give up my practice of astrology."
"aalilaiyiṉ reekaiyil eṉṉa viceeʂam jootiʈaree?" "Sir, what is so special about the sign of the banyan leaf?"
"aalilaiyiṉ meel paɭɭikoɳʈa perumaaṉ yaar eṉpatu teriyaataa? anta makaaviʂɳuviṉ amcattuʈaṉ ivaɭ vayiṟṟil oru piɭɭai piṟappaaṉ. ivaɭuʈaiya naayakaṉukkaavatu pala iʈaiɲcalkaɭ, taʈaŋkalkaɭ, apaayaŋkaɭ, kaɳʈaŋkaɭ ellaam uɳʈu. aaṉaal intap peɳɳiṉ vayiṟṟil avatarikkap pookum kumaaraṉukkut taʈaŋkal eṉpatee kiʈaiyaatu. avaṉ niṉaittatellaam kaikuuʈum; eʈuttatellaam niṟaiveeṟum, avaṉ toʈʈatellaam poṉṉaakum; avaṉ kaal vaitta iʈamellaam avaṉuʈaiya aaʈcikku uɭɭaakum; avaṉ kaɳɳaal paartta iʈamellaam pulikkoʈi paṟakkum.taayee! ivaɭuʈaiya kumaaraṉ naʈattic cellum caiṉyaŋkaɭ poṉṉi natiyiṉ putu veɭɭattaip pool eŋkum taŋkutaʈaiyiṉṟic cellum. jayalakʂmi avaṉukkuk kaikaʈʈi niṉṟu ceevakam purivaaɭ. avaṉ piṟanta naaʈʈiṉ pukaɻ muuvulakamum paravum. avaṉ piṟanta kulattiṉ kiirtti ulakam uɭɭa aɭavum niṉṟu nilavum!..." "Don't you know who the Lord who reposed on the banyan leaf is? A son with the aspects of that great Vishnu will be born to her. Her Lord is likely to have all sorts of dangers, obstacles, impediments and bad periods. But the son who is to be born of this Lady will have nothing that is a deterrent. All that he thinks of will be concluded; anything he undertakes will be completed; his touch will be golden; wherever he steps will be under his sway; whatever he sees will have the tiger-flag flying over it. Madam, the armies led by her son will flow in all directions, with no hinderance, like the fresh floods of the Ponni. The Goddess of Victory will be his bonded slave. The fame of his native lands will spread across the worlds. The fame of his clan will last as long as this world exists!"
ivvaaṟu jootiʈar aaveecam vantavar pool colli vantapootu kuntavai teevi avaruʈaiya mukattaiyee paarttuk koɳʈu, avar kuuṟiya vaarttaikaɭai oṉṟuviʈaamal viɻuŋkupavaɭ pool keeʈʈuk koɳʈiruntaaɭ. When the Astrologer spoke these words as if in some frenzy, Kundavai was looking at him with rapt attention, listening to every word he uttered.
"akkaa!" eṉṟa tiiṉamaaṉa kuralaik keeʈʈut tiʈukkiʈʈut tirumpip paarttaaɭ. She was startled to turn around upon hearing the distressed call, "Akka!"
"eṉakku eṉṉamoo ceykiṟatu!" eṉṟu meelum tiiṉamaakak kuuṟiṉaaɭ vaaṉati; "Something is happening to me," said Vanathi in further distress.
tiʈiireṉṟu mayaŋkit taraiyil caayntaaɭ. Suddenly she fainted and fell to the floor.
"joociyaree! ciikkiram koɲcam taɳɳiir koɳʈu vaaruŋkaɭ!" eṉṟu kuntavai colliviʈʈu, vaaṉatiyait tuukki maʈiyil pooʈʈuk koɳʈaaɭ. "Sir! Please fetch some water quickly," saying this, Kundavai lifted Vanathi onto her lap.
cootiʈar taɳɳiir koɳʈu vantaar; kuntavai taɳɳiirai vaaŋki vaaṉatiyiṉ mukattil tauittaaɭ. The Astrologer brought some water. Kundavai sprinkled the water on her friends face.
"oṉṟum neeraatu, ammaa! kavalaippaʈaatiirkaɭ..." ṉṟaar jootiʈar. "Nothing will go wrong, Amma! Do not be concerned," said the Astrologer.
"oru kavalaiyum illai; ivaɭukku itu vaɻakkam. inta maatiri ituvaraiyil aintaaṟu taʈavai aakiviʈʈatu! caṟṟup pooṉaal kaɳ viɻittu eɻuntiruppaaɭ, eɻuntatum itu puulookamaa, kailaacamaa eṉṟu keeʈpaaɭ!" eṉṟaaɭ kuntavai. "I am not worried. This is her habit. This has happened four or five times till now. She will open her eyes and get up after some time. Upon waking she will ask if this is the earth or paradise," said Kundavai.
piṟaku ciṟitu melliya kuralil, "joociyaree! mukkiyamaaka oṉṟu keeʈpataṟkaakavee uŋkaɭiʈam vanteeṉ.naaʈu nakaraŋkaɭilee cila kaalamaaka jaṉaŋkaɭ eeteetoo peecik koɭkiṟaarkaɭaamee? vaaṉattil cila naaɭaaka vaal naʈcattiram tooṉṟukiṟatee? itaṟkellaam uɳmaiyil eeteeṉum poruɭ uɳʈaa? iraajyattukku eetaavatu aapattu uɳʈaa? maaṟutal kuɻappam eeteeṉum eeṟpaʈumaa?" eṉṟu iɭaiyapiraaʈʈi keeʈʈaaɭ. Then, in a much softer voice she asked "Sir! I came to ask you something very important. Have you any news?" The Astrologer's manner changed; he said some words to her very precisely and quickly. Then, Kundavai Pirati asked, "I hear that people in the countryside and cities are talking about all sorts of things. The long tailed comet has been appearing in the skies for sometime. Is there some meaning to all this? Is there some danger to the empire? Will there be any change, confusion?"
"atai maʈʈum eṉṉaik keeʈkaatiirkaɭ, taayee! teecaŋkaɭ, iraajyaŋkaɭ, iraajaaŋka nikaɻccikaɭ ivaṟṟukkellaam jaatakamum kiʈaiyaatu; joociyamum colla muʈiyaatu. naaṉ payiṉṟa vittaiyil atellaam varavillai. ɲaaṉikaɭum, riʂikaɭum, makaaṉkaɭum, yookikaɭum oruveeɭai ɲaaṉa tiruʂʈiyil paarttuc collalaam. inta eeɻaikku antac cakti kiʈaiyaatu. iraajariika kaariyaŋkaɭil naaɭ, naʈcattiram, jaatakam, joociyam ellaam caktiyaṟṟup pooyviʈukiṉṟaṉa..." "Lady, don't ask me about that. There is no horoscope for kingdoms, nations or political events; neither can I predict anything by astrology about them. I know nothing about such matters through the science I practice. Perhaps the seers and philosophers or the devout and ascetic may be able to foresee something. This poor fellow does not have such powers. In politics the stars and planets of astrology have no power." The Astrologer spoke these words with a certain emphasis, looking at Vanathi who seemed to be stirring.
"joociyaree! mika caamarttiyamaakap peecukiṟiir! iraajaaŋkattukku jaatakam paarkka veeɳʈaam. aaṉaal eṉ tantaiyaip paṟṟiyum cakootararkaɭaip paṟṟiyum paarttuc collalaam allavaa? avarkaɭuʈaiya jaatakattaip paarttaal iraajaaŋka jaatakattaip paarttatupool aakiviʈum allavaa?" "Sir! You talk very skillfully. You don't have to study the horoscope of a nation. But can you not reveal particulars about my father and my brothers? Isn't studying their horoscopes like looking at the horoscope of the nation?"
"caavakaacamaaka iṉṉoru naaɭ paarttuc colkiṟeeṉ, ammaa! potuvaaka, itu kuɻappaŋkaɭum apaayaŋkaɭum niṟainta kaalam. elloorumee ciṟitu jaakkirataiyaaka irukka veeɳʈiyatutaaṉ..." "I will look into them at leisure and tell you. Thaye, in general this period is full of confusion and danger. All of us have to be a little careful."
"joociyaree! eṉ tantai, cakkaravartti... paɻaiyaaṟaiyai viʈʈut taɲcaavuurukkup pooṉatili untu eṉakku oree kavalaiyaayirukkiṟatu." "Sir! About my father..., the Emperor ... I am quite worried ever since he moved to Tanjore from Pazlayarai."
"muṉṉamee coṉṉeeṉee, taayee! makaaraajaavukkup periya kaɳʈam irukkiṟatu. taŋkaɭ kuʈumpattukkum periya apaayaŋkaɭ irukkiṉṟaṉa. turkkaateeviyiṉ aruɭ makimaiyiṉaal ellaam nivarttiyaakum." "I have told you earlier, Madam. This is a period of grave danger for the Emperor. All your family has to face great danger. With the grace of the Goddess Durga all will be overcome."
"akkaa! naam eŋkee irukkiṟoom?" eṉṟu vaaṉatiyiṉ tiiṉak kural keeʈʈatu. "Akka, where are we?" asked the faint voice of Vanathi.
kuntavaiyiṉ maʈiyil talai vaittup paʈuttirunta vaaṉati kaɳɳimaikaɭai vaɳʈiṉ ciṟakukaɭaippool koʈʈi malara malara viɻittaaɭ. Vanathi, who had her head on Kundavai's lap, blinked her eyelids several times like the buzzing bee, and looked around.
"kaɳmaɳi! iṉṉum naam inta puulookattileetaaṉ irukkiṟoom! corkka lookattukku aɻaittup pooka puʂpaka vimaaṉam iṉṉum vantu ceeravillai. eɻuntiru! nammuʈaiya kutirai puuʈʈiya ratattileeyee eeṟik koɳʈu araɳmaṉaikkup pookalaam!" eṉṟaaɭ kuntavai. "Darling, we are still on this earth. The flower-laden flying-chariot has not yet come to take us to the heavens. Get up! Let us get into our mere horse drawn chariot and go back to the palace."
vaaṉati eɻuntu uʈkaarntu koɳʈu, "naaṉ mayakkam pooʈʈu viɻuntuviʈʈeeṉaa?" eṉṟaaɭ. Vanathi sat up and asked, "Did I faint?"
"mayakkam pooʈavillai; akkaaviṉ maʈiyil paʈuttuk koɲcam tuuŋkiviʈʈaay! taalaaʈʈupkuuʈap paaʈiṉeeṉ uṉ kaatil viɻavillaiyaa?" "No. You did not faint. You took a little nap on my lap. I even sang you a lullaby. Did you not hear it?"
"koopikkaatiirkaɭ, akkaa! eṉṉai aṟiyaamalee talai kiṟukiṟuttu vantuviʈʈatu." "Please do not scold me Akka. Without my awareness, I felt dizzy."
"kiṟukiṟukkum, kiṟukiṟukkum; inta joociyar eṉakku appaʈi joociyam colliyiruntaal eṉakkuk kuuʈattaaṉ kiṟukiṟuttirukkum." "Yes. You will feel dizzy. Quite dizzy. If this Astrologer had predicted all those grand fortunes for me, even my head would feel dizzy."
"ataṉaal illai, akkaa! ivar coṉṉataiyellaam naaṉ nampiviʈʈeeṉaa?" "Not because of that Akka. You think I believed everything he said?"
"nii nampiṉaayoo, nampavillaiyoo? aaṉaal joociyar payantee pooyviʈʈaar! uṉṉaip pooṉṟa payaŋkoɭɭiyai iṉimeel eŋkum aɻaittup pookak kuuʈaatu." "I don't know if you believed it or not? But this Astrologer was quite worried about you. I should not take a poor spirited person like you, anywhere, anymore."
"naaṉtaaṉ cootiʈariʈam varavillaiyeṉṟu appootee coṉṉeeṉee! niiŋkaɭtaaṉee..?" "I said that I did not want to come to this astrologer. You insisted ..."
"eṉ kuṟṟantaaṉ eɻuntiru, pookalaam vaacal varaiyil naalu aʈi naʈakka muʈiyumaa? illaaviʈʈaal iʈuppil eʈuttu vaittuk koɳʈu pooka veeɳumaa?" "It is my fault. Get up. Let us go. Can you walk to the doorstep? Or, should I carry you on my hip?
"veeɳʈaam! veeɳʈaam! naṉṟaay naʈakka muʈiyum." "No. No. I can walk very well."
"caṟṟup poṟuŋkaɭ, taayee! teeviyiṉ piracaatam tarukiṟeeṉ, vaaŋkik koɳʈu pooŋkaɭ" eṉṟu joociyar colli viʈʈu olaiccuvaʈiyaik kaʈʈat toʈaŋkiṉaar. "Please have a little patience Thaye! I shall give you some sacrament-offerings of the Goddess. Please receive them before you go," said the Astrologer as he tidied his papers.
"joociyaree! eṉakku eṉṉavellaamoo coṉṉiirkaɭ; akkaavukku oṉṟumee collavillaiyee?" eṉṟu vaaṉati kuuṟiṉaaɭ. "Sir, you described all sorts of things for me. But you did not say anything for Akka?" asked Vanathi.
"ammaa! iɭaiyapiraaʈʈikku ellaam colliyirukkiṟeeṉ putitaaka eṉṉa colla veeɳʈum?" "Amma, I have told everything to the Younger Pirati. What is there to say afresh?"
"akkaavai maɳantu koɭɭap pookum viiraati viirar" "About the bravest of warriors who is to marry Akka ...."
"acakaaya cuurar" eṉṟu kuntavai kuṟukkiʈʈuc coṉṉaaɭ. "That dauntless gallant ...." interrupted Kundavai with some surprise.
"canteekam eṉṉa?..makaa paraakkiramacaaliyaaṉa iraajakumaarar..." "Doubtless. A very capable prince..."
"muppattiraɳʈu caamutrikaa laʈcaɳamum poruntiyavar; puttiyil pirakaspati; vittaiyil carasvati, aɻakilee maṉmataṉ; aaṟṟalil arjuṉaṉ!" "He who has all the thirty-two signs of good looks; Jupiter in wisdom; a veritable Saraswati (deity of learning) in knowledge; handsome as Cupid, Oh no, as Arjuna (epic warrior)!"
"iɭaiyapiraaʈʈikku eeṟṟa anta sukumaararaaṉa iraajakumaarar eŋkiruntu eppootu varuvaar? varukiṟaar, taayee! varukiṟaar! kaʈʈaayam varappookiṟaar ati ciikkirattileeyee varuvaar." "When will that handsome prince, deserving of the Younger Pirati come and where will he come from?"
"eppaʈi varuvaar? kutirai meel varuvaaraa? ratattil eeṟi varuvaaraa? yaaṉai meel varuvaaraa? kaal naʈaiyaaka varuvaaraa? allatu neeree aakaacattiliruntu kuuraiyaip pottuk koɳʈu vantu kutippaaraa!" eṉṟu kuntavaiteevi keeliyaakak keeʈʈaaɭ. "How will he come? Upon a horse? On a chariot? By walk? Or, will he rip open the roof and jump down from the skies?" asked Kundavai with a mocking voice.
"akkaa! kutirai kaalaʈic cattam keeʈkiṟatu!" eṉṟu vaaṉati ciṟitu paraparappuʈaṉ coṉṉaaɭ. "Akka, I can hear the hoof beats of a horse," Vanathi said this with some agitation.
"oruvarukkum keeɭaatatu uṉakku maattiram aticayamaayk keeʈkum!" "You will hear most wonderful things unheard by anyone else."
"illai, veeʈikkaikkuk collavillai itoo keeɭuŋkaɭ!" "No. I am not joking. Listen."
uɳmaiyaakavee appootu viitiyil kutirai oṉṟu viraintu varum kaalaʈic cattam keeʈʈatu. By now all three could hear the quick gallop of a horse on the road outside.
"keeʈʈaal eṉṉaʈi? kuʈantaip paʈʈaɳattiṉ viitikaɭil kutirai pookaamalaa irukkum?" eṉṟaaɭ kuntavai. "What if you hear it? Won't horses gallop along the road?" said Kundavai.
"illai; iŋkee varukiṟatu maatiri tooṉṟiyatu!" "No. It seems to be coming here, to this house."
"uṉakku eetaavatu vicittiramaakat tooṉṟa m eɻuntiru, pookalaam!" "OK. Get up. Let us go."
iccamayattil anta viiʈʈiṉ vaacalil eetoo kuɻappamaaṉa captam keeʈʈatu; kural olikaɭum keeʈʈaṉa. At this time they could hear some confused noises outside the door. Voices could be heard.
"itutaaṉee joociyar viiʈu?" "Is this the Astrologer's house?"
"aamaam; nii yaar?" "Yes; who are you?"
"joociyar irukkiṟaaraa?" "Is the Astrologer home?"
"uɭɭee pookak kuuʈaatu?" "You cannot enter."
"appaʈittaaṉ pooveeṉ!" "I will do so."
"viʈamaaʈʈeeṉ" "I cannot let you."
"joociyaraip paarkka veeɳʈum" "I must see the Astrologer."
"appuṟam vaa" "Come later."
"appuṟam vara muʈiyaatu; eṉakku mikka avacaram!" "I cannot come later; I am in a great hurry."
"aʈee! aʈee! nil! nil!" "Hey! You! You fellow! Stop! Stop!"
"caṟṟu! vilakippoo! taʈuttaayoo koṉṟuviʈuveeṉ..." "Damn it! Move away! I'll kill you if you stand in the way."
"aiyaa! aiyaa! veeɳʈaam! uɭɭee pooka veeɳʈaam!" "Sir! Sir! Please! Please! Don't enter! Don't go in."
ittakaiya kuɻappamaaṉa kuuccal neruŋki neruŋkik keeʈʈatu; paʈaar eṉṟu vaacaṟ katavu tiṟantatu. The confused shouts came closer, and closer. The wooden front door opened with a bang. With all this great commotion, a young man made a sudden entry.
avvaɭavu piramaatamaaṉa taʈapuʈaluʈaṉ oru vaalipaṉ uɭɭee tiʈumpiraveecamaaka vantaaṉ. avaṉaip piṉṉaaliruntu tooɭkaɭaip piʈittu iɻukka oruvaṉ muyaṉṟu koɳʈiruntaaṉ. Another fellow was trying to drag him back by pulling at his shoulders. The youth shook off the hands, stepped across the doorway and came into the room.
vaalipaṉ timiṟik koɳʈu vaacaṟpaʈiyaik kaʈantu uɭɭee vantaaṉ. vanta vaalipaṉ yaar eṉṟu vaacakarkaɭ uukittiruppaarkaɭ namatu viiraṉ vantiyatteevaṉ taaṉ!. viiʈʈukkuɭɭee irunta muuṉṟu peeruʈaiya kaɳkaɭum eeka kaalattil avviiraṉaip paarttaṉa. Our readers would have guessed the identity of the newcomer! Yes, it was our youthful hero, Vandiya Devan. All the three pairs of eyes inside the house looked at that warrior.
vantiyatteevaṉum uɭɭiruntavarkaɭaip paarttaaṉ. illai; uɭɭeeyiruntavarkaɭil oruvarait taaṉ paarttaaṉ. atukuuʈa illai; kuntavai teeviyai avaṉ muɻumaiyaakap paarkkavillai.avaɭuʈaiya poṉ mukattai maʈʈumee paarttaaṉ. mukattaiyaavatu muɻumaiyum paarttaaṉoo eṉṟaal, atuvum illai! viyappiṉaal ciṟitu virintirunta avaɭuʈaiya pavaɭac cevvaayiṉ itaɻkaɭaip paarttaaṉ; kampiiramum viyappum kuṟumpuc cirippum tatumpiyirunta avaɭuʈaiya akaṉṟa kaɳkaɭaip paarttaaṉ. kaɳɳimaikaɭaiyum kariya puruvaŋkaɭaiyum paarttaaṉ; kuŋkumac civappaaṉa kuɻinta kaṉṉaŋkaɭaip paarttaaṉ. caŋkaiyotta vaɻuvaɻuppaa ṉa kaɻuttaip paarttaaṉ. ivvaɭavaiyum oree camayattil taṉi taṉiyaakap paarttaaṉ. taṉittaṉiyaaka avai avaṉ maṉattil patintaṉa. Vandiya Devan also looked at the people inside; no; he looked at only one person inside. Not even that. He did not see even Kundavai Devi completely. He just saw her golden face. Did he at least see her face completely? -- not even that! He saw the petals of her coral red lips, opening slightly with surprise; he saw her wide open eyes brimming with mischief, surprise and laughter; he saw the dark eyelashes and eyebrows; he saw the sandal colored forehead; he saw the rosy dimpled cheeks; he saw the throat shaped like a smooth conch-shell. He saw all these at the same time and individually! They became embossed in his heart.
itellaam cila vinaaʈi neerantaaṉ, uʈaṉee caʈʈeṉṟu tirumpic cootiʈaruʈaiya ciiʈaṉai nookki, "eeṉappaa, uɭɭee peɳ piɭɭaikaɭ irukkiṟaarkaɭ eṉṟu nii collak kuuʈaatu? colliyiruntaal naaṉ ippaʈi vantiruppeeṉaa?" eṉṟu keeʈʈuk koɳʈee ciiʈaṉai maṟupakkam taɭɭik koɳʈu vaacaṟpaʈiyai miiɳʈum kaʈantaaṉ. aayiṉum vauiyil poovataṟkuɭ iṉṉum oru taʈavai kuntavaiteeviyait tirumpip paarttu viʈʈuttaaṉ pooṉaaṉ. All this was for just a second. He quickly turned towards the Astrologer's disciple and said, "Why man, inside ... Why didn't you say that these ladies were inside the house? If you had said it, would I have come in like this?" With these words he pushed the man outside and crossed the doorstep once again. Even then, he turned to look at Kundavai once more, before going out.
"aʈee appaa! puyal aʈittu oyntatu pool allavaa irukkiṟatu?" eṉṟaaɭ kuntavaip piraaʈʈi. "Dear me! It seems like the quiet after a raging storm!" said Kundavai Pirati.
"iṉṉum oyntapaaʈillai; atoo keeɭuŋkaɭ!" eṉṟaaɭ koʈumpaaɭuur iɭavaraci. "Listen. The storm has not stopped!" said Vanathi of Kodumbalur.
vaacalil iṉṉamum vantiyatteevaṉukkum cootiʈariṉ ciiʈaṉukkum tarkkam naʈantu koɳʈiruntatu. The debate between the Astrologer's disciple and Vandiya Devan was still continuing.
"joociyaree! ivar yaar?" eṉṟaaɭ kuntavai. "Sir! Who was that?" asked Kundavai.
"teriyaatu, taayee! yaaroo acaluurkkaarar maatiri irukkiṟatu. periya muraʈʈup piɭɭaiyeṉṟu tooṉṟukiṟatu." "I do not know, Thaye. Seems like a foreigner; looks as if he is a rough fellow."
kuntavai tiʈiireṉṟu etaiyoo niṉaittuk koɳʈu kalakalaveṉṟu cirittaaɭ. Kundavai suddenly thought of something and laughed brightly.
"etaṟkaaka akkaa cirikkiṟiirkaɭ?" "Why are you laughing, Akka?" asked Vanathi.
"etaṟkaakavaa? eṉakku varappookum maɳaaɭaṉ kutiraiyil varap pookiṟaaṉaa, yaaṉaiyil varap pookiṟaaṉaa, allatu kuurai vaɻiyaaka vantu kutikkap pookiṟaaṉaa eṉṟu peecik koɳʈiruntoomee, atai niṉaittuk koɳʈu ciritteeṉ!" "Why am I laughing? We were talking about my bridegroom -- if he would come on horseback, riding an elephant or jump down from the roof; I thought of that and laughed!"
ippootu vaaṉatikkum ciripput taaŋka muʈiyaamal vantatu. iruvaruʈaiya cirippum kalantu alai alaiyaaka eɻuntatu.vauiyil eɻunta caccaravuc captaŋkuuʈa inta iru maŋkaiyariṉ cirippiṉ oliyil aʈaŋkiviʈʈatu. Vanathi was also possessed of an uncontrollable laughter now. Their laughter rose like waves on the ocean shore. Because of their laughter even the noises of the dispute outside stopped.
cootiʈar mauṉa cintaṉaiyil aaɻntavaraay, araca kumaarikaɭ iruvarukkum kuŋkumam koʈuttaar. peṟa ṟuk koɳʈu iruvarum eɻuntaṉar; viiʈʈukku vauiyil ceṉṟaṉar. cootiʈarum kuuʈa vantaar. Immersed in silent thought the Astrologer gave kumkum to both ladies. After receiving it both women rose and walked outside. The Astrologer went along.
viiʈʈu vaacalil ciṟitu otuŋki niṉṟa vantiyatteevaṉ, peɳmaɳikaɭaip paarttatum, "maṉṉikka veeɳʈum.uɭɭee peɳkaɭ irukkiṟaarkaɭ eṉṟu intap putticaali collavillai. aakaiyiṉaaltaaṉ appaʈi avacaramaaka vantu viʈʈeeṉ. ataṟkaaka maṉṉikka veeɳʈum!" eṉṟu uratta kuralil coṉṉaaṉ. Vandiya Devan who was standing aside near the doorstep, saw them and said loudly, "I beg pardon. This genius did not tell me that you ladies were inside. That is why I entered in such a hurry. Forgive me for that."
kuntavai malarnta mukattuʈaṉ kuṟumpum keeliyum miʈukkum tatumpiya kaɳkaɭiṉaal vantiyatteevaṉai oru taʈavai eeṟiʈʈup paarttaaɭ. oru vaarttaiyum maṟumoɻi collavillai. vaaṉatiyai oru kaiyiṉaal piʈittu iɻuttuk koɳʈu ratam niṉṟa aalamarattaʈiyai nookkic ceṉṟaaɭ. With a pleasant face and mischief- filled, teasing eyes, Kundavai looked up at him once. She did not say a single word in reply. She took hold of Vanathi's hand and walked towards her chariot under the banyan tree.
"kuʈantai nakarattup peɳkaɭukku mariyaataiyee teriyaatu poolirukkiṟatu. eetaʈaa oru maṉitaṉ valiya vantu peecukiṟaaṉee eṉpataṟkaakavaavatu tirumpip paarttu oru vaarttai patil collak kuuʈaatoo?" eṉṟu vantiyatteevaṉ iraintu kuuṟiyatu avarkaɭ kaatil viɻuntatu. "The women of Kudanthai seem to have no manners. Can they not utter even one word in reply to a gentleman who accosts them?" The words of Vandiya Devan spoken in a loud voice could be heard by everyone.
ratattil kutiraiyaip puuʈʈic caarati aayattamaaka niṟuttiyiruntaaṉ. iɭavaracikaɭ iruvarum ratattil eeṟik koɳʈatum, rata caaratiyum muṉṉaal eeṟik koɳʈaaṉ. ratam aricilaaṟṟaŋkaraiyai nookki viraintu ceṉṟatu. vantiyatteevaṉ ratam maṟaiyum varaiyil paarttuk koɳʈu niṉṟaaṉ. After helping them both be seated, the charioteer also climbed to his perch. The horse drawn chariot moved swiftly towards the banks of River Arisil. Vandiya Devan stood watching till the chariot disappeared from sight.
