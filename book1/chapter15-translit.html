patiṉaintaam attiyaayam Chapter 15
vaaṉatiyiṉ jaalam Vanathi's Tricks
iɭaiyapiraaʈʈi kuntavaiteeviyum koʈumpaaɭuur iɭavaraci vaaṉatiyum ratattil eeṟik kuʈantai nakarai nookkic ceṉṟaarkaɭ allavaa? ataṉ piṟaku paʈakil irunta peɳkaɭ eṉṉa peeciṉaarkaɭ, eṉṉa ceytaarkaɭ eṉpatai naam ciṟitu terintu koɭɭa veeɳʈum. We request our readers to please recall that earlier moment in our story when Kundavai and Vanathi climbed onto the chariot and rode towards Kudanthai, leaving their friends on the banks of the River Arisil. We shall now listen to the conversation of those maids who were left behind.
"aʈiyee, taarakai!, intak koʈumpaaɭuurkkaarikku vanta yookattaip paaraʈi! avaɭ peeril nam iɭaiyapiraaʈʈikku eṉṉaʈi ivvaɭavu aacai?" eṉṟaaɭ orutti. "My dear Tarika, look at this good fortune that has blown the way of that Kodumbalur female! What is so fascinating about her for our Younger Pirati?"
"aacaiyumillai, oṉṟumillaiyaʈi, vaariɳi! naalu maatamaaka antap peɳ oru maatiri kiṟukkup piʈittavaɭ pool irukkiṟaaɭ. aʈikkaʈi mayakkam pooʈʈu viɻuntu tolaikkiṟaaɭ. taay takappaṉaar illaata peɳɳai nammai nampi oppuvittirukkiṟaarkaɭee eṉṟu iɭaiyapiraaʈʈikkuk kavalai. ataṉaaltaaṉ, vaaṉatikku eṉṉa vantuviʈʈatu eṉṟu keeʈkac cootiʈariʈam aɻaittup pooyirukkiṟaar! eetaavatu peey picaacukaɭiṉ ceeʂʈaiyaayirukkalaam allavaa? appaʈiyiruntaal eetaavatu mantiram kintiram pooʈʈu oʈʈa veeɳʈum allavaa?" eṉṟaaɭ taarakai. "No fascination, no nothing! For the past four months that girl has been going around like one deranged; she is often falling down in one of her fainting fits. The Younger Pirati is worried because they have left that orphan girl in her care. She is taking Vanathi to the Astrologer to find out what ails her. Perhaps it is because of some ghost or spirit? If it is so, don't they have to get rid of it with some magic or spell?"
"peeyumillai, picaacumillaiyaʈi! ivaɭai vantu entap picaacu piʈikkap pookiṟatu? ivaɭee nuuṟu picaacai aʈittu oʈʈi viʈuvaaɭee?" eṉṟaaɭ vaariɳi. "It is no ghost and no phantom. Which spirit will come and posses her? She is capable of driving away a hundred ghosts." A girl known as Varini uttered these words with some venom.
"vaaṉati mayakkam pooʈʈu viɻuvatu kuuʈap paacaaŋkutaaṉaʈi! ippaʈiyellaam ceytaal metuvaaka iɭavaracarait taṉ valaiyil pooʈʈuk koɳʈu viʈalaam eṉṟu avaɭuʈaiya eɳɳam!" eṉṟaaɭ iṉṉorutti. "All those fainting fits of Vanathi are one big deception my dear. That snake thinks that she can capture the Prince in her coils if she does such things."
"niravati colluvatutaaṉ cari! atu maʈʈumaa! aṉṟaikku tiipat taʈʈaik kiiɻee pooʈʈaaɭee? atukuuʈat taṉṉai avar kava ikka veeɳʈumeṉpataṟkaakac ceyta kaariyantaaṉ! iraɳʈu kaiyaalum eentik koɳʈirunta taʈʈu appaʈit tavaṟi viɻuntu viʈumaa? allatu nam iɭavaracar eṉṉa puliyaa, karaʈiyaa, avaraip paarttu ivaɭ payappaʈuvataṟku?" eṉṟaaɭ vaariɳi. "What Niravati says is true. And that is not the whole story. Remember that day when the Prince was leaving on his campaign she dropped the platter with the lighted lamp? Even that, she did mainly to attract the attention of the Prince. How can a platter carried in both hands slip like that? Or is our Prince some sort of fearsome tiger or bear for her to be frightened?" continued Varini.
"uʈaṉee muurccai pooʈʈu viɻuntu viʈʈataakap paacaaŋku ceytaaɭee? ataṟku evvaɭavu keʈʈikkaarattaṉam veeɳʈum?" eṉṟaaɭ niravati. "And she pretended to fall into a faint immediately; How clever."
"avaɭ ceyta jaalattaik kaaʈʈilum anta jaalattil kuntavaiteeviyum iɭavaracarum eemaantu pooṉaarkaɭee, atutaaṉ periya veeʈikkai!" eṉṟaaɭ centiru eṉpavaɭ. "More than all her tricks, the funny thing is that Kundavai Pirati and the Prince believed her!" added Sendiru.
"poyyum puṉaicuruʈʈum jaalamum maaymaalamum ceykiṟavarkaɭukkuttaaṉ itu kaalam!" eṉṟaaɭ mantaakiṉi eṉpavaɭ. Mandakini offered the comment, "These are times for those who deceive and tell lies and falsehoods."
"yuttattukkup puṟappaʈʈaaṉa piṟaku iɭavaracar, tirumpi vantu inta vaaṉatiyaip paarttuviʈʈup pooṉaaree, itaiviʈa eṉṉaʈi veeɳʈum? avaɭuʈaiya maayaajaalam evvaɭavu tuuram palittu viʈʈatu paarttaayaa?" eṉṟaaɭ vaariɳi. "The Prince who had already mounted his horse, leaving for the campaign; he came back into the palace to look at her! What else does one need? See how successful her tricks were?"
"atellaam oṉṟumillai; iɭavaracar avvaɭavu meeṉmaiyaaṉa kuɳamuɭɭavar. oru peɳ mayakkam pooʈʈu viɻuntu viʈʈaaɭ eṉṟaal, avaɭaip paarttu vicaariyaamal poovaaraaʈi? atiliruntu nii oṉṟum arttam kaṟpikka veeɳʈaam!" eṉṟaaɭ taarakai.
"iɭavaracaraip paṟṟi nii colvatu uɳmaitaaṉ. avaraip pooṉṟa kuɳacaali inta iireeɻu patiṉaalu ulakattilum veeṟu yaar irukka muʈiyum? kataikaɭilum kaaviyaŋkaɭilum kuuʈak kiʈaiyaatu; aaṉaal naaṉ colkiṟatu veeṟu. ivaɭ - inta vaaṉati - mayakkam pooʈʈu viɻuntaaɭee, atu eṉṉa mayakkam teriyumaa? ataik keeʈkak jootiʈariʈamee pooyirukka veeɳʈiyatillai. eṉṉaik keeʈʈiruntaal naaṉee colliyiruppeeṉ!" eṉṟaaɭ vaariɳi. "What you say about the Prince is of course true. There is none in the fourteen known worlds to compare with him. There is none like him even in the imaginary tales and epics. But what I say is something else. This Vanathi, -- who fainted -- do you know what that fainting is really about? There is no need of going to an astrologer to find the cause! If anyone had asked me I would have explained." Varini was sure of herself.
"atu eṉṉa mayakkamaʈi? eŋkaɭukkuttaaṉ colleeṉ!" eṉṟaaɭ centiru. "What is that fainting? You can tell us, can't you?" asked Sendiru.
vaariɳi centiruviṉ kaatooʈu eetoo ca ṉṉaaɭ. "eṉṉaʈi irakaciyam coṉṉaaɭ? eŋkaɭukkut teriyak kuuʈaataa?" eṉṟu niravati keeʈʈaaɭ. Varini then whispered something in her friend's ears. Niravati intervened, "Hey! What's the secret? Let us know it too!"
"atu caataaraɳa mayakkamillaiyaam! maiyal mayakkamaam!" eṉṟaaɭ centiru. "I believe it is not an ordinary fainting fit! It is a lovelorn fit!"
uʈaṉee elloorum kalakalaveṉṟu cirittaarkaɭ. ataik keeʈʈu viʈʈu natik karai maraŋkaɭil irunta paṟavaikaɭ caʈacaʈaveṉṟu iṟakkaiyai aʈittuk koɳʈu paṟantu ceṉṟaṉa. Upon hearing these words all the girls laughed merrily. Hearing this noise, the birds upon the trees flew out with noisy wing-beats.
"nam iɭavaracar ilaŋkaiyiliruntu tirumpi vantaal maṟupaʈiyum ivaɭ maayappoʈi pooʈap paarppaaɭ. ataṟku naam iʈaŋkoʈuttuviʈaamal jaakkirataiyaayirukka veeɳʈum!" eṉṟu coṉṉaaɭ niravati. "When our Prince comes back from Lanka she will again try her magic tricks on him. We should not give place for that."
"iɭavaracar tirumpi varuvataṟkuɭ inta vaaṉati paittiyam piʈittup pitaṟṟa aarampikkaaviʈʈaal eṉ peyar taarakai illai; peyarait taaʈakai eṉṟu maaṟṟi vaittuk koɭɭukiṟeeṉ!" eṉṟaaɭ taarakai. "If this Vanathi does not go mad by the time the Prince returns, I shall change my name from Tarika to Tataka (an ogress of the epic Ramayana)."
"atu kiʈakkaʈʈumaʈi! iɭaiyapiraaʈʈi colliviʈʈup pooṉa kaariyattai avar varuvataṟkuɭ ceytu vaikka veeɳʈaamaa? vaaŋkaɭaʈi" eṉṟaaɭ mantaakiṉi. "OK! Let it go! Don't we have to complete the task assigned to us before the Younger Pirati comes back? Come girls, enough of this," said Mandakini.
piṟaku appeɳkaɭil iruvar paʈakiṉ aʈiyil eeṟkeṉavee ciṟitu peyarntirunta oru palakaiyaip peyarttu eʈuttaarkaɭ. peyarkkappaʈʈa iʈattil niiɭamaaṉa peʈʈi pool amainta paɭɭattil oru mutalai kiʈantatu! ataavatu cettuppooṉa mutalaiyiṉ uʈalaip patappaʈutti uɭɭee paɲcum naarum tiɳittu vaittirunta pommai mutalai. atai eʈuttu vauiyil vaittuk koɳʈaarkaɭ. paʈakaic ciṟitu tuuram celuttik koɳʈu ceṉṟu, natikkarai orattil periya periya veerkaɭ viʈʈu vaɭarntirunta oru perumarattiṉ arukil vantaarkaɭ. ammarattiṉ orattil attool mutalaiyai eʈuttu viʈʈaarkaɭ.atu mara veerkaɭilee paatiyum nati veɭɭattil paatiyumaakak kiʈantatu. paarppataṟku nija mutalaiyaip poolavee payaŋkaramaaṉa tooṟṟam aɭittatu. veɭɭam aʈittuk koɳʈu pooy viʈaamal oru ciṟiya maɳik kayiṟṟai ataṉ kaal oṉṟil kaʈʈi veerooʈu ceerttup piɳaittaarkaɭ. kayiṟu vauiyil teriyaatapaʈi niirukkuɭɭeeyee amuŋkiyirukkumpaʈi kaʈʈiṉaarkaɭ. After that, two of them removed one of the loose planks at the bottom of the barge. In a long alcove under it, they found a crocodile! That is, a dead crocodile preserved and stuffed with fibre and cotton! They took it out. The girls then paddled the barge a little way along the bank and pulled in near a clearing; a large mango tree grew along the bank with thick spreading roots. They pulled the stuffed crocodile amidst the tree roots. It lay among the roots, half in the water and half out. The girls bound a thin string to one of its legs and tied it to the root, preventing the stuffed reptile from floating away!
"eeṉaʈi, mantaakiṉi! etaṟkaaka intap pommai mutalaiyai ippaʈi marattaʈiyil kaʈʈi vaikkac colliyirukkiṟaar iɭaiyapiraaʈʈi?" eṉṟu taarakai keeʈʈaaɭ. Tarika asked, "Why did the Younger Pirati want us to leave the crocodile here, like this?"
"uṉakkut teriyaataa? vaaṉati mikka payantaaŋkoɭɭiyaayirukkiṟaaɭ allavaa? avaɭuʈaiya payattaip pookkit tairiyacaali aakkuvataṟkuttaaṉ!" eṉṟaaɭ mantaakiṉi. "Don't you know? Vanathi is so timid and poor-spirited, frightened of each and everything. To get rid of her fear and make her brave."
"ellaavaṟṟaiyum ceerttup paarttaal, vaaṉatiyai iɭavaracarukkuk kaliyaaɳam paɳɳi vaittuviʈa veeɳʈum eṉṟee kuntavaiteevi utteecittirukkiṟaar poolirukkiṟatu!" eṉṟaaɭ niravati. "If we consider all these things, it seems as if the Younger Pirati really intends to wed this foolish Vanathi to our Prince!" exclaimed Niravati.
"appaʈi eetaavatu peeccu vantaal naaṉ inta vaaṉatikku viʂattaik koʈuttuk koṉṟu viʈukiṟeeṉ. paarttuk koɳʈiru!" eṉṟaaɭ poṟaamaikkaariyaaṉa vaariɳi. "If anything like that happens, I shall poison this Vanathi and kill her," said the jealous maid, Varini.
"nii ippaʈiyellaam ericcal aʈaivataṟkuk kaaraɳamee illai. maaṉiya keeʈattu iraʈʈai maɳʈalac cakkaravarttiyum veeŋki naaʈʈiṉ maṉṉarum kaliŋka teecattu raajaavum vaʈakkee veku tuurattil uɭɭa kaṉṉooci cakkaravarttiyum kuuʈa nam iɭavaracarukkup peɳ koʈukkak kaattirukkiṟaarkaɭaam! appaʈiyirukka intak koʈumpaaɭuur vaaṉatiyai yaaraʈi ilaʈciyam ceyyap pookiṟaarkaɭ!" eṉṟaaɭ mantaakiṉi. "All this jealousy of yours is not warranted. All the kings of the world, the monarch of Manyaketa in Rashtrakuta, the Emperor of Vengi, the King of Kalinga and even the Emperor of Kanouj are ready to give their daughters in marriage to our Prince. Who will care about this Vanathi from Kodumbalur?" said soft spoken Mandakini.
"nii collukiṟapaʈi anta aracarkaɭ kaattirukkalaamaʈi! aaṉaal nam iɭavaracaruʈaiya viruppam allavaa mukkiyam? iɭavaracar 'naaṉ eppootaavatu kaliyaaɳam ceytu koɳʈaal tamiɻakattup peɳɳaittaaṉ maɳantu koɭveeṉ' eṉṟu collik koɳʈirukkiṟaaraam! uŋkaɭukkellaam itu teriyaataa?" eṉṟaaɭ centiru. "All those kings may be ready, like you say; but what are the wishes of our Prince? I believe he always says `If I ever marry, I shall marry a girl from the Tamil lands.' Don't you all know this? asked Sendiru.
"appaʈiyaaṉaal mikavum nallataayp pooyiṟṟu. naam elloorum ceern u taṉittaṉiyee nam kaivaricaiyaik kaaʈʈa veeɳʈiyatutaaṉee? inta vaaṉatiyiṉaal muʈikiṟa kaariyam nammaal muʈiyaatu pooyviʈumaa? avaɭiʈam uɭɭa maayap poʈi nammiʈamum illaiyaa, eṉṉa?" eṉṟaaɭ taarakai. "Then, all is well. All of us should polish our charms and show our competence. What this Vanathi can do, we can also achieve!"
ippaʈiyellaam intap peɳkaɭ peeciyataṟku aataaramaaṉa nikaɻcci eṉṉaveṉpatai neeyarkaɭukku ippootu terivikka virumpukiṟoom. Let us now explain the reason for such talk amongst these women.
