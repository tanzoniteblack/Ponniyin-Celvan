iraɳʈaam attiyaayam Chapter 2
aaɻvaarkkaʈiyaaṉ nampi Azlvar-adiyan Nambi
eerik karaiyiliruntu kiiɻiṟaŋkit teṉticai ceṉṟa paataiyil kutiraiyaic celuttiyapootu vantiyatteevaṉuʈaiya uɭɭam eeri alaikaɭiṉ miitu naʈaṉamaaʈiya paʈakaip pool aaṉantak kuuttaaʈiyatu. uɭɭattiṉ uɭɭee maṟaintu kiʈanta kutuukalam poŋkit tatumpiyatu. vaaɻkkaiyil veeṟu yaarum kaaɳaata aticaya aṉupavaŋkaɭait taaṉ aʈaiyum kaalam neruŋki viʈʈateṉṟu avaṉuʈaiya uɭɭuɳarcci colliyatu. Vallavarayan Vandiya Devan rode down the embankment and turned his horse towards the southern path; his heart danced with joy: like those sailboats skipping across the waves. The excitement buried deep in his heart seemed to erupt outward. His intuition said that he was going to meet adventures experienced by none in one lifetime.
cooɻa naaʈʈai aɳukumpootee ivvaɭavu aaṉantak koolaakalamaayirukkiṟatee? koɭɭiʈattait taaɳʈi viʈʈa piṉṉar accooɻa naaʈʈiṉ niirvaɭamum nilavaɭamum eppaʈiyirukkum? annaaʈʈil vaaɻum makkaɭum maŋkaiyarum eppaʈiyiruppaarkaɭ? ettaṉai natikaɭ? ettaṉai kuɭaŋkaɭ? ettaṉai tauiniir oʈaikaɭ? kavikaɭilum kaaviyaŋkaɭilum paaʈappeṟṟa poṉṉi natiyiṉ kaaʈci eppaʈiyirukkum? ataṉ karaikaɭilee puuttuk kuluŋkum puṉṉai maraŋkaɭum koṉṉai maraŋkaɭum kaʈampa maraŋkaɭum ettakaiya maṉookaramaaṉa kaaʈciyaayirukkum? niirooʈaikaɭil kuvaɭaikaɭum kumutaŋkaɭum kaɳkaaʈʈi aɻaippatum centaamaraikaɭ mukamalarntu varaveeṟpatum ettakaiya iṉiya kaaʈciyaayirukkum? kaaveeriyiṉ iru karaikaɭilum civapaktic celvarkaɭaaṉa cooɻap paramparaiyiṉar eʈuppittuɭɭa aṟputa veelaippaaʈamainta aalayaŋkaɭ evvaɭavu aɻakaayirukkum? What joyous delight even as I approach the Chozla Kingdom! What wonders will the fertile, bountiful land beyond Kollidam hold? How would the men and women of that country behave? How many rivers? How many reservoirs? How many clear streams? How wondrous would be the river Ponni (Cauvery) praised in song and epic? How enchanting would be the flower laden punnai, konnai and kadamba trees on its banks? Would not the wink of the lily and the blue-lotus or the blossoming welcome of a red-lotus be a pleasant sight? How magnificent would be the spectacular temples erected by these devout Chozlas along both banks of the Cauvery?
aakaa! paɻaiyaaṟai nakar! cooɻa maṉṉarkaɭiṉ talainakar! puumpukaaraiyum uṟaiyuuraiyum ciṟiya kukkiraamaŋkaɭaakac ceytuviʈʈa paɻaiyaaṟai! annakariluɭɭa maaʈamaaɭikaikaɭum kuuʈa koopuraŋkaɭum paʈai viiʈukaɭum kaʈaiviitikaɭum civaalayak kaṟṟaɭikaɭum tirumaalukkuriya viɳɳakaraŋkaɭum eppaʈiyirukkum? Aha! Pazlayarai! Capital city of Chozlas! Pazlayarai that turned Poompuhar and Uraiyoor into small country towns! What of its palaces, towering columns, armaments, guard houses, busy markets, Shiva temples of granite and towering Vishnu temples?
anta aalayaŋkaɭil icai vallavarkaɭ iṉiya kuralil teevaarap paaʈalkaɭaiyum tiruvaaymoɻip paacuraŋkaɭaiyum paaʈakkeeʈʈoor paravacamaʈaivaarkaɭ eṉṟu vantiyatteevaṉ keeɭviyuṟṟiruntaaṉ. avaṟṟaiyellaam keeʈkum peeṟu taṉakku viraivil kiʈaikkap pookiṟatu itu maʈʈuntaaṉaa? cila naaɭaikku muṉpu varaiyil taaṉ kaṉavilum karutaata cila peeṟukaɭum kiʈʈappookiṉṟaṉa. viirattil veelaṉaiyum aɻakil maṉmataṉaiyum nikartta paraantaka cuntara cooɻa makaaraajaavai neerukku neer kaaɳappookiṟaaṉ. avvaɭavutaaṉaa? avaruʈaiya celvap putalvi, oppuyarvillaata naariimaɳi, kuntavaip piraaʈʈiyaiyum kaaɳap pookiṟaaṉ. Vandiya Devan had heard about the captivating devotional poems - Thevaram & Thiru-vaaimozli - sung by music experts in those temples. He was soon to have the fortune of hearing them. That was not just it -- he was soon to attain favors beyond his wildest dreams. He was to personally meet Emperor Paranthaka Sundara Chozla, who was comparable to the God Velan in valor; who was as handsome as Manmatha the God of Love. Furthermore, he was soon to meet the Emperor's beloved daughter, an incomparable jewel among women, the Younger Pirati Kundavai!
aaṉaal vaɻiyil taʈai etuvum neeraamal irukka veeɳʈum. entat taʈai neerntaaltaaṉ eṉṉa? kaiyilee veel irukkiṟatu. iʈaiyil toŋkiya uṟaiyilee vaaɭ irukkiṟatu; maarpilee kavacam irukkiṟatu; neɲcilee uramirukkiṟatu. aaṉaal makaataɳʈa naayakar, iɭavaracar aatittar, oru periya muʈʈukkaʈʈai pooʈʈirukkiṟaar; oppuvitta kaariyattai niṟaiveeṟṟuvataṟku muṉpu yaariʈamum caɳʈai piʈikkak kuuʈaateṉṟu. antak kaʈʈaɭaiyai niṟaiveeṟṟuvatutaaṉ mikavum kaʈiṉamaayiruntatu. eetoo ivvaɭavu tuuramum pirayaaɳam ceytapootu niṟaiveeṟṟiyaaki viʈʈatu. iṉṉum iraɳʈu naaɭaiyap pirayaaɳamtaaṉee miccamirukkiṟatu? atuvarai poṟumaiyuʈaṉ iruntee tiira veeɳʈum. Hopefully there would be no interruptions along the way. So what if there are obstacles? I have a spear in hand, a sword at my waist, armor on my chest and courage in my heart. But the orders of the Commander-in-Chief, my Prince, are strict: do not enter into any skirmish until the assigned task is completed. It is pretty trying to obey that order! I have followed it thus far in my travels. A mere two-day journey remains. I must be patient until then.
aatavaṉ maṟaivataṟkuɭ kaʈampuurai aʈaiya veeɳʈum eṉṟa karuttuʈaṉ ceṉṟu koɳʈirunta vantiyatteevaṉ ciṟitu neerattukkellaam viira naaraayaɳapura viɳɳakarak kooyilai neruŋkiṉaaṉ. Travelling with the intention of reaching Kadamboor Fort before sunset, Vandiya Devan soon reached the Vaishnava temple in the town of Veera-narayana-puram.
aṉṟu aaʈit tirumaɲcaṉat tiruviɻaavum ceerntiruntapaʈiyaal kooyilaic cuṟṟiyuɭɭa marat tooppukaɭil perum jaṉakkuuʈʈam ceerntiruntatu. Since it was the day of the Aadi Festival, a large crowd of people had gathered in the woods and glades around the temple.
palaac cuɭaikaɭum vaaɻaip paɻaŋkaɭum karumpuk kaɻikaɭum palavakait tiṉpaɳʈaŋkaɭum viṟpavarkaɭ aaŋkaaŋkee kaʈai vaittiruntaarkaɭ. peɳkaɭ talaiyil cuuʈik koɭɭum malarkaɭaiyum, teeva puujaikkuriya taamarai moʈʈukkaɭ mutaliyavaṟṟaiyum cilar viṟṟuk koɳʈiruntaarkaɭ. teeŋkaay, iɭaniir, akil, cantaṉam, veṟṟilai, vellam, aval, pori mutaliyavaṟṟaic cilar kuppal kuppalaakap pooʈʈuk koɳʈiruntaarkaɭ. aaŋkaaŋkee veeʈikkai vinootaŋkaɭ naʈantu koɳʈiruntaṉa.joociyarkaɭ, reekai caastirattil vallavarkaɭ, kuṟi collukiṟavarkaɭ, viʂakkaʈikku mantirippavarkaɭ, ivarkaɭukkum aŋkee kuṟaiyillai. itaiyellaam paarttuk koɳʈu ceṉṟa vantiyatteevaṉ oriʈattil oru peruŋkuuʈʈam niṉṟu koɳʈiruppataiyum antak kuuʈʈattukkuɭɭeeyiruntu yaaroo cilar uratta kuralil vaakkuvaatam ceyyum cattam varuvataiyum kavaṉittaaṉ. eṉṉa vivaatam naʈaipeṟukiṟatu eṉpatai aṟintu koɭɭa avaṉukku aaval piiṟik koɳʈu eɻuntatu. anta aavalai aʈakkik koɭɭa avaṉaal muʈiyavillai. kuuʈʈattukku vauiyil caalai oramaakak kutiraiyai niṟutti viʈʈuk kiiɻee iṟaŋkiṉaaṉ. kutiraiyai aŋkeeyee niṟkumpaʈi taʈʈik koʈuttuc camikɲaiyaal colliviʈʈuk kuuʈʈattaip piɭantu koɳʈu uɭɭee pooṉaaṉ. Vendors selling jack-fruit, banana, sugar-cane and various other edibles had set up shops. Others sold flowers such as lotus buds for worship of the Gods as well as blooms to decorate the braids of women. Mounds of tender coconuts, myrrh, candy, jaggery, betel leaves, pressed rice and puffed grain were on display for sale. Fun-games and other amusements were in progress here and there. Astrologers, expert palm readers, soothsayers and magicians who cured poison-bites: of these there was no dearth. Amidst all this Vandiya Devan noticed a large gathering standing on one side and he heard loud noises of argument among people in its middle. An immense urge rose in him to find out what the argument was about. He could not control it! Stopping his horse by the roadside near the crowd, he dismounted. Signaling the horse to wait, he parted the crowd and walked right in.
aŋkee vivaatattil iiʈupaʈʈiruntavarkaɭ muuṉṟee peertaaṉ eṉpataip paarkka avaṉukku viyappu eeṟpaʈʈatu. aaṉaal vivaatattil iiʈupaʈʈavarkaɭ muuṉṟee peertaaṉ eṉṟaalum, kuuʈʈattiliruntavarkaɭ palar avvappootu avaravarkaɭukku ukanta vaatakkaarariṉ kaʈciyai aatarittuk kooʂaŋkaɭaik kiɭappiṉaarkaɭ. ataṉaaleetaaṉ avvaɭavu cattam eɻuntatu eṉpatai vantiyatteevaṉ terintu koɳʈaaṉ. piṟaku eṉṉa vivaatam naʈaipeṟukiṟatu eṉpataik kavaṉittaaṉ. It surprised him to find only three persons involved in the debate. Even though they were merely three men, the crowd around them periodically cheered their chosen favorites rather loudly. Vandiya Devan realized the cause for this commotion and watched to find out the reason for the argument.
vaatamiʈʈa muuvaril oruvar uʈampellaam uurtvapuɳʈaramaakac cantaṉam aɳintu talaiyil muṉ kuʈumi vaittirunta vaiʂɳava pakta cikaamaɳi. kaiyil avar oru kuṟuntaʈiyum vaittiruntaar. kaʈʈaiyaayum kuʈʈaiyaayum vairam paaynta tirumeeṉiyuʈaṉ viɭaŋkiṉaar. One of the debaters seemed to be an exceptional follower of the Vaishnava faith: he wore the sandal-paste namam markings of the sect all over his body; he had styled his hair into a topknot on his forehead. A short staff was in his hand; his short, squat frame seemed quite strong.
iṉṉoruvar tamatu meeṉiyellaam paʈʈai paʈʈaiyaayt tiruniiṟu aɳintirunta civapaktar. The other was a follower of Shiva, wearing broad ashen marks on his devout body.
muuṉṟaavatu maṉitar kaavi vastiram tarittut talaiyaiyum muɳʈaṉam ceytu koɳʈiruntaar. avar vaiʂɳavarum alla, caivarum alla, iraɳʈaiyum kaʈantavaraaṉa atvaita veetaanti eṉṟu teriyavantatu. The third wore ocher robes and had completely shaved all the hair off his head. He was neither a Vaishnava nor a Saiva: he seemed to be an Advaita philosopher beyond any sect.
caivar coṉṉaar: "o, aaɻvaarkkaʈiyaaṉ nampiyee! itaṟku viʈai collum! civaperumaaṉuʈaiya muʈiyaik kaaɳpataṟkup pirammaavum, aʈiyaik kaaɳpataṟkut tirumaalum muyaṉṟaarkaɭaa, illaiyaa? muʈiyum aʈiyum kaaɳaamal iruvarum vantu civaperumaaṉuʈaiya paataŋkaɭil caraɳaakati aʈaintaarkaɭaa, illaiyaa? appaʈiyirukkac civaperumaaṉaik kaaʈʈilum uŋkaɭ tirumaal eppaʈip periya teyvam aavaar?" The Saiva devotee was saying, "Oh you Azlvar-adiyan Nambi! Give me an answer to this! Did not Brahma try to find Shiva's head and Vishnu search for His feet? Unable to see neither head nor foot did not both seek the sanctuary of Lord Shiva's auspicious feet? That being so, how can your Thirumal Vishnu be a greater God than Lord Shiva?"
itaik keeʈʈa aaɻvaarkkaʈiyaaṉnampi taṉ kait taʈiyai aaʈʈik koɳʈu, " caritaaṉ kaaɳum! viira caiva paatatuuɭi paʈʈaree! niṟuttum um peeccai! ilaŋkai aracaṉaakiya tacakaɳʈa raavaɳaṉukku ummuʈaiya civaṉ varaŋkaɭ koʈuttaaree? anta varaŋkaɭ ellaam eŋkaɭ tirumaaliṉ avataaramaakiya iraamapiraaṉiṉ kootaɳʈattiṉ muṉṉaal taviʈupoʈiyaakap pookavillaiyaa? appaʈiyirukka, eŋkaɭ tirumaalaik kaaʈʈilum uŋkaɭ civaṉ eppaʈip periya teyvamaavaar?" eṉṟu keeʈʈaaṉ. Hearing this Azlvar-adiyan pounded his staff saying, "Well fella! You fanatic Saiva dust-worshiping priest! Stop your nonsense. Recall that your Shiva gave boons to the ten-headed Ravana, King of Lanka. Did not all those boons turn to sawdust when confronted by Sri Rama, an incarnation of our Thirumal Vishnu? When that is so, how can your Shiva be a greater God than our Thirumal?"
intac camayattil kaavi vastiram aɳinta atvaita canniyaaci talaiyiʈʈuk kuuṟiyataavatu: "niiŋkaɭ iruvarum etaṟkaaka viiɳil vaatam iʈukiṟiirkaɭ? civaṉ periya teyvamaa, viʂɳu periya teyvamaa eṉṟu ettaṉai neeram niiŋkaɭ vaatittaalum vivakaaram tiiraatu. intak keeɭvikkup patil veetaantam colkiṟatu. niiŋkaɭ kiiɻaaṉa pakti maarkkattil irukkiṟa varaiyiltaaṉ civaṉ - viʂɳu eṉṟu caɳʈaiyiʈuviirkaɭ. paktikku meelee ɲaaṉamaarkkam irukkiṟatu. ɲaaṉattukku meelee ɲaasam eṉṟu oṉṟu irukkiṟatu. anta nilaiyai aʈaintu viʈʈaal civaṉum illai, viʂɳuvum illai. carvam pirammamayam jakat. sarii caŋkara pakavat paataaccaariyaar piramma cuuttira paaʂyattil eṉṉa colliyirukkiṟaar eṉṟaal...." At this the ocher-clad monk who believed in the One-supreme Being, intervened to say, "Why do you both argue wastefully? There is no end to your arguments about Shiva being a bigger God or Vishnu being greater. Only while you are involved in these pagan rituals of devotion will you quarrel about Shiva and Vishnu. The path of Knowledge is greater than devotion. Then there is the state of Absolute Knowing, beyond even Knowledge. When you reach that state there is no Shiva and no Vishnu. All existence is the Supreme Being. Do you know what Sri Sankara the Teacher says in his dissertation on the scriptures ...."
iccamayam aaɻvaarkkaʈiyaaṉ nampi kuṟukkiʈʈu, "caritaaṉ kaaɳum, niṟuttum! ummuʈaiya caŋkaraaccaariyaar avvaɭavu upaniʂataŋkaɭukkum pakavatkiitaikkum piramma cuuttirattukkum paaʂyam eɻuti viʈʈuk kaʈaiciyil eṉṉa coṉṉaar teriyumaa? At this point Azlvar-adiyan Nambi interrupted, "Stop it you fellow! Do you know what your Sankara who wrote all those explanations for the Upanishads, the Bhagavat Gita and other scriptures finally say? 
'paja koovintam paja koovintam
paja koovintam muuʈamatee!'

Sing of Govinda, Sing of Govinda,
Sing of Govinda, Oh foolish mind!
eṉṟu muuṉṟu vaaʈʈi coṉṉaar. ummaip pooṉṟa mauʈiikarkaɭaip paarttuttaaṉ 'muuʈamatee!' eṉṟu caŋkaraaccaariyaar coṉṉaar!" eṉak kuuṟiyatum, antak kuuʈʈattil 'aahaa' kaaramum, parikaacac cirippum karakooʂamum kalantu eɻuntaṉa. He said it three times! It is to dumbheads like you that he refers when he mentions foolish minds." The crowd responded to this with thunderous clapping, mocking laughter and loud cheers of "Aha, oho".
aaṉaal canniyaaci cummaa irukkavillai. "aʈee! muṉkuʈumi nampi! naaṉ 'muuʈamati' eṉṟu nii coṉṉatu caritaaṉ eeṉeṉṟaal, uṉ kaiyil veṟuntaʈiyai vaittuk koɳʈirukkum nii veṟuntaʈiyaṉ aakiṟaay. uṉṉaip pooṉṟa veṟuntaʈiyaṉooʈu peeca vantatu eṉṉuʈaiya muuʈamatiyiṉaaltaaṉee?" eṉṟaar. The monk did not stay quiet. "Hey you with the topknot on the forehead! It is correct that you refer to me as a foolish mind. You hold an ordinary wood-staff in your hands; you are surely a wood-brained wastrel. It is surely because of my foolish mind that I have come to talk with a wastrel like you."
"oy cuvaamikaɭee! eṉ kaiyil vaittiruppatu veṟuntaʈiyalla. veeɳʈiya camayattil ummuʈaiya moʈʈai maɳʈaiyai uʈaikkum cakti uʈaiyatuŋ kaaɳum!" eṉṟu kuuṟik koɳʈee aaɻvaarkkaʈiyaaṉ kaiyilirunta kuṟuntaʈiyai oŋkiṉaaṉ. ataip paartta avaṉ kaʈciyaar 'ohoo!' eṉṟu aarpparittaṉar. "Oh Sir! This is not an ordinary wood-staff. If need be it has the power to break open your shaven head," and saying this Nambi raised his staff as the crowd cheered him.
appootu atvaita cuvaamikaɭ, "appaṉee! niṟuttik koɭ! taʈi uṉṉuʈaiya kaiyileeyee irukkaʈʈum. appaʈiyee nii uṉ kaittaʈiyaal eṉṉai aʈittaalum ataṟkaaka naaṉ koopaŋkoɭɭa maaʈʈeeṉ. uṉṉuʈaṉ caɳʈaikku varavum maaʈʈeeṉ. aʈippatum pirammam; aʈipaʈuvatum pirammam. eṉṉai nii aʈittaal uṉṉaiyee aʈittuk koɭkiṟavaṉaavaay!" eṉṟaar. The Philosopher interrupted. "Dear fellow! Stop it! Keep the staff in your own hands. I shall not be angry even if you hit me with your stick. Neither will I dispute with you. That which hits is Supreme! That which is hit is also Supreme! If you lay hands upon me it is like hitting your own self!"
aaɻvaarkkaʈiyaaṉ nampi, "itoo elloorum paaruŋkaɭ! pirammattaip parappirammam tiruccaattuc caattap pookiṟatu. eṉṉai naaṉee taʈi koɳʈu taakkap pookiṟeeṉ!" eṉṟu taʈiyaic cuɻaṟṟik koɳʈu cuvaamikaɭai neruŋkiṉaaṉ. Upon hearing this Nambi announced, "All of you watch! The Supreme is going to serve the Supreme a hallowed-hit. I am going to hit myself with this staff," swirling his staff he moved towards the monk.
itaiyellaam paarttuk koɳʈirunta vallavaraiyaṉukku oru kaɳam anta muṉ kuʈumi nampiyiṉ kaittaʈiyai vaɻimaṟittup piʈuŋkik koɳʈu avaṉai antat taʈiyiṉaal naalu tiruccaattuc caattalaamaa eṉṟu tooṉṟiyatu. Watching all this, Vandiya Devan wondered if he should grab the staff from the fellow with the topknot on his forehead and serve him a few smacks from it.
aaṉaal tiʈiireṉṟu cuvaamiyaaraik kaaɳoom! kuuʈʈattil pukuntu avar maṟaintu viʈʈaar. ataip paarttuk koɳʈirunta vaiʂɳava kooʂʈiyaar meelum aarpparittaarkaɭ. However the monk seemed to have suddenly disappeared. He mingled into the crowd and vanished! At this the Vaishnava supporters in the crowd cheered even louder.
aaɻvaarkkaʈiyaaṉ viiracaivaruʈaiya pakkamaakat tirumpi, "oy paata tuuɭi paʈʈaree! niir eṉṉa collukiṟiir? meelum vaatam ceyya virumpukiṟiiraa? allatu cuvaamiyaaraip pool niirum oʈʈam eʈukkiṟiiraa?" eṉṟaaṉ. Azlvar-adiyan turned towards the Saiva devotee, "You foot-dust worshipper, what do you say? Will you continue this debate or would you too run away like the monk?" he said.
"naaṉaa? orunaaɭum naaṉ anta vaay veetaantiyaip pool oʈʈam eʈukka maaʈʈeeṉ. eṉṉaiyum ummuʈaiya kaɳɳaṉ eṉṟu niṉaittiiroo? koopiyar viiʈʈil veɳɳey tiruʈi uɳʈu mattaal aʈipaʈʈavaṉtaaṉee ummuʈaiya kaɳɳaṉ!..." eṉṟu paatatuuɭipaʈʈar colvataṟkuɭ, aaɻvaarkkaʈiyaaṉ kuṟukkiʈʈaaṉ. "Me? I shall never run away like that philosopher spouting mere words. Did you think of me also as your Kannan (Krishna)? Is not your Krishna the same fellow who received beatings from the butter-churn because he stole butter from the homes of milkmaids? ..." Before the Shiva worshipper could finish, Nambi interrupted.
"eeṉ kaaɳum? ummuʈaiya paramacivaṉ piʈʈukku maɳ cumantu mutukil aʈipaʈʈatai maṟantu viʈʈiiroo?" eṉṟu keeʈʈuk koɳʈu kaittaʈiyai viicik koɳʈu viira caivar arukil neruŋkiṉaaṉ. "Why sir? Did not your Shiva carry loads of dirt for the sake of dry pudding and get beaten on his back? Have you forgotten that?" Swirling his staff he approached closer.
aaɻvaarkkaʈiyaaṉ nalla kuɳʈaati kuɳʈaṉ. viiracaivaraakiya paatatuuɭipaʈʈaroo caṟṟu melinta maṉitar. Azlvar-adiyan was a stocky, well-built brute. The Shiva devotee was an emaciated man.
meeṟkuuṟiya iruvaraiyum vivaatattil uṟcaakappaʈutti vantavarkaɭ taaŋkaɭum kaikalakka aayattamaaki aaravaaram ceytaarkaɭ. The cheering supporters in the crowd were ready to enter the tussle.
inta muuʈac caɳʈaiyait taʈukka veeɳʈum eṉṟa eɳɳam vallavaraiyaṉ maṉatil uɳʈaayiṟṟu. Vallavarayan Vandiya Devan felt that he must stop this absurd rumpus.
avaṉ niṉṟa iʈattiliruntu caṟṟum muṉṉaal vantu, "etaṟkaaka aiyaa niiŋkaɭ caɳʈai pooʈukiṟiirkaɭ? veeṟu veelai oṉṟum uŋkaɭukku illaiyaa? caɳʈaikkut tiṉavu eʈuttaal iiɻanaaʈʈukkup poovatutaaṉee? aŋkee perum poor naʈantu koɳʈirukkiṟatee?" eṉṟaaṉ. He came forward and said, "Sirs why are you arguing? Do you have nothing better to do? If you itch for a fight, why not go to Lanka? There is a big war going on there."
nampi caʈʈeṉṟu avaṉait tirumpip paarttu, "ivaṉ yaaraʈaa niyaayam colla vantavaṉ!" eṉṟaaṉ. Saying, "Who is this fellow trying to make peace?" Nambi quickly turned towards him.
kuuʈʈattilee iruntavarkaɭil cilarukku, vantiyatteevaṉuʈaiya viirat tooṟṟamum avaṉuʈaiya aɻakiya mukavilaacamum piʈittiruntaṉa. Some people in the crowd liked Vandiya Devan's fearless mein and charismatic face.
"tampi! nii collu! intac caɳʈaikkaararkaɭukku niyaayattai eʈuttuc collu! uṉakkup pakkapalamaaka naaŋkaɭ irukkiṟoom!" eṉṟu avarkaɭ coṉṉaarkaɭ. "Thambi! You tell them." They cheered, "Tell these quarrel mongers the truth. We will support you."
"eṉakkut terinta niyaayattaic colkiṟeeṉ. civaperumaaṉum naaraayaɳamuurttiyum taŋkaɭukkuɭ caɳʈai pooʈʈukkoɭvataakat teriyavillai. avarkaɭ cineekamaakavum cumukamaakavum iruntu varukiṟaarkaɭ. appaʈiyirukka, inta nampiyum paʈʈarum etaṟkaakac caɳʈai pooʈʈuk koɭɭa veeɳʈum?" eṉṟu vallavaraiyaṉ kuuṟiyataik keeʈʈu, akkuuʈʈattil palarum nakaittaarkaɭ. "I will tell the facts I know. There seems to be no quarrel between Lord Shiva and Lord Vishnu-Narayana. Those two seem to be quite friendly and pleasant towards each other. Then why are these two men arguing?" Hearing these words of Vallavarayan Vandiya Devan the people snickered.
appootu viiracaivapaʈʈar, "intap piɭɭai aṟivaaɭiyaakavee tooṉṟukiṟaaṉ. aaṉaal veeʈikkaip peecciṉaal maʈʈum vivaatam tiirntuviʈumaa? civaperumaaṉ tirumaalai viʈap periya teyvamaa, illaiyaa eṉṟa keeɭvikku ivaṉ viʈai collaʈʈum!" eṉṟaar. The Shiva devotee commented "This boy seems to be intelligent. But jovial words cannot end the argument. Let him answer this question: is Lord Shiva a greater God than Vishnu or is he not?"
"civaṉum periya teyvantaaṉ; tirumaalum periya teyvantaaṉ iruvarum camamaaṉa teyvaŋkaɭ. yaarai veeɳʈumaaṉaalum toɻutu koɭɭuŋkaɭ caɳʈai etaṟku?" eṉṟaaṉ vallavaraiyaṉ. "Shiva is a great God. So is Thirumal Vishnu. Both are equal. Worship whomever you want. Why quarrel?" said Vallavarayan.
"atu eppaʈic collalaam? civaṉum viʂɳuvum camamaaṉa teyvaŋkaɭ eṉṟu colluvataṟku aataaram eṉṉa?" eṉṟu aaɻvaarkkaʈiyaaṉ ataʈʈik keeʈʈaaṉ. "How can you say that? Where is the proof that Shiva and Vishnu are equal?" chided Azlvar-adiyan.
"aataaramaa? itoo colkiṟeeṉ! neeṟṟu maalai vaikuɳʈattukkup pooyirunteeṉ. atee camayattil paramacivaṉum aŋkee vantiruntaar. iruvarum cama aacaṉattil amarntiruntaarkaɭ.avarkaɭuʈaiya uyaram oṉṟaakavee iruntatu. aayiṉum aiyattukku iʈamiṉṟi eṉ kaiyiṉaal muɻam pooʈʈu iruvar uyarattaiyum aɭantu paartteeṉ..." "Proof? I will give you proof. Last night I had been to Vaikunta, the abode of Vishnu. At the same time Shiva came visiting. Both were seated on equal thrones. They seemed to be of equal height. Still, to avoid any doubt, I measured their height with my arm ..."
"aʈa piɭɭaay! parikaacamaa ceykiṟaay?" eṉṟu aaɻvaarkkaʈiyaaṉ karjaṉai ceytaaṉ. "Young man! Are you mocking me?" roared Azlvar-adiyan.
kuuʈʈattiṉar, "collu, tampi! collu!" eṉṟu aarpparittaarkaɭ. "Yes, Thambi yes. Tell us more!" applauded the crowd.
"aɭantu paarttatil iruvarum camamaaṉa uyaramee iruntaarkaɭ. atooʈu viʈaamal civaṉaiyum tirumaalaiyum neerileeyee keeʈʈu viʈʈeeṉ. avarkaɭ eṉṉa coṉṉaarkaɭ, teriyumaa? 'ariyum civaṉum oɳɳu, aṟiyaatavar vaayilee maɳɳu' eṉṟu coṉṉaarkaɭ. avvitam colli, taŋkaɭaip paṟṟic caɳʈai pooʈukiṟavarkaɭiṉ vaayilee pooʈuvataṟku intap piʈi maɳɳaiyum koʈuttaarkaɭ!" eṉṟu kuuṟiya vallavaraiyaṉ, muuʈiyirunta taṉatu valakkaiyait tiṟantu kaaʈʈiṉaaṉ. ataṟkuɭɭee oru piʈi maɳ iruntatu atai viici utaṟiṉaaṉ. "Upon measuring them, I found both to be of exactly the same height. Without stopping at that I asked both Shiva and Thirumal directly. Do you know what they said? `Hari and Shiva are one and the same. Those who don't know this should have their mouths filled with dirt!' Saying it they gave me this fistful of dirt to throw in the mouths of the idiots who quarrel about it." Vandiya Devan opened his fist to show a handful of dirt. He then threw it among them.
kuuʈʈattiliruntavarkaɭil palar appootu perum uṟcaakaŋkoɳʈu talaikkut talai taraiyiliruntu oru piʈi maɳ eʈuttu, nampiyiṉ talaiyilum paʈʈar talaiyilum viici eṟiya aarampittaarkaɭ. intat tuuraakrakac ceyalaic cilar taʈukka muyaṉṟaarkaɭ. Upon this all hell broke loose. The men in the exited crowd started picking up dirt and rubble and began throwing it at each other.
"aʈee! tuurttarkaɭaa? naastikarkaɭaa?" eṉṟu collik koɳʈu aaɻvaarkkaʈiyaaṉ taṉ kait taʈiyaic cuɻaṟṟik koɳʈu kuuʈʈattiṟkuɭ piraveecittaaṉ. Azlvar-adiyan also entered the fracas with shouts of "Idiots! Unbelievers!" and used his staff. A great disturbance and commotion was about to erupt.
oru periya kalavaramum aʈitaʈi caɳʈaiyum appootu aŋkee nikaɻum pooliruntaṉa. nallaveeɭaiyaaka, antac camayattil caṟṟut tuurattil oru periya calacalappu eeṟpaʈʈatu. Luckily, a stentorian announcement thwarting all this, was heard nearby.
"cuuraati cuurar, viirappirataapar, maaṟapaaɳʈiyaṉ paʈaiyai viiṟukoɳʈu taakki veerooʈu aṟutta veṟṟi veel uʈaiyaar, irupattu naalu poorkaɭil caɳʈaiyiʈʈu aṟupattu naaṉku viɻuppuɳkaɭaip peṟṟa tirumeeṉiyar, cooɻa naaʈʈut taṉaatikaari, taaṉiya paɳʈaara naayakar, iṟaivitikkum teevar, periya paɻuveeʈʈaraiyar vijayam ceykiṟaar! paraak! paraak! vaɻi viʈuŋkaɭ!" eṉṟu iʈimuɻakkak kuralil kaʈʈiyam kuuṟutal keeʈʈatu. "Best of warriors, bravest of the brave, he who destroyed the very roots of the Pandiya army by attacking furiously, he of the victorious spear, he who engaged in twenty-four combats and wears sixty-four battle-wounds on his sacred body, the Chozla Secretary for Finance and Food, the lord who can levy taxes, The Elder Lord of Pazluvoor, is announced. Make way! Make way!" A thundering voice made the announcement.
ivvaaṟu kaʈʈiyam kuuṟiyavarkaɭ mutalil vantaarkaɭ. piṟaku muracu aʈippavarkaɭ vantaarkaɭ. avarkaɭukkup piṉṉaal paṉaimarak koʈi taaŋkuvoor vantaarkaɭ. piṉṉar, kaiyil veel piʈitta viirarkaɭ cilar kampiiramaaka naʈantu vantaarkaɭ. ivarkaɭukkup piṉṉaal vanta alaŋkaritta yaaṉaiyiṉ miitu aajaaṉupaakuvaaṉa kariya tirumeeṉiyar oruvar viiṟṟiruntaar. mattakajattiṉ meel anta viirar viiṟṟirunta kaaʈci, oru maamalaic cikarattiṉ miitu kariyakoɳʈal oṉṟu taŋkiyatu pool iruntatu. The heralds who made these announcements came first. Then came the drummers. Behind them came men carrying the palmtree-flag. Next marched several smart footmen bearing lances and spears. Behind all these men came a dark, well-built man seated on a finely decorated elephant. The sight of the warrior on the elephant looked like a dark cloud resting atop a mountain peak.
kuuʈʈattil iruntavarkaɭ attaṉai peerum caalaiyiṉ irupuṟattilum vantu niṉṟatu pool vallavaraiyaṉum vantu niṉṟu paarttaaṉ. yaaṉai miitu iruntavar taaṉ paɻuveeʈʈaraiyar eṉpatai uukittuk koɳʈaaṉ. People stood on both sides of the roadway and watched. Vandiya Devan guessed that the man seated on the elephant was Lord Pazluvoor.
yaaṉaikkup piṉṉaal paʈʈut tiraiyiṉaal muuʈappaʈʈa civikai oṉṟu vantatu. ataṟkuɭ iruppatu yaaroo eṉṟu vallavaraiyaṉ cintippataṟkuɭɭee, cekkac civanta niṟattuʈaṉ vaɭaiyalkaɭum kaŋkaɳaŋkaɭum aɳinta oru karam civikaikkuɭɭeeyiruntu vauippaʈʈup pallakkiṉ paʈʈut tiraiyaic ciṟitu vilakiyatu. meekattiṉaal muuʈappaʈʈirunta puuraɳa cantiraṉ meekat tirai vilakiyatum paɭiireṉṟu oɭi viicuvatu pool civikaikkuɭɭee kaantimayamaaṉa oru peɳɳiṉ mukam terintatu. Behind the elephant came a palanquin with silken drapes pulled close. Before he could wonder who could be inside, Vallavarayan saw a fair hand full of bracelets and bangles come out and slightly part the curtains. The dazzling face of a girl could be seen inside the palanquin: like the moon shining forth from behind a shifting cloud cover.
peɳ kulattiṉ aɻakaik kaɳʈu kaɭikkum kaɳkaɭ vallavaraiyaṉukku uɳʈu eṉṟaalum, antap peɳɳiṉ mukam pirakaacamaaṉa puuraɳa cantiraṉaiyotta poṉ mukamaayiruntaalum ekkaaraɳattiṉaaloo vallavaraiyaṉukku ammukattaip paarttatum uɭɭattil makiɻcci tooṉṟavillai. iṉanteriyaata payamum aruvaruppum eeṟpaʈʈaṉa. Though he was a connoisseur of the beauty of womankind, though her captivating face seemed like the radiating full moon, for some reason, Vandiya Devan was not happy to see that face. An irrational fear and disgust took hold of him.
atee neerattil antap peɳɳiṉ kaɳkaɭ vallavaraiyaṉukku arukil uṟṟu nookkiṉa. maṟukaɳam oru piitikaramaaṉa peɳ kuralil 'kiṟiic' eṉṟa kuuccal keeʈʈatu uʈaṉee civikaiyiṉ paʈʈut tirai muṉpool muuʈik koɳʈatu. At the same time the woman's eyes focussed on something near Vandiya Devan. The next instant the horrified sound of a girl's scream was heard. The screens pulled shut once again!
vallavaraiyaṉ taṉ akkam pakkattil nookkiṉaaṉ. taṉakku arukil etaiyoo yaaraiyoo paarttuviʈʈuttaaṉ anta maatu 'kiṟiic'ciʈʈu viʈʈuc civikait tiraiyai muuʈik koɳʈaaɭ eṉṟu avaṉ uɭɭuɳarcci colliṟṟu. eṉavee, cuṟṟumuṟṟum paarttaaṉ. aaɻvaarkkaʈiyaaṉ taṉakkuc caṟṟup piṉṉaal oru puɭiya marattil caayntu koɳʈu niṟpataik kaɳʈaaṉ. anta viira vaiʂɳava nampiyiṉuʈaiya mukam colla muʈiyaata vikaarattai aʈaintu koora vaʈivamaaka maaṟiyiruppataiyum paarttaaṉ. vallavaraiyaṉuʈaiya uɭɭattil kaaraɳam viɭaŋkaata tikaippum aruvaruppum eeṟpaʈʈaṉa. Vallavarayan looked around him. His intuition said that the lady had screamed upon sighting something nearby. He searched around again. Azlvar-adiyan was leaning back on a tamarind tree just behind him. He saw that the fanatic Vaishnava's face had turned undescribably horrible and ugly. His heart filled with irrational surprise and revolt.
